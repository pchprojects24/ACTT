Bacitracin Zinc

Stocked on board

  • Bacitracin Zinc Ointment

Adult dosing

  • Minor cuts, scrapes, burns (prophylaxis/treatment of superficial skin infections)
    • Apply a small amount (thin layer) to affected area 1–3 times daily
    • May cover with sterile bandage if appropriate

Indications

  • Prevention of infection in minor cuts, scrapes, and burns
  • Treatment of minor superficial skin infections
  • Adjunct to wound care in clean, minor wounds

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to bacitracin or zinc (contact dermatitis can occur)
  • Not for deep or puncture wounds, animal bites, or serious burns (requires medical evaluation)
  • Not for ophthalmic use (unless specifically formulated for eye use)
  • Do not use over large areas of the body
  • Avoid prolonged use (beyond 1 week) without medical advice; prolonged use may promote resistant organisms or fungal overgrowth
  • Contact sensitization: bacitracin is a common cause of contact dermatitis with topical use

Adverse effects

  • Contact dermatitis / allergic reaction (rash, itching, redness at application site)
  • Rarely: anaphylaxis (with hypersensitivity)
  • Local irritation

Preparation & administration

  • Clean the affected area thoroughly with mild soap and water; pat dry
  • Apply a thin layer of ointment to cover the affected area
  • May apply 1–3 times daily as needed
  • May cover with a sterile bandage or dressing if desired
  • For external use only; avoid contact with eyes
  • Counsel: if condition worsens or does not improve within 1 week, seek medical evaluation

Monitoring

  • Wound healing progress (reduction in redness, swelling, drainage)
  • Signs of worsening infection (increasing pain, swelling, purulent discharge, fever, red streaking)
  • Local allergic reaction (rash, itching, worsening redness at application site)
  • If no improvement within 3–7 days or condition worsens, reassess and consider alternate therapy or systemic antibiotics

At-sea considerations

  • Useful for basic first aid and minor wound care in operational settings
  • Ensure proper wound cleaning and assessment before application; do not rely on topical antibiotics alone for contaminated or deep wounds
  • Document wound characteristics and progression (size, depth, signs of infection)
  • If signs of spreading infection, cellulitis, or systemic symptoms develop, initiate systemic antibiotics and consider evacuation
  • Remind crew that topical antibiotics are adjuncts to proper wound care (cleaning, debridement, dressing changes)
  • For marine-related wounds (especially deep punctures, contamination with seawater/marine organisms), consider broader antimicrobial coverage and medical consultation
