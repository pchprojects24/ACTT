Permethrin

Stocked on board

  • Permethrin 5% Cream 60gm

Adult dosing

  • Scabies (Sarcoptes scabiei infestation)
    • Apply 5% cream from neck down to entire body (including soles of feet); massage thoroughly into skin
    • Leave on for 8–14 hours (typically apply before bedtime and wash off in morning)
    • One application is usually sufficient; may repeat after 7–14 days if living mites observed or new lesions appear
    • Typical tube (60 g) sufficient for one full-body application in average adult
  • Head lice (off-label for 5% formulation; 1% formulation more common for lice but 5% can be used)
    • Apply to dry hair/scalp; leave on 10 minutes then rinse thoroughly
    • Repeat in 7–10 days (to kill newly hatched lice)

Indications

  • Treatment of scabies (5% cream)
  • Treatment of head lice, pubic lice (crabs) - typically 1% formulation but 5% can be used off-label

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to permethrin, pyrethrins, or chrysanthemums
  • Avoid contact with eyes, mouth, nose, and mucous membranes
  • Not for oral or vaginal use
  • Pregnancy/lactation: Generally considered safe topically (limited systemic absorption); use when benefit outweighs risk
  • Children <2 months: safety not established; use under medical direction
  • Existing skin inflammation/breakdown may increase absorption and irritation

Adverse effects

  • Local: burning, stinging, tingling, pruritus (itching may persist after mite eradication due to hypersensitivity)
  • Erythema, rash, numbness at application site
  • Contact dermatitis (uncommon)
  • Systemic effects rare (minimal absorption through intact skin)

Preparation & administration

  • Scabies:
    • Shower/bathe before application; dry skin thoroughly
    • Apply cream from neck down (include all skin folds, under nails, between fingers/toes, genitals, soles of feet)
    • For infants/young children or immunocompromised: may need to apply to head/scalp as well (follow medical direction)
    • Leave on 8–14 hours; wash off thoroughly with soap and water
    • Put on clean clothes and wash all bedding/clothing in hot water after treatment
  • Environmental measures:
    • Treat all household/close contacts simultaneously to prevent re-infestation
    • Wash all clothing, bedding, towels in hot water (≥50°C/122°F) and dry on high heat
    • Items that cannot be washed: seal in plastic bag for 72 hours (scabies mites die without human host)
  • Counsel: itching may persist 2–4 weeks after successful treatment due to hypersensitivity reaction to dead mites (does not indicate treatment failure)

Monitoring

  • Skin examination for resolution of lesions/burrows (scabies) or lice/nits (pediculosis)
  • Persistent itching beyond 2–4 weeks or new burrows/lesions may indicate treatment failure or re-infestation
  • Local skin reaction (significant erythema, blistering, or severe irritation)
  • Confirm all close contacts treated to prevent re-infestation

At-sea considerations

  • Scabies can spread rapidly in close-quarters living; treat all close contacts and cabin-mates simultaneously
  • Emphasize environmental decontamination: hot-water laundry, sealing non-washable items, thorough cabin cleaning
  • Distinguish treatment failure from post-scabies pruritus (persistent itching after cure); avoid over-treatment
  • If crusted (Norwegian) scabies suspected (hyperkeratotic, widespread): requires more aggressive treatment and medical consultation (highly contagious)
  • Document affected individuals, treatment dates, and close contacts for outbreak tracking
