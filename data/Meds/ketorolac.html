Analgesia / Anti-inflammatory

Ketorolac

Stocked on board

  • Ketorolac 10mg Tablets
  • Ketorolac 30mg/mL Injection 1mL

Adult dosing

  • Moderate to moderately severe pain (short-term use; maximum 5 days total therapy)
    • IM/IV: 30 mg IV/IM single dose OR 30 mg IV/IM every 6 hours as needed (maximum 120 mg/day)
    • PO (if transitioning from parenteral): 10 mg PO every 4–6 hours as needed (maximum 40 mg/day)
    • Combined IV/IM + PO duration: maximum 5 days total
  • Dose adjustments
    • Adults ≥65 years, renally impaired, or weight <50 kg: reduce dose (e.g., 15 mg IV/IM every 6 hours; max 60 mg/day)

Indications

  • Short-term management of moderate to moderately severe acute pain requiring opioid-level analgesia
  • Pain control when oral route not feasible initially (e.g., severe nausea/vomiting, post-trauma)
  • Renal colic, musculoskeletal pain, post-procedural pain (selected cases)

Contraindications / cautions

  • NSAID or ASA hypersensitivity (including ASA-exacerbated respiratory disease)
  • Active peptic ulcer disease, recent GI bleeding, inflammatory bowel disease
  • Advanced renal impairment or risk of renal failure (dehydration, concurrent nephrotoxins)
  • History of or risk for bleeding (anticoagulants, bleeding disorders, perioperative setting with high bleeding risk)
  • Cerebrovascular bleeding (suspected or confirmed)
  • Pregnancy and labor/delivery (increases bleeding risk; contraindicated in late pregnancy)
  • Breastfeeding (excreted in breast milk; avoid)
  • Concomitant ASA or other NSAIDs (increases toxicity)
  • Use for >5 days increases serious adverse events (GI bleed, renal dysfunction, cardiovascular events)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset, nausea; GI ulceration and bleeding (dose and duration dependent)
  • Renal impairment/acute kidney injury (especially with dehydration or pre-existing renal disease)
  • Increased bleeding risk (inhibits platelet function)
  • Injection site pain (IM)
  • Headache, dizziness, drowsiness
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, bronchospasm, anaphylaxis in susceptible patients)

Preparation & administration

  • IV: May give undiluted as slow IV push over ≥15 seconds; flush line before/after
  • IM: Deep IM injection into large muscle (gluteal or deltoid); rotate sites if multiple doses
  • PO: Take with food or milk to reduce GI upset
  • Ensure adequate hydration before and during therapy
  • Do not exceed 5 days total therapy (combined parenteral + oral)
  • Do not co-administer with other NSAIDs or ASA

Monitoring

  • Pain relief (assess response within 30–60 minutes IV/IM; reassess need for ongoing therapy)
  • Signs of GI bleeding (melena, hematemesis, abdominal pain)
  • Renal function monitoring in at-risk patients (dehydration, older adults, pre-existing renal impairment)
  • Hydration status (maintain adequate oral/IV fluids)
  • Bleeding events (surgical sites, bruising, epistaxis)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, respiratory symptoms)

At-sea considerations

  • Reserve for short-term use in moderate-severe pain when other analgesics insufficient; reassess daily
  • Document start date clearly and enforce 5-day maximum to prevent serious adverse events
  • High risk in dehydration/heat stress environments—ensure hydration and monitor renal risk carefully
  • If patient on anticoagulants or antiplatelets, weigh bleeding risk vs benefit; consider alternatives
  • Not appropriate for chronic pain or routine mild pain (use acetaminophen/ibuprofen when appropriate)
  • If administering IM, ensure proper technique to minimize injection site complications
