Dextromethorphan

Stocked on board

  • Dextromethorphan 15mg/5mL Syrup 100mL

Adult dosing

  • Cough suppression (symptomatic relief of non-productive cough)
    • 10–20 mg PO every 4 hours as needed, OR
    • 30 mg PO every 6–8 hours as needed
    • Maximum daily dose: 120 mg/day (follow product labeling; some sources allow higher doses)
    • Syrup concentration onboard: 15 mg/5 mL, so typical adult dose is 10–20 mL (30–60 mg) every 6–8 hours

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of non-productive (dry) cough associated with upper respiratory tract infections
  • Not indicated for productive cough (cough with sputum expectoration) or chronic cough from smoking/asthma/COPD

Contraindications / cautions

  • Concurrent use or recent use (within 14 days) of monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs)—risk of serotonin syndrome
  • Hypersensitivity to dextromethorphan
  • Chronic productive cough or cough with excessive secretions (may impair clearance)
  • Asthma, COPD exacerbation (address underlying cause; cough suppressants generally not recommended)
  • Caution with other serotonergic medications (SSRIs, SNRIs, etc.)—potential serotonin syndrome risk, though uncommon at therapeutic doses
  • Caution with CNS depressants/sedatives (additive sedation)
  • Abuse potential: dextromethorphan can be misused at high doses for dissociative effects; counsel appropriate use

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness, dizziness
  • Nausea, vomiting, GI upset (uncommon at therapeutic doses)
  • Confusion, hallucinations (at high/supratherapeutic doses)
  • Serotonin syndrome (rare; risk increased with MAOIs or multiple serotonergic agents)

Preparation & administration

  • Measure dose accurately using dosing cup or syringe (avoid "tablespoon" estimates)
  • May take with or without food
  • Encourage adequate hydration (helps with overall symptom management)
  • Counsel: use only for short-term symptomatic relief (typically 3–7 days); seek medical attention if cough persists >7 days, worsens, or is accompanied by high fever, rash, or persistent headache
  • Avoid combining with other cough/cold products containing dextromethorphan (risk of overdose)

Monitoring

  • Cough frequency and severity (expect gradual improvement)
  • Sedation/dizziness (caution with watchkeeping and operational duties)
  • Worsening symptoms or development of productive cough, fever, or respiratory distress (reassess diagnosis and consider antibiotics or other treatment if indicated)
  • Monitor for misuse/overuse, particularly in longer voyages

At-sea considerations

  • Upper respiratory infections are common in close-quarters environments; dextromethorphan provides symptomatic relief but does not treat underlying viral infection
  • Emphasize supportive care (hydration, rest) and infection control (hand hygiene, respiratory etiquette) to limit spread
  • If cough becomes productive or patient develops fever/dyspnea, reassess for bacterial superinfection (pneumonia, sinusitis) and consider antibiotics if indicated
  • Sedation may impair watchkeeping or operational tasks; schedule dosing appropriately or consider non-sedating alternatives if available
  • Document dose and duration to prevent overuse and ensure appropriate follow-up if symptoms persist
