Alginic Acid with Aluminum

Stocked on board

  • Alginic Acid with Aluminum Tablets

Adult dosing

Gastroesophageal reflux, heartburn, acid indigestion (symptomatic relief)

  • Typical formulation (e.g., Gaviscon Regular Strength Tablets): 2–4 tablets chewed thoroughly after meals and at bedtime as needed
  • Maximum: Follow product-specific labeling (typically up to 16 tablets/day; do not exceed recommended daily dose)
  • Take after meals and at bedtime for best effect

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of heartburn and acid indigestion associated with gastroesophageal reflux
  • Symptomatic relief of upset stomach associated with excess stomach acid
  • Alginic acid forms a protective "raft" that floats on stomach contents, reducing reflux symptoms

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to alginic acid, aluminum-containing antacids, or any product component
  • Severe renal impairment: aluminum can accumulate and cause toxicity (encephalopathy, bone disease) with chronic use
  • Hypophosphatemia risk with prolonged or high-dose aluminum antacid use
  • Drug interactions: antacids can reduce absorption of many medications (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, iron, levothyroxine, bisphosphonates, etc.)—separate administration by 2–4 hours
  • Not for chronic daily use without medical evaluation; persistent symptoms may indicate more serious GI disease
  • Sodium content: some formulations contain significant sodium—caution in heart failure, hypertension, or sodium-restricted diets (check product label)

Adverse effects

  • Constipation (aluminum component)
  • Chalky taste
  • Hypophosphatemia (with prolonged use)
  • Aluminum accumulation in renal impairment (chronic use)
  • Generally well tolerated for short-term, intermittent use

Preparation & administration

  • Chew tablets thoroughly before swallowing for best effect
  • Take after meals and at bedtime when reflux symptoms are most likely
  • May follow with water if desired
  • Separate from other oral medications by at least 2 hours to avoid absorption interactions
  • For occasional use only; if using >2 weeks continuously, reassess and consider medical evaluation

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief and frequency of use
  • If symptoms persist or worsen despite treatment, reassess for alternative diagnosis (peptic ulcer disease, gastritis, cardiac chest pain, etc.)
  • Monitor for constipation; encourage hydration and fiber
  • If prolonged use (>2 weeks) or high-dose use: consider monitoring for hypophosphatemia and aluminum accumulation (especially in renal impairment)

At-sea considerations

  • Useful for symptomatic relief of occasional heartburn/reflux onboard (common with irregular meal timing, stress)
  • Encourage lifestyle measures: elevate head of bed, avoid large/late meals, avoid trigger foods (caffeine, alcohol, spicy/fatty foods), smoking cessation
  • If symptoms are frequent or severe, consider alternative diagnosis (cardiac, biliary) or need for stronger acid suppression (PPI if available)
  • Document use pattern; frequent need may indicate need for medical evaluation ashore
  • Warn about medication interactions if patient is on other oral medications—space dosing appropriately
