Respiratory / Allergy

Fluticasone

Stocked on board

  • Fluticasone 50mcg Nasal Spray

Adult dosing

  • Allergic rhinitis (seasonal or perennial)
    • Initial: 2 sprays (50 mcg each = 100 mcg total) per nostril once daily
    • Maintenance: Once symptoms controlled, may reduce to 1 spray per nostril once daily
    • Maximum: 2 sprays per nostril once daily (200 mcg total per day)
  • Nasal polyps (if indicated)
    • Typically requires higher doses/different formulations; follow provider direction

Indications

  • Seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever)
  • Perennial allergic rhinitis
  • Non-allergic rhinitis (selected cases per provider direction)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to fluticasone or formulation components
  • Recent nasal surgery or trauma (allow healing before use; risk of impaired healing)
  • Active or untreated nasal infection (bacterial, fungal, viral including herpes simplex)
  • Caution with tuberculosis (latent or active), ocular herpes simplex
  • Chronic use: monitor for nasal septal perforation, epistaxis, local fungal infection (Candida)
  • Systemic absorption generally minimal but prolonged high-dose use may cause systemic corticosteroid effects (growth suppression in children, HPA axis suppression, etc.)

Adverse effects

  • Epistaxis (nosebleed), nasal irritation/burning (most common)
  • Headache
  • Pharyngitis, cough
  • Rare: nasal septal perforation (with prolonged use or improper technique)
  • Rare: local fungal infection (thrush in nose/throat)
  • Systemic effects uncommon at recommended doses

Preparation & administration

  • Prime the pump before first use or if not used for ≥7 days (shake well, spray into air until fine mist appears)
  • Shake well before each use
  • Clear nasal passages gently (blow nose) before administration
  • Administration technique:
    • Tilt head slightly forward (not backward)
    • Insert nozzle into nostril, aiming spray toward outer wall of nose (away from septum) to reduce septal irritation/bleeding risk
    • Close opposite nostril, spray while inhaling gently
    • Avoid blowing nose immediately after use
  • Clean nozzle regularly per product instructions to prevent clogging
  • Onset of benefit may take 12–24 hours; maximal effect typically 1–2 weeks (counsel on regular use even when asymptomatic for best control)

Monitoring

  • Symptom control (nasal congestion, rhinorrhea, sneezing, itching)
  • Epistaxis or nasal irritation (proper technique reduces risk)
  • Signs of local infection (unusual nasal discharge, pain, fever)
  • If used chronically: periodic nasal examination for septal integrity and mucosal health when feasible

At-sea considerations

  • Most effective when used regularly/preventively (not as-needed); plan supply for duration of allergy season or voyage
  • Counsel on proper technique to maximize efficacy and minimize bleeding risk (critical with limited medical resources)
  • Non-sedating option for rhinitis control (pairs well with antihistamines if needed)
  • If epistaxis develops, review technique and consider temporary discontinuation; minor bleeding usually self-limited
  • Store at controlled room temperature; protect from freezing
