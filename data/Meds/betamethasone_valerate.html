Betamethasone Valerate

Stocked on board

  • Betamethasone Valerate 0.1% Cream

Adult dosing

  • Inflammatory dermatoses (eczema, contact dermatitis, psoriasis, etc.)
    • Apply thin layer of 0.1% cream to affected area(s) 1â€“2 times daily
    • Use twice daily initially for acute flares; reduce to once daily as condition improves
    • Rub in gently and completely
  • Duration
    • Use for shortest duration necessary to control symptoms
    • Reassess if no improvement after 2 weeks
    • Avoid prolonged continuous use (risk of skin atrophy and systemic absorption)

Indications

  • Corticosteroid-responsive dermatoses including eczema, atopic dermatitis, contact dermatitis
  • Psoriasis (limited areas; not as sole therapy for extensive disease)
  • Seborrheic dermatitis
  • Other inflammatory skin conditions responsive to potent topical corticosteroids

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to betamethasone or other corticosteroids
  • Untreated bacterial, viral, or fungal skin infections (including rosacea, acne, perioral dermatitis)
  • Do not use on face, groin, or axillae unless specifically directed (higher absorption/atrophy risk)
  • Avoid occlusive dressings unless directed (increases absorption and side effect risk)
  • Caution in children: use lower potency agents when possible; higher risk of systemic absorption and growth suppression with extensive or prolonged use
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: use only if clearly needed and on limited areas
  • Not for ophthalmic use

Adverse effects

  • Local: skin atrophy, striae, telangiectasia (with prolonged use)
  • Burning, itching, dryness, irritation (especially initially)
  • Folliculitis, hypertrichosis, acneiform eruptions
  • Hypopigmentation
  • Secondary infection (fungal or bacterial)
  • Systemic effects (with extensive or prolonged use): HPA axis suppression, Cushing's syndrome, hyperglycemia
  • Contact dermatitis (allergic sensitization to corticosteroid or vehicle)

Preparation & administration

  • Wash and dry affected area before application
  • Apply thin layer and rub in gently until absorbed
  • Wash hands after application (unless hands are the treated area)
  • Avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes
  • Do not apply to open wounds or weeping lesions unless specifically directed
  • If using on large areas or under occlusion: limit duration and monitor for systemic effects
  • For eczema: may use with emollients; apply emollient first, then topical steroid, or as directed

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (reduction in erythema, pruritus, scaling)
  • Signs of skin atrophy (thinning, shiny appearance, easy bruising) with prolonged use
  • Secondary infection (worsening redness, pustules, lack of improvement)
  • If no improvement after 2 weeks: reassess diagnosis and treatment plan
  • If using extensively or for prolonged periods: monitor for signs of systemic corticosteroid effects (hyperglycemia, HPA suppression)

At-sea considerations

  • Potent topical steroid: effective for inflammatory dermatoses but requires careful use to avoid adverse effects
  • Environmental factors (sun exposure, salt water, dry air, friction from clothing/gear) can exacerbate dermatitis; address environmental triggers when possible
  • Counsel on proper use: thin layer, limited duration, avoid face/intertriginous areas unless directed
  • If skin infection suspected (bacterial/fungal): treat infection before or concurrently with topical steroid, or use combination product if available and appropriate
  • Taper frequency as condition improves rather than abrupt discontinuation (helps prevent rebound flare)
  • Document area treated, frequency, and duration to track cumulative exposure
