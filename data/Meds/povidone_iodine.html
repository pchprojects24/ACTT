Povidone-Iodine

Stocked on board

  • Povidone-Iodine 10% Solution 1 Litre

Adult dosing

  • Skin antisepsis (pre-procedural/surgical site preparation)
    • Apply 10% solution directly to skin and allow to dry (typically 2 minutes or per protocol)
    • May apply with sterile gauze or applicator
    • Do not wipe off once dry (leave on for procedure)
  • Wound cleansing/irrigation (diluted solution)
    • Dilute 10% povidone-iodine solution to 1% or less (e.g., 1 part 10% solution to 9 parts sterile water/saline)
    • Use diluted solution to irrigate or cleanse wound
    • Rinse thoroughly after use if appropriate for wound type
  • Skin disinfection (minor wounds, abrasions)
    • Apply 10% solution to intact or broken skin; allow to dry
    • May be used 1–3 times daily or as needed per clinical situation

Indications

  • Broad-spectrum antiseptic for skin disinfection prior to procedures, injections, or surgery
  • Wound cleansing and irrigation (when diluted appropriately)
  • Prevention of infection in minor cuts, abrasions, and burns
  • Pre-operative skin preparation

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known hypersensitivity or allergy to iodine or povidone-iodine
  • Caution in patients with thyroid disorders (prolonged/extensive use may affect thyroid function due to iodine absorption)
  • Avoid use in neonates and premature infants (risk of thyroid dysfunction and skin irritation)
  • Not for use in eyes (may cause corneal damage)
  • Avoid concurrent use with other antiseptics (e.g., hydrogen peroxide) as efficacy may be reduced
  • Do not use on large open wounds or severe burns without medical supervision (systemic absorption risk)
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: use with caution; iodine can be absorbed systemically

Adverse effects

  • Local skin irritation, redness, or dryness
  • Allergic reactions (rash, itching, rarely anaphylaxis)
  • Staining of skin and clothing (temporary; washes off over time)
  • Systemic iodine absorption with extensive/prolonged use (may cause thyroid dysfunction, metabolic acidosis—rare)
  • Chemical burns if used at full strength on sensitive or open tissue for prolonged periods

Preparation & administration

  • For skin prep: apply 10% solution directly; allow to dry completely before procedure
  • For wound irrigation: dilute 10% solution to 1% or less with sterile water or saline before use
  • Do not apply heat to accelerate drying (may inactivate iodine)
  • Store at room temperature; protect from light; avoid freezing
  • Discard solution if it becomes lighter in color (indicates loss of potency)
  • Use clean or sterile technique when applying to open wounds

Monitoring

  • Local skin reaction (irritation, rash, hypersensitivity)
  • Wound healing and signs of infection (redness, swelling, purulent drainage)
  • For prolonged or extensive use: monitor for signs of systemic iodine toxicity (rare) such as metallic taste, increased salivation, GI upset
  • Thyroid function if used extensively over large areas or prolonged periods (especially in at-risk patients)

At-sea considerations

  • Essential broad-spectrum antiseptic for procedural skin prep and wound care in remote settings
  • Ensure proper dilution for wound irrigation to avoid tissue toxicity (full-strength may delay healing in some wounds)
  • Useful for pre-injection site cleaning, IV insertion site prep, and minor surgical procedures
  • Document any allergic history before use; have alternative antiseptic available if iodine allergy known
  • Store properly to maintain efficacy; check color before use (should be deep brown/amber)
