Metformin

Stocked on board

  • Metformin 500mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Note: Metformin is typically stocked for continuation therapy in known diabetics aboard ship; initiation of new metformin therapy should be provider-directed.

  • Type 2 diabetes mellitus (continuation therapy)
    • Continue patient's established dose (common maintenance doses range from 500 mg once daily to 1000 mg twice daily)
    • Maximum dose: 2000–2550 mg/day (divided doses), but follow patient's home regimen
  • If initiating therapy onboard (provider-directed only)
    • Start 500 mg PO once or twice daily with meals
    • Titrate slowly (every 1–2 weeks) to minimize GI side effects; follow medical direction

Indications

  • Type 2 diabetes mellitus (first-line oral agent when appropriate)
  • Continuation therapy for patients with established diabetes already on metformin
  • Off-label: polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), prediabetes prevention (provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Contraindicated: significant renal impairment (eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²; use caution if <45); metabolic acidosis; acute/unstable heart failure
  • Lactic acidosis risk: hold during acute illness with dehydration, sepsis, hypoxia, or shock
  • Hold before and for 48 hours after iodinated contrast studies (risk of contrast-induced nephropathy → lactic acidosis)
  • Excessive alcohol intake increases lactic acidosis risk
  • Caution in hepatic impairment; avoid in severe liver disease
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency: long-term use may reduce B12 absorption (monitor if feasible)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort) – common, especially at initiation; often improves with dose titration and taking with food
  • Metallic taste
  • Lactic acidosis (rare but serious; risk increased in renal impairment, acute illness, dehydration)
  • Hypoglycemia risk is low when used alone (but increases with insulin or sulfonylureas)
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term use)

Preparation & administration

  • Take with meals to reduce GI side effects
  • Swallow tablets whole with water; do not crush extended-release formulations (if onboard)
  • Maintain adequate hydration
  • Hold during acute illness (vomiting, diarrhea, dehydration) and restart only when stable and eating/drinking normally

Monitoring

  • Blood glucose (if glucometer available onboard): target fasting ~4–7 mmol/L, postprandial <10 mmol/L per guidelines
  • Symptoms of hyperglycemia (polyuria, polydipsia, fatigue) and hypoglycemia (shakiness, sweating, confusion)
  • GI side effects; if severe/persistent diarrhea or vomiting, hold metformin and reassess
  • Signs of lactic acidosis (rare): unexplained hyperventilation, severe fatigue, muscle pain, abdominal pain; stop metformin and seek urgent care
  • Renal function (if labs available): ensure eGFR adequate before continuing therapy

At-sea considerations

  • Primarily a continuation medication; verify patient's home dose and recent glucose control/renal function if possible
  • If patient becomes acutely unwell (vomiting, dehydration, infection, sepsis), hold metformin until stable to reduce lactic acidosis risk
  • Limited lab capability: be conservative; if renal function unknown and patient is older/at risk, consider medical consultation
  • Document blood glucose readings when available to assess control and guide ongoing management
  • Ensure patient has adequate supply for voyage duration; arrange resupply or medical evacuation if medication runs out
