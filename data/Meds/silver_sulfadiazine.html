Silver Sulfadiazine

Stocked on board

  • Silver Sulfadiazine 1% Cream

Adult dosing

  • Second- and third-degree burns
    • Apply 1% cream to a thickness of approximately 1/16 inch (1–2 mm) to cover the burned area
    • Apply once or twice daily using sterile technique
    • Keep burn covered with cream at all times; reapply if removed by patient activity or wound care
    • Continue until healing is progressing or until site is ready for grafting

Indications

  • Prevention and treatment of infection in second- and third-degree burns
  • Adjunct to burn wound management to reduce bacterial colonization

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known hypersensitivity to silver sulfadiazine, sulfonamides, or any component of the cream
  • Avoid use in premature infants or neonates less than 2 months of age (risk of kernicterus from sulfonamide)
  • Pregnancy (especially near term): sulfonamides may increase risk of kernicterus in newborn; use only if clearly needed
  • Breastfeeding: use with caution; sulfonamides excreted in breast milk
  • G6PD deficiency: sulfonamides may cause hemolytic anemia in susceptible patients
  • Hepatic or renal impairment: monitor for systemic sulfonamide accumulation with extensive burns
  • Avoid use on face near eyes or on large areas of the body without medical supervision (systemic absorption risk)
  • Prolonged use may lead to fungal superinfection or resistant organisms

Adverse effects

  • Burning sensation at application site (usually transient)
  • Skin discoloration (gray or blue-gray; reversible)
  • Rash, itching, or allergic contact dermatitis
  • Leukopenia (transient; usually resolves without discontinuation; monitor CBC in extensive burns)
  • Rare: hemolytic anemia (especially in G6PD deficiency), interstitial nephritis, hepatotoxicity
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis—rare)
  • Fungal superinfection with prolonged use

Preparation & administration

  • Clean and debride burn wound as appropriate before application
  • Apply using sterile technique (sterile gloves or sterile applicator)
  • Apply to thickness of approximately 1/16 inch (1–2 mm) to cover entire burn area
  • Reapply cream whenever it is removed by patient activity or during dressing changes
  • May be used with or without dressings depending on burn management protocol
  • Continue therapy until burn is healed or ready for grafting
  • If cream darkens (due to reaction with wound exudate), this is expected and does not indicate loss of efficacy

Monitoring

  • Wound healing progress and signs of infection (increasing redness, purulent drainage, fever)
  • Allergic reaction or hypersensitivity (rash, worsening pain, systemic symptoms)
  • For extensive burns: monitor CBC (especially WBC count) every 2–3 days during first weeks of therapy (leukopenia screening)
  • Renal and hepatic function if extensive burns or prolonged use (systemic absorption risk)
  • Signs of fungal superinfection (especially with prolonged use)

At-sea considerations

  • Essential for burn management in remote settings where infection risk is high
  • Ensure proper wound cleaning and debridement before application to maximize efficacy
  • Use sterile technique to minimize contamination and infection risk
  • For large or severe burns, prioritize evacuation while initiating therapy
  • Document burn size (% body surface area), depth, and location for continuity of care
  • Limited lab monitoring at sea: prioritize clinical assessment for signs of infection, healing, or adverse effects
  • Store at room temperature; do not freeze; protect from light
  • Screen for sulfonamide allergy history before use
