Antibiotics / Antivirals

Ampicillin

Stocked on board

  • Ampicillin 500mg Injection

Adult dosing

  • Respiratory tract infections (mild-moderate pneumonia)
    • 250–500 mg IM/IV every 6 hours
    • Severe infections: up to 1–2 g IV every 4–6 hours
  • Sepsis / severe infections
    • 1–2 g IV every 4–6 hours (in combination with other antibiotics as per clinical guidelines)
  • Bacterial meningitis (suspected/confirmed)
    • 2 g IV every 4 hours (urgent medical evacuation required; follow local protocols)
  • Endocarditis (streptococcal)
    • Dosing varies; typically 2 g IV every 4–6 hours in combination therapy (follow infectious disease guidance)
  • Urinary tract infections
    • 250–500 mg IM/IV every 6 hours (if oral therapy not appropriate)

Indications

  • Susceptible bacterial infections including respiratory tract, urinary tract, GI, and soft tissue infections
  • Listeria monocytogenes infections
  • Enterococcal infections (often in combination therapy)
  • Bacterial meningitis (in combination with other antibiotics)
  • Sepsis when susceptible organisms suspected (empiric or directed therapy)

Contraindications / cautions

  • History of immediate hypersensitivity to penicillins or beta-lactam antibiotics
  • Cross-reactivity with cephalosporins possible (caution based on reaction severity)
  • Infectious mononucleosis or other viral infections (high risk of rash with aminopenicillins)
  • Dose adjustment required in significant renal impairment
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk: monitor for severe diarrhea during/after therapy
  • Drug interactions: may increase methotrexate levels; may reduce effectiveness of oral contraceptives

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
  • Rash (particularly in infectious mononucleosis)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions ranging from urticaria to anaphylaxis
  • Pain at injection site (IM); thrombophlebitis (IV)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea
  • Rarely: seizures (with high doses, especially in renal impairment or meningitis)

Preparation & administration

  • IM administration: Reconstitute 500 mg vial with 1.8 mL sterile water for injection to yield 250 mg/mL. Inject deep IM into large muscle mass
  • IV administration: Reconstitute and dilute as per product monograph. May give by slow IV push (over 3–5 minutes) or IV infusion (over 15–30 minutes)
  • Use freshly prepared solutions; stability varies (check product monograph for reconstitution stability)
  • Administer at evenly spaced intervals to maintain therapeutic levels
  • Monitor injection sites for signs of local reactions or phlebitis

Monitoring

  • Clinical response within 48–72 hours (fever curve, symptoms, vital signs)
  • Allergic reactions: watch for rash, urticaria, bronchospasm, or signs of anaphylaxis
  • Injection site reactions (pain, swelling, phlebitis)
  • Diarrhea severity (consider C. difficile if severe or persistent)
  • Renal function in prolonged therapy or high-risk patients when feasible
  • CNS effects (confusion, seizures) especially in renal impairment or high-dose therapy

At-sea considerations

  • Reserve for serious infections when parenteral therapy required and oral options inadequate
  • Ensure proper storage and check expiry dates; reconstituted solutions have limited stability
  • Document allergy history carefully before administration
  • Have epinephrine and resuscitation equipment available due to anaphylaxis risk
  • For severe infections requiring ampicillin, plan early for medical evacuation as appropriate
  • If limited diagnostic capacity, reassess non-response early and escalate care
