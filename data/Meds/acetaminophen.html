Analgesia / Anti-inflammatory

Acetaminophen

Stocked on board

  • Acetaminophen 325mg Tablets (24/bottle)
  • Acetaminophen 325mg Tablets/Caplets

Adult dosing

  • Pain/fever (typical short-term use)
    • 325–650 mg PO every 4–6 hours as needed
    • Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration
  • Maximum daily dose
    • Do not exceed 3,250–4,000 mg in 24 hours (follow local/product guidance)
    • Many authorities recommend max 3,000 mg/day for chronic use to reduce hepatotoxicity risk

Indications

  • Mild to moderate pain (headache, muscle aches, dental pain, menstrual cramps)
  • Fever reduction
  • Preferred analgesic/antipyretic when NSAIDs contraindicated (e.g., peptic ulcer, anticoagulant use, pregnancy)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known hypersensitivity to acetaminophen
  • Severe hepatic impairment or active liver disease
  • Chronic alcohol use: increased risk of hepatotoxicity; use with caution and lower doses
  • Patients taking multiple acetaminophen-containing products: risk of accidental overdose
  • Renal/hepatic impairment: dose reduction may be required

Adverse effects

  • Generally well-tolerated at recommended doses
  • Nausea, vomiting, stomach pain (uncommon)
  • Hepatotoxicity: major risk with overdose or chronic excessive use; can lead to acute liver failure
  • Rare: severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome), allergic reactions

Preparation & administration

  • Take with water; may take with or without food
  • Counsel patients to check all medications for acetaminophen content to avoid "double-dosing"
  • Do not combine with other acetaminophen-containing products (e.g., cold/flu medications)

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief (pain/fever)
  • Signs of hepatotoxicity: jaundice, dark urine, right upper quadrant pain, nausea/vomiting, fatigue
  • If prolonged use or high-risk patient: consider liver function monitoring when feasible
  • Total daily acetaminophen intake from all sources

At-sea considerations

  • Safest analgesic/antipyretic for most patients; first-line for mild-moderate pain/fever
  • Document dose and timing to prevent inadvertent overdose if multiple caregivers involved
  • Maintain awareness of acetaminophen in combination products onboard (e.g., with codeine, cold medications)
  • If accidental overdose suspected: activate emergency protocols; N-acetylcysteine is the antidote (time-sensitive)
