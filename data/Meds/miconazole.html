Miconazole

Stocked on board

  • Miconazole 2% Cream

Adult dosing

  • Cutaneous fungal infections (tinea corporis, tinea cruris, tinea pedis)
    • Apply topically to affected area twice daily (morning and evening)
    • Continue for 2–4 weeks or until at least 1 week after symptoms resolve
  • Cutaneous candidiasis (yeast infections of skin/folds)
    • Apply topically to affected area twice daily
    • Typical duration: 2–4 weeks; reassess if no improvement

Indications

  • Treatment of superficial fungal skin infections (dermatophytes: tinea corporis, tinea cruris, tinea pedis)
  • Treatment of cutaneous candidiasis (yeast infections in skin folds, groin, interdigital areas)
  • May be used for selected vaginal yeast infections (if vaginal formulation available; 2% cream is topical for skin use)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to miconazole or other imidazole antifungals
  • Avoid application to eyes, mucous membranes, or open wounds (unless specifically formulated for that use)
  • If infection does not improve after 2–4 weeks, reassess diagnosis (may not be fungal or may require systemic therapy)
  • Pregnancy/lactation: topical use generally considered safe; minimal systemic absorption

Adverse effects

  • Local irritation, burning, itching at application site
  • Contact dermatitis (uncommon)
  • Systemic effects are rare with topical use (minimal absorption)

Preparation & administration

  • Clean and dry the affected area before application
  • Apply a thin layer of cream to affected area and surrounding skin (~1 cm margin)
  • Rub in gently
  • Wash hands thoroughly after application (unless hands are the treatment area)
  • For tinea pedis (athlete's foot): treat between toes and entire foot; ensure feet are kept dry; change socks daily
  • Continue treatment for full course even if symptoms improve early (to prevent recurrence)

Monitoring

  • Symptom improvement (reduced itching, redness, scaling) within 1–2 weeks
  • Local skin reaction (if worsening irritation/rash, consider contact dermatitis or incorrect diagnosis)
  • If no improvement after 2 weeks or worsening: reassess diagnosis; may require alternate therapy or systemic antifungal

At-sea considerations

  • Common indication: tinea pedis (athlete's foot), tinea cruris (jock itch) in warm/humid conditions with occlusive footwear or limited hygiene
  • Emphasize hygiene measures: keep area clean and dry, change socks/undergarments daily, avoid sharing towels
  • For persistent/recurrent infections: consider environmental factors (wet boots, shared showers) and encourage drying/ventilation
  • If infection spreads or becomes severe (pustules, cellulitis), consider bacterial superinfection and need for systemic therapy
  • Document treatment start date and planned duration; reassess at 2 weeks
