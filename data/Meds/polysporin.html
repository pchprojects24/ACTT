Polysporin

Stocked on board

  • Polysporin Ointment

Adult dosing

  • Minor cuts, scrapes, abrasions, and minor burns
    • Apply a small amount (thin layer) to the affected area 1–3 times daily after cleansing
    • May cover with a sterile bandage if appropriate

Indications

  • Prophylaxis/treatment of superficial skin infections in minor wounds (cuts, scrapes, abrasions)
  • First-degree burns and minor second-degree burns (small, superficial)
  • Protection of minor wounds to reduce risk of bacterial infection

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known hypersensitivity to polymyxin B, bacitracin, or any component of the ointment
  • Avoid use in deep or puncture wounds, animal bites, or serious burns (requires medical evaluation)
  • Not for use in eyes or on large areas of the body
  • Prolonged use may result in overgrowth of non-susceptible organisms (fungal superinfection)
  • Polymyxin B may cause nephrotoxicity and neurotoxicity if absorbed systemically (rare with topical use)

Adverse effects

  • Local irritation, itching, redness, or rash at application site
  • Allergic contact dermatitis (especially to neomycin in some formulations—Polysporin does NOT contain neomycin, which reduces this risk)
  • Rare: anaphylaxis or severe hypersensitivity reaction

Preparation & administration

  • Clean and dry the affected area before application
  • Apply a thin layer to cover the wound; do not overapply
  • May be covered with a sterile dressing or left open to air as appropriate
  • Wash hands before and after application
  • Discontinue if signs of hypersensitivity or irritation develop

Monitoring

  • Wound healing progress (expect improvement within 3–7 days for minor wounds)
  • Signs of infection (increasing redness, swelling, warmth, purulent drainage, fever)
  • Allergic reaction or contact dermatitis (rash, itching, worsening irritation)
  • If no improvement or worsening after 3–5 days, reassess and consider alternative therapy or medical evaluation

At-sea considerations

  • Useful first-line topical for minor wound care in remote settings
  • Ensure wounds are properly irrigated/cleaned before application (saline or clean water)
  • If wound becomes infected despite topical therapy, consider oral antibiotics and medical consultation
  • Document wound characteristics and response to treatment for continuity of care
  • Store in cool, dry place; avoid excessive heat which may affect ointment stability
