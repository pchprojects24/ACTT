Glycerin

Stocked on board

  • Glycerin 2g Suppositories

Adult dosing

  • Constipation (occasional/acute relief)
    • 1 suppository (2 g) rectally as needed
    • Onset of action typically within 15–30 minutes

Indications

  • Relief of occasional constipation
  • Bowel preparation (when other agents unavailable)
  • Stimulation of defecation when rectal stimulation is appropriate

Contraindications / cautions

  • Rectal bleeding or fissures (rectal irritation risk)
  • Nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain (possible obstruction or acute abdomen; do not use without assessment)
  • Known or suspected bowel obstruction or fecal impaction requiring disimpaction
  • Rectal surgery or inflammatory bowel disease (mucosal injury risk)
  • Prolonged use not recommended; address underlying cause of constipation

Adverse effects

  • Rectal discomfort, burning, or irritation
  • Cramping (mild, transient)
  • Rectal urgency
  • Rarely: hypersensitivity reaction

Preparation & administration

  • Insert suppository rectally (pointed end first) after removing wrapper
  • If suppository is soft, chill in refrigerator or run under cold water (with wrapper on) before use
  • Retain suppository for a few minutes to allow absorption before bowel movement
  • May be used with or without additional bowel prep agents depending on clinical need

Monitoring

  • Onset of bowel movement (typically 15–30 minutes)
  • Relief of constipation symptoms
  • If no response or symptoms worsen: reassess for impaction, obstruction, or other cause
  • Monitor for rectal discomfort or bleeding

At-sea considerations

  • First-line for acute constipation when oral agents have failed or are not tolerated
  • If limited diagnostic capability and abdominal pain present: rule out acute abdomen before use
  • Address contributing factors: hydration, diet, opioid use, immobility
  • If repeated use needed: consider underlying causes and reassess treatment plan
