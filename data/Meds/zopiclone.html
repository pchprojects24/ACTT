Zopiclone

Stocked on board

  • Zopiclone 7.5mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Insomnia (short-term treatment)
    • 7.5 mg PO at bedtime (taken immediately before bed or after getting into bed)
    • Use lowest effective dose for shortest duration (typically 7–10 days, maximum 4 weeks)
  • Older adults (≥65 years) or debilitated patients
    • Initial: 3.75 mg PO at bedtime (half tablet if scored, or use lower-strength formulation if available)
    • May increase to 7.5 mg if needed and tolerated, but use caution
  • Hepatic impairment
    • Start with 3.75 mg; avoid in severe hepatic impairment
  • Renal impairment
    • Mild-moderate: no dose adjustment usually needed; use caution
    • Severe (CrCl <10 mL/min) or dialysis: start with 3.75 mg and monitor closely

Indications

  • Short-term treatment of insomnia (difficulty falling asleep, frequent nocturnal awakenings, early morning awakening)
  • Transient insomnia due to situational stress or environmental factors (e.g., shift work, time zone changes)
  • NOTE: Use only for short-term management onboard; reassess need frequently and discontinue as soon as feasible

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to zopiclone or other cyclopyrrolones
  • Severe respiratory insufficiency, sleep apnea syndrome, myasthenia gravis (risk of respiratory depression)
  • Severe hepatic impairment (risk of encephalopathy)
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding: avoid (sedation and withdrawal risk in newborn)
  • History of substance abuse or dependence (zopiclone has abuse potential and risk of dependence with prolonged use)
  • CNS depression: avoid with alcohol, opioids, benzodiazepines, or other sedatives (additive effects)
  • Complex sleep behaviors (sleepwalking, sleep-driving, sleep-eating): discontinue if occurs
  • Depression/suicidal ideation: may worsen depression; monitor closely
  • Older adults: increased fall risk, confusion, cognitive impairment; use lowest dose

Adverse effects

  • Bitter or metallic taste (very common; may persist into next day)
  • Drowsiness, dizziness, headache
  • Dry mouth, nausea
  • Daytime sedation, impaired coordination (especially if insufficient sleep time or with alcohol/other CNS depressants)
  • Amnesia, confusion (especially in older adults)
  • Complex sleep behaviors (sleepwalking, sleep-driving) — rare but serious
  • Dependence and withdrawal symptoms with prolonged use (anxiety, rebound insomnia, tremor, sweating)
  • Respiratory depression (rare, more likely with overdose or concurrent CNS depressants)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; take immediately before bedtime or after getting into bed
  • Ensure patient has 7–8 hours available for sleep (risk of daytime impairment if insufficient sleep time)
  • Avoid alcohol and other CNS depressants
  • Do not take with or immediately after a heavy or high-fat meal (may delay onset)
  • Short-term use only (7–10 days; maximum 4 weeks); reassess frequently and discontinue as soon as possible
  • If used for more than a few nights, taper dose gradually to avoid rebound insomnia and withdrawal
  • Counsel on sleep hygiene: avoid caffeine/stimulants before bed, maintain regular sleep schedule, minimize screen time, create restful environment

Monitoring

  • Sleep quality and duration; reassess need for continued therapy daily
  • Daytime sedation, cognitive impairment, coordination (especially before watchkeeping duties)
  • Fall risk (especially in older adults or those on other sedatives)
  • Complex sleep behaviors: question patient about any unusual nighttime activities
  • Signs of dependence or misuse: escalating dose requests, preoccupation with medication
  • Mood changes, depression, suicidal ideation (especially in patients with psychiatric history)

At-sea considerations

  • Operational safety: zopiclone causes significant sedation—patient must have 7–8 hours off-duty after dose; do NOT use before watchkeeping or critical operations
  • First dose should be given when patient has full night off duty to assess individual response (residual morning sedation varies)
  • Use only for transient insomnia; reassess daily and stop as soon as sleep normalizes
  • Non-pharmacologic approaches first: optimize sleep environment (noise reduction, darkness, temperature), limit caffeine, encourage regular sleep schedule, address underlying anxiety/stress
  • Avoid in patients with known sleep apnea or loud snoring unless already established on therapy and monitored
  • Document indication, dose, start date, planned duration, and nightly use clearly; limit supply to avoid prolonged use
  • If patient requests continuation beyond 7–10 days, reassess for underlying sleep disorder, mood disorder, or medication side effects and consider evacuation for formal evaluation
  • Taper if used for >3–5 consecutive nights: reduce dose by half for 2–3 nights before stopping to minimize rebound insomnia
