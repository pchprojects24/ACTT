Benzoyl Peroxide

Stocked on board

  • Benzoyl Peroxide 5% Gel 60gm

Adult dosing

  • Acne vulgaris (mild to moderate)
    • Apply thin layer of 5% gel to affected areas once daily initially
    • May increase to twice daily (morning and evening) after skin tolerance established (usually after 1–2 weeks)
    • Start with once daily application to minimize irritation
  • Duration
    • Improvement typically seen in 4–6 weeks; continued use required to maintain benefit

Indications

  • Mild to moderate acne vulgaris
  • Antibacterial and keratolytic agent for acne treatment

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to benzoyl peroxide
  • Avoid contact with eyes, lips, mucous membranes, and broken or inflamed skin
  • May cause bleaching of hair and colored fabrics (clothing, towels, bedding)
  • Increased sun sensitivity: use sunscreen and limit sun exposure
  • Caution with concurrent topical acne products (retinoids, salicylic acid) due to increased irritation risk
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: generally considered safe but consult as appropriate

Adverse effects

  • Skin dryness, peeling, erythema (common, especially initially)
  • Burning, stinging, irritation
  • Contact dermatitis/allergic reaction (rare)
  • Photosensitivity

Preparation & administration

  • Wash and dry affected area before application
  • Apply thin layer to affected areas (not spot treatment only; apply to entire acne-prone area)
  • Wash hands thoroughly after application
  • Allow to dry completely before applying other products or cosmetics
  • Start with once daily (preferably evening) and increase to twice daily as tolerated
  • Use non-comedogenic moisturizer if dryness develops
  • Avoid excessive washing or harsh scrubbing which increases irritation

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (reduction in acne lesions over 4–6 weeks)
  • Skin irritation/dryness (adjust frequency if excessive)
  • Allergic contact dermatitis (persistent rash, worsening erythema)
  • If no improvement after 8–12 weeks or worsening: reassess treatment plan

At-sea considerations

  • Warn about bleaching of fabrics; use white towels/pillowcases or allow product to dry thoroughly
  • Sun exposure: reinforce sun protection measures (sunscreen, hats, protective clothing) particularly important in maritime environment
  • Initial irritation is common and usually improves with continued use; counsel on expectation management
  • Consider moisture barrier function in harsh weather/environmental conditions (wind, salt water); may need additional moisturization
  • If severe irritation develops: reduce frequency or temporarily discontinue and reassess
