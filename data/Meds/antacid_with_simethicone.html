Antacid with Simethicone

Stocked on board

  • Antacid with Simethicone Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Heartburn / dyspepsia / gas
    • Chew 2–4 tablets as needed when symptoms occur
    • Maximum: typically 8–12 tablets per 24 hours (check specific product label)
    • Do not use maximum dose for more than 2 weeks except under medical advice

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of heartburn, acid indigestion, and sour stomach
  • Relief of bloating and gas (due to simethicone component)
  • Short-term management of dyspepsia

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to antacid components (aluminum, magnesium, calcium, or simethicone)
  • Severe renal impairment (aluminum and magnesium can accumulate)
  • Hypercalcemia or hypophosphatemia (if calcium-based antacid)
  • Drug interactions: antacids can reduce absorption of many medications (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, bisphosphonates, thyroid hormones, iron); separate dosing by 2–4 hours when possible
  • Milk-alkali syndrome risk with excessive calcium carbonate use
  • If symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks or worsen, seek medical evaluation (may indicate more serious condition)

Adverse effects

  • Diarrhea (more common with magnesium-containing antacids)
  • Constipation (more common with aluminum or calcium-containing antacids)
  • Nausea
  • Rarely: electrolyte disturbances with prolonged high-dose use (hypermagnesemia, hypophosphatemia)

Preparation & administration

  • Chew tablets thoroughly before swallowing for best effect
  • May be taken with water
  • Best taken after meals and at bedtime when treating heartburn
  • If taking other medications, separate antacid use by at least 2 hours to avoid interaction
  • Counsel: this is for symptomatic relief only; persistent symptoms require medical assessment

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief (onset typically within minutes)
  • Bowel pattern changes (diarrhea or constipation)
  • Duration of use: if symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks, reassess for possible peptic ulcer disease, GERD, or other pathology
  • If prolonged use in renal impairment: monitor for signs of magnesium or aluminum toxicity (rare)

At-sea considerations

  • Useful for minor dyspepsia related to diet, stress, or seasickness-related nausea
  • If symptoms are severe, persistent, or associated with alarm features (weight loss, dysphagia, hematemesis, melena), escalate care and consider evacuation
  • Document duration and frequency of use; frequent need may indicate need for alternate therapy (H2 blocker, PPI) if available and appropriate
  • Remind patients to separate from other medications (especially antibiotics) to avoid reduced effectiveness
