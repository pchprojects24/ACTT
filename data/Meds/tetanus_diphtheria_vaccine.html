Tetanus-Diphtheria Vaccine

Stocked on board

  • Tetanus-Diphtheria Vaccine 0.5mL

Adult dosing

  • Routine booster (primary series previously completed)
    • 0.5 mL IM every 10 years
    • Tdap (tetanus-diphtheria-acellular pertussis) preferred for one dose in adulthood; subsequent boosters may be Td
  • Wound management (tetanus prophylaxis)
    • Clean, minor wounds:
      • If last dose >10 years ago: give Td or Tdap (0.5 mL IM)
      • If <3 doses in lifetime or unknown history: complete primary series
    • Contaminated, deep, or high-risk wounds (puncture, crush, burn, soil contamination):
      • If last dose >5 years ago OR <3 doses/unknown history: give Td or Tdap (0.5 mL IM)
      • If <3 doses in lifetime or unknown history: also consider tetanus immunoglobulin (TIG) per protocol
  • Primary immunization series (previously unvaccinated or incomplete series)
    • 0.5 mL IM at 0, 4–8 weeks, and 6–12 months (3-dose series)
    • Tdap preferred for one dose; complete series with Td

Indications

  • Routine tetanus and diphtheria booster immunization in adults
  • Tetanus prophylaxis following injury (wound management)
  • Completion of primary immunization series in previously unvaccinated or incompletely vaccinated adults
  • Post-exposure prophylaxis for diphtheria (in outbreak settings, per public health guidance)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) to previous dose of tetanus or diphtheria toxoid-containing vaccine
  • Encephalopathy within 7 days of previous dose (pertussis component; relevant if Tdap used)
  • Progressive neurologic disorder (defer until stabilized)
  • Moderate to severe acute illness (defer until recovered; minor illness not a contraindication)
  • History of Arthus reaction or severe local reaction following previous dose: defer booster for at least 10 years
  • Pregnancy: Tdap recommended in each pregnancy (ideally 27–32 weeks) to provide passive immunity to infant; Td generally safe if needed
  • Immunocompromised patients: may receive vaccine but response may be reduced

Adverse effects

  • Local reactions (very common): pain, redness, swelling at injection site
  • Systemic reactions: headache, fatigue, myalgia, fever (usually mild and self-limited)
  • Severe local reactions (Arthus-type hypersensitivity): extensive limb swelling, severe pain (more common with frequent boosters; typically occurs if given too frequently)
  • Rare: anaphylaxis, brachial neuritis, Guillain-Barré syndrome (extremely rare; causal relationship uncertain)

Preparation & administration

  • Shake vial/syringe well before use (suspension; do not use if particulate matter or discoloration present)
  • Administer 0.5 mL IM (deltoid muscle preferred in adults)
  • Use aseptic technique; do not inject into same site as other vaccines (if multiple vaccines given)
  • Do not administer IV, subcutaneous, or intradermal
  • Document vaccine name, lot number, date, site of administration, and patient immunization history
  • Observe patient for 15 minutes post-vaccination for immediate allergic reactions

Monitoring

  • Immediate monitoring for 15 minutes post-vaccination (anaphylaxis, syncope risk)
  • Local and systemic reactions (counsel patient on expected side effects and when to seek care)
  • If severe local reaction occurs, note in immunization record and defer future boosters appropriately
  • Wound healing and signs of tetanus (if administered for wound prophylaxis; tetanus symptoms include muscle rigidity, jaw spasm, difficulty swallowing)

At-sea considerations

  • Wound management priority: early wound cleansing and debridement reduce tetanus risk; vaccine is adjunct to proper wound care
  • Immunization history critical: attempt to confirm prior doses with patient/medical records; if unknown, err on side of administration (especially for high-risk wounds)
  • Tetanus immunoglobulin (TIG): if indicated for high-risk wounds and patient unvaccinated/unknown history, consult medical direction; TIG may not be stocked onboard
  • Storage: vaccine requires refrigeration (2–8°C); do not freeze; ensure cold chain maintained in maritime environment
  • Document clearly: record lot number, expiry date, dose, and indication for medico-legal and continuity of care purposes
  • Evacuation planning: if severe wound with inadequate immunization history and no TIG available, consider evacuation for comprehensive management
  • Functional impact: warn patient of potential arm soreness; may affect work duties for 24–48 hours
