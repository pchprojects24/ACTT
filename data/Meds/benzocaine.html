Benzocaine

Stocked on board

  • Benzocaine 10% Gel 5.3gm

Adult dosing

  • Oral mucosal pain (minor mouth/gum irritation, toothache, denture discomfort)
    • Apply small amount of 10% gel to affected area up to 4 times daily as needed
    • Use sparingly; do not exceed recommended frequency
  • Temporary relief only
    • Not a substitute for dental/medical evaluation of underlying problem

Indications

  • Temporary relief of pain associated with minor oral irritation (toothache, canker sores, minor gum injury, denture irritation)
  • Topical anesthetic for oral mucosa before minor procedures (when appropriate)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to benzocaine or other ester-type local anesthetics (PABA derivatives)
  • Methemoglobinemia risk: rare but serious; higher risk with excessive use, spray formulations, infants/children, G6PD deficiency, or methemoglobin reductase deficiency
  • Avoid in infants under 2 years (teething gels): FDA warning due to methemoglobinemia risk
  • Do not use on large areas or broken/inflamed tissue
  • Avoid prolonged or frequent use

Adverse effects

  • Local: burning, stinging, contact dermatitis/allergic reaction
  • Methemoglobinemia (rare but serious): cyanosis, dyspnea, altered mental status, tachycardia
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, angioedema)

Preparation & administration

  • Apply small amount to affected area with clean finger or cotton swab
  • Do not swallow immediately; allow to remain in contact with affected area
  • Avoid eating/drinking for at least 1 hour after application if possible to maximize contact time
  • Wash hands before and after application
  • Do not use more than 4 times daily

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief and duration of need
  • Signs of methemoglobinemia (cyanosis, dyspnea, altered consciousness) if used repeatedly or excessively
  • Local allergic reactions (rash, swelling)
  • If pain persists >7 days or worsens: reassess for underlying pathology requiring definitive care

At-sea considerations

  • Provides temporary symptomatic relief only; identify and address underlying cause (dental abscess, trauma, etc.)
  • If dental pain is severe or associated with fever/swelling: consider antibiotics and evacuation planning
  • Document use and counsel on limitation: this is not treatment, only symptom control
  • If methemoglobinemia suspected (rare): stop use immediately and arrange urgent medical evaluation/evacuation
