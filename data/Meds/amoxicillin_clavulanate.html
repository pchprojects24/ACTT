Amoxicillin-Clavulanate

Stocked on board

  • Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 500mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Note: Dosing expressed as amoxicillin component. Common onboard formulation: 500 mg amoxicillin / 125 mg clavulanate tablets.

  • Acute bacterial sinusitis (when antibiotics indicated and broader coverage needed)
    • 500 mg (amoxicillin component) PO every 8 hours OR 875 mg (if available) PO every 12 hours for 5–7 days
    • If using 500 mg tablets onboard: 500 mg PO every 8 hours
  • Community-acquired pneumonia (when broader coverage needed vs amoxicillin alone)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours OR 875 mg (if available) PO every 12 hours for 5–7 days
  • Skin and soft tissue infections (e.g., cellulitis, infected wounds; when beta-lactamase-producing organisms suspected)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours OR 875 mg (if available) PO every 12 hours for 5–10 days depending on severity
  • Animal/human bites (prophylaxis or early infection)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours OR 875 mg (if available) PO every 12 hours for 3–5 days (prophylaxis) or 5–7 days (established infection)
  • Dental infections (odontogenic infections)
    • 500 mg PO every 8 hours for 5–7 days
  • Renal dosing adjustments
    • Dose adjustment required in significant renal impairment; follow product monograph/medical direction

Indications

  • Bacterial infections caused by beta-lactamase-producing organisms resistant to amoxicillin alone
  • Clavulanate is a beta-lactamase inhibitor that extends amoxicillin spectrum to include many Staph aureus, H. influenzae, Moraxella, anaerobes, and other resistant organisms
  • Acute bacterial sinusitis, community-acquired pneumonia, skin/soft tissue infections, bite wounds, dental infections (when broader coverage vs amoxicillin alone is indicated)

Contraindications / cautions

  • History of immediate hypersensitivity to penicillins (and caution with cephalosporin cross-reactivity depending on reaction)
  • History of amoxicillin-clavulanate-associated cholestatic jaundice or hepatic dysfunction
  • Suspected/confirmed infectious mononucleosis (rash risk with aminopenicillins)
  • Dose adjustment required in significant renal impairment
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk: reassess if significant diarrhea develops during/after therapy
  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea) more common than with amoxicillin alone due to clavulanate component
  • Hepatotoxicity (rare but serious): monitor for signs of liver dysfunction; risk increases with treatment >14 days or repeated courses
  • Interactions: INR may increase with warfarin (monitor if applicable)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort) – more common than amoxicillin alone
  • Rash, urticaria
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (urticaria → anaphylaxis)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea (important but uncommon)
  • Hepatotoxicity (rare; cholestatic jaundice, hepatitis—usually reversible upon discontinuation)
  • Headache, dizziness (uncommon)

Preparation & administration

  • Take at the start of a meal to enhance absorption and reduce GI upset
  • Take at evenly spaced intervals (every 8 or 12 hours depending on formulation)
  • Counsel: finish the prescribed course unless told otherwise; do not share leftover antibiotics
  • If significant diarrhea develops, reassess for C. difficile; if mild GI upset, taking with food may improve tolerance
  • Do NOT substitute tablets with different amoxicillin:clavulanate ratios (different formulations are not interchangeable mg-for-mg)

Monitoring

  • Clinical response within 48–72 hours (symptoms, fever, function)
  • Allergy/rash; stop and seek help if signs of anaphylaxis
  • GI tolerability and diarrhea severity (consider C. difficile if severe/persistent)
  • If prolonged course (>14 days) or high-risk patient: monitor liver function and renal function when feasible
  • If on warfarin: monitor INR more closely

At-sea considerations

  • Broader spectrum than amoxicillin alone; use when beta-lactamase-producing organisms suspected (e.g., MSSA, resistant H. influenzae) or for specific indications (bite wounds, dental infections)
  • Antibiotic stewardship: reserve for appropriate indications; use amoxicillin alone when sufficient to avoid unnecessary broad-spectrum exposure
  • GI upset more common—counsel patient and encourage taking with food; ensure adequate hydration
  • If diagnostics are limited, reassess early non-response and consider alternate diagnosis or need for escalation/evac
  • Document start date, dose, planned duration, allergy status, and indication clearly
