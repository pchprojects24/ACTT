Sodium Bicarbonate

Stocked on board

  • Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4% Injection 50mL

Adult dosing

  • Cardiac arrest (advanced life support - specific indications)
    • 1 mEq/kg IV (50 mL of 8.4% = 50 mEq) during resuscitation for hyperkalemia, severe metabolic acidosis, or tricyclic antidepressant overdose (per ACLS protocols)
    • May repeat 0.5 mEq/kg every 10 minutes if indicated; follow medical direction
  • Severe metabolic acidosis (non-arrest)
    • Dose depends on base deficit and clinical context; typical initial dose 50–100 mEq IV slow infusion
    • Requires careful monitoring; consult medical direction/guidelines
  • Tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) overdose with QRS widening or hypotension
    • 1–2 mEq/kg IV bolus (50–100 mEq); repeat as guided by ECG/clinical response
    • Target serum pH 7.45–7.55; requires close monitoring and medical direction

Adverse effects

  • Metabolic alkalosis (from overcorrection)
  • Hypernatremia, fluid overload (high sodium content)
  • Paradoxical intracellular acidosis (rapid CO2 generation)
  • Tissue necrosis if extravasation occurs (highly alkaline)
  • Hypocalcemia, hypokalemia (shift effects)

Indications

  • Cardiac arrest with specific indications (hyperkalemia, severe pre-existing metabolic acidosis, tricyclic antidepressant overdose)
  • Severe metabolic acidosis (pH <7.1–7.2) when underlying cause is being treated
  • Tricyclic antidepressant overdose with cardiac toxicity (QRS widening, arrhythmias, hypotension)
  • Selected poisonings/overdoses requiring urinary alkalinization (e.g., salicylate, methotrexate)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypocalcemia, hypokalemia (can worsen with bicarbonate administration)
  • Metabolic or respiratory alkalosis
  • Severe pulmonary edema or fluid overload (high sodium load)
  • Inadequate ventilation (cannot eliminate CO2 generated by bicarbonate)
  • Caution in hypernatremia or salt-retaining states (heart failure, renal impairment)
  • Do not mix with calcium-containing solutions (precipitation risk)

Preparation & administration

  • 8.4% solution = 1 mEq/mL (50 mL vial = 50 mEq)
  • IV push: Give slowly (max 1 mEq/kg over 1–2 minutes in arrest; slower in non-arrest)
  • IV infusion: Dilute in D5W or saline for slower administration when appropriate
  • Flush line before/after; ensure secure IV access (extravasation causes tissue damage)
  • Do not administer simultaneously with calcium or catecholamines in same IV line

Monitoring

  • Clinical response (hemodynamics, ECG in TCA overdose/hyperkalemia)
  • Arterial or venous blood gas (pH, pCO2, bicarbonate) when feasible
  • Serum electrolytes (sodium, potassium, calcium) if available
  • IV site for extravasation (stop immediately if occurs)
  • Ventilation adequacy (ensure patient can eliminate CO2)

At-sea considerations

  • High-risk medication: use only for specific indications per ACLS/toxicology protocols and medical direction
  • In cardiac arrest: focus on high-quality CPR and defibrillation; bicarbonate is adjunct for specific scenarios only
  • Limited lab capability: rely on clinical response (ECG changes, hemodynamics) and serial reassessment
  • Document dose, time, indication, and response clearly for medical handover
  • If TCA overdose suspected: consider early evacuation planning; bicarbonate is temporizing measure
