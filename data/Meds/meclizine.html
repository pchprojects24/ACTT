Meclizine

Stocked on board

  • Meclizine 25mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Motion sickness prevention
    • 25–50 mg PO 1 hour before travel, may repeat every 24 hours as needed
  • Vertigo associated with vestibular system disorders
    • 25–100 mg PO daily in divided doses (e.g., 25 mg every 12–24 hours or 50 mg daily)
    • Titrate to lowest effective dose

Indications

  • Prevention and treatment of nausea, vomiting, and dizziness associated with motion sickness
  • Management of vertigo associated with diseases affecting the vestibular system (e.g., labyrinthitis, Meniere's disease)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to meclizine
  • Sedation and drowsiness common: caution with watchkeeping, machinery operation, and alcohol/CNS depressants
  • Anticholinergic effects: caution in narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention, prostatic hypertrophy
  • Older adults: increased risk of anticholinergic effects (confusion, falls, dry mouth, constipation)
  • Avoid in pregnancy unless clearly needed (Category B; some sources recommend avoiding first trimester)

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness, sedation, fatigue
  • Dry mouth, blurred vision
  • Urinary retention (especially in susceptible patients)
  • Headache, dizziness (paradoxical)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • For motion sickness: take 1 hour before travel for best effect
  • Avoid alcohol and other CNS depressants
  • Chewable tablets may be chewed or swallowed whole

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief (nausea, vertigo)
  • Sedation/functional impairment (especially with first dose and during critical duties)
  • Anticholinergic side effects (dry mouth, urinary retention, confusion in older adults)
  • If vertigo persists or worsens: reassess for alternate diagnosis or need for medical evaluation

At-sea considerations

  • Operational safety: warn about drowsiness; first dose should be given when not on critical watch duties
  • Motion sickness: timing is important—dose 1 hour before departure when possible
  • If symptoms don't improve within 24–48 hours, consider alternate diagnosis (e.g., central vertigo, posterior circulation stroke in older/high-risk patients)
  • Document response to guide future selection; some patients respond better to alternate agents (dimenhydrinate, scopolamine)
