Tranexamic Acid

Stocked on board

  • Tranexamic Acid 100mg/mL Injection 10mL

Adult dosing

  • Trauma-associated hemorrhage (within 3 hours of injury)
    • Loading dose: 1 g IV over 10 minutes
    • Followed by: 1 g IV infusion over 8 hours
    • Must be given within 3 hours of injury; benefit decreases after 3 hours and may be harmful if >3 hours post-injury
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding (acute management)
    • 1 g IV or 1.3 g PO TID for up to 5 days during menstruation (if oral formulation available)
    • Onboard IV formulation can be used if IV access in place; otherwise consider evacuation for severe cases
  • Other acute bleeding (off-label; per medical direction)
    • 1 g IV over 10 minutes; may repeat based on clinical response and medical guidance

Indications

  • Trauma-associated hemorrhage with significant bleeding or risk of significant bleeding (early treatment critical)
  • Life-threatening hemorrhage when evacuation is delayed or unavailable (case-by-case medical direction)
  • Heavy menstrual bleeding (when clinically significant and other measures insufficient)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Active thromboembolic disease (DVT, PE, acute MI, stroke) or high thrombotic risk
  • History of seizures (tranexamic acid can lower seizure threshold, especially at high doses)
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage (increased risk of cerebral ischemia/infarction)
  • Do NOT use >3 hours post-trauma—may increase mortality; timing is critical
  • Renal impairment: dose reduction required (CrCl <30 mL/min: reduce dose or extend dosing interval)
  • Use caution in patients with history of VTE, atrial fibrillation, or cardiovascular disease

Adverse effects

  • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
  • Dizziness, headache
  • Hypotension (if infused too rapidly)
  • Thromboembolic events (DVT, PE, stroke, MI—uncommon but serious)
  • Seizures (dose-related; rare at recommended doses)
  • Visual disturbances (rare)

Preparation & administration

  • IV injection/infusion: 100 mg/mL concentration (10 mL vial = 1 g total)
  • Loading dose: dilute 1 g (10 mL) in 100 mL normal saline or D5W; infuse over 10 minutes
  • Maintenance infusion: dilute 1 g (10 mL) in 250–500 mL normal saline or D5W; infuse over 8 hours
  • Do NOT administer as rapid IV bolus—risk of hypotension
  • Compatible with most IV solutions; can be given via same line as crystalloids but avoid mixing with blood products in same syringe/bag
  • Timing is critical in trauma: start as soon as possible within 3 hours of injury

Monitoring

  • Hemodynamic status: BP, HR, signs of ongoing bleeding
  • Clinical response: reduction in bleeding, stabilization of vital signs
  • Thromboembolic signs: chest pain, shortness of breath, leg swelling/pain, neurological changes
  • Seizure activity (especially in high-risk patients or if higher doses considered)
  • Renal function if prolonged use or pre-existing renal impairment
  • Volume status and need for concurrent resuscitation (crystalloids, blood products if available)

At-sea considerations

  • Trauma hemorrhage: administer within 3 hours of injury—document exact time of injury and time of administration
  • Do NOT delay evacuation to give tranexamic acid; give en route if within 3-hour window
  • Tranexamic acid is an adjunct to hemorrhage control—continue direct pressure, tourniquets, wound packing, and hemostatic dressings as indicated
  • Ensure IV access secured before starting infusion; prepare maintenance infusion to follow loading dose without delay
  • If patient has history of clotting disorders or recent VTE, discuss risk/benefit with medical direction before administration
  • Monitor closely for hypotension during infusion; slow rate if BP drops
  • Document clear indication, time of injury, time of administration, and clinical response
