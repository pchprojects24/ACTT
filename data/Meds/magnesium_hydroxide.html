Magnesium Hydroxide

Stocked on board

  • Magnesium Hydroxide 400mg/5mL Suspension 500mL

Adult dosing

  • Antacid (for symptomatic relief of heartburn, acid indigestion, upset stomach)
    • 5–15 mL (400–1200 mg) PO as needed, up to 4 times daily
    • Take between meals and at bedtime
  • Laxative (for relief of occasional constipation)
    • 30–60 mL (2400–4800 mg) PO once daily at bedtime OR in divided doses
    • Onset typically 30 minutes to 6 hours

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of heartburn, acid indigestion, sour stomach (antacid use)
  • Relief of occasional constipation (laxative use; osmotic effect)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Severe renal impairment (risk of hypermagnesemia; magnesium accumulation)
  • Intestinal obstruction, appendicitis, or acute surgical abdomen (when used as laxative)
  • Myasthenia gravis (magnesium may worsen muscle weakness)
  • Avoid prolonged use as antacid without medical evaluation (may mask serious GI pathology)
  • Drug interactions: can reduce absorption of many medications (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, bisphosphonates, levothyroxine, iron supplements); separate dosing by 2 hours when possible
  • Caution in elderly or debilitated patients (laxative use—risk of fluid/electrolyte imbalance)

Adverse effects

  • Diarrhea (dose-dependent; common with laxative dosing)
  • Abdominal cramping, nausea
  • Hypermagnesemia (in renal impairment): can cause nausea, vomiting, hypotension, bradycardia, CNS depression, muscle weakness
  • Chalky taste (antacid use)

Preparation & administration

  • Shake suspension well before each use
  • Measure dose accurately using a dosing cup or oral syringe
  • May follow with water to improve palatability
  • Antacid use: Take between meals and at bedtime; avoid taking with other medications (separate by 2 hours if possible)
  • Laxative use: Take on an empty stomach with a full glass of water for best effect; onset typically 30 minutes to 6 hours
  • Encourage adequate fluid intake, especially when using as laxative

Monitoring

  • Antacid use: Symptom relief; if symptoms persist >2 weeks or worsen, reassess and consider alternate diagnosis (GERD, peptic ulcer disease, other pathology)
  • Laxative use: Bowel movement frequency and consistency; discontinue once constipation resolves
  • Monitor for diarrhea and dehydration (especially with higher laxative doses)
  • In renal impairment: monitor for signs of hypermagnesemia (muscle weakness, hypotension, bradycardia, CNS depression)
  • Drug interactions: verify timing of other medications

At-sea considerations

  • Useful dual-purpose agent: antacid for dyspepsia and laxative for constipation
  • Antacid use: reassess if symptoms persist >2 weeks; consider H2-blocker or PPI if available and GERD suspected
  • Laxative use: counsel on delayed onset (30 min–6 hours); avoid multiple laxatives simultaneously
  • Ensure adequate hydration, especially in warm environments or with physical work
  • Document medication list to identify and manage potential drug interactions (separate dosing by 2 hours when feasible)
  • Store suspension at room temperature; shake well; verify expiry as settling/separation may affect dosing accuracy over time
