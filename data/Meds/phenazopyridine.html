Phenazopyridine

Stocked on board

  • Phenazopyridine 200mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Symptomatic relief of urinary tract discomfort (dysuria, urgency, frequency, burning)
    • 200 mg PO 3 times daily after meals
    • Maximum duration: 2 days (symptomatic relief only; does not treat infection)
    • Use as adjunct to antibiotics when treating UTI; discontinue when symptoms improve (typically within 48 hours)

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of pain, burning, urgency, frequency associated with lower urinary tract irritation
  • Adjunct to antibiotics in urinary tract infection (does NOT treat infection; provides only symptom relief)
  • Post-procedural urinary discomfort (catheterization, cystoscopy)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to phenazopyridine
  • Renal insufficiency (CrCl <50 mL/min or significant renal disease): contraindicated due to accumulation risk
  • Hepatic disease: use with caution
  • Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency: risk of hemolytic anemia
  • Pregnancy: limited data; use only if clearly needed
  • Not for long-term use (treats symptoms only, not underlying condition)

Adverse effects

  • Headache, dizziness, GI upset
  • Orange-red discoloration of urine (expected; counsel patient to avoid alarm)
  • Staining of contact lenses possible
  • Rare but serious: methemoglobinemia (cyanosis, shortness of breath), hemolytic anemia (especially in G6PD deficiency), hepatotoxicity, renal toxicity
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, pruritus)

Preparation & administration

  • Take with or after food to minimize GI upset
  • Counsel patient: urine will turn orange or red (normal effect; will stain clothing/underwear)
  • Emphasize this is symptomatic relief only; must take full antibiotic course if UTI is being treated
  • Stop after 2 days or when symptoms resolve (whichever comes first)
  • If symptoms persist beyond 2 days, reassess for inadequate antibiotic therapy or alternate diagnosis

  • If no improvement or worsening symptoms (fever, flank pain, hematuria): reassess for pyelonephritis or complicated infection
  • Watch for signs of methemoglobinemia (cyanosis, dyspnea, gray-brown skin discoloration) or hemolysis (pale, jaundice, dark urine beyond expected orange-red color)
  • Discontinue immediately if hypersensitivity reaction or serious adverse effect

At-sea considerations

  • Ensure UTI diagnosis confirmed (or strongly suspected) and appropriate antibiotics initiated before using phenazopyridine
  • This medication does NOT treat infection; it only masks symptoms temporarily
  • Document baseline renal function if known; avoid in patients with known renal disease
  • If limited diagnostic capability: use with caution and reassess early if no clinical improvement
  • Counsel patient about urine discoloration to prevent unnecessary alarm or misinterpretation as hematuria
