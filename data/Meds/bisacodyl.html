Bisacodyl

Stocked on board

  • Bisacodyl 10mg Suppositories
  • Bisacodyl 5mg Enteric-Coated Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Constipation (oral)
    • 5–10 mg PO once daily (usually at bedtime)
    • Effect typically occurs in 6–12 hours
    • Maximum: 30 mg/day (rarely needed)
  • Constipation (rectal suppository)
    • 10 mg rectally as a single dose
    • Effect typically occurs in 15 minutes to 1 hour
  • Bowel preparation (when indicated)
    • Follow specific protocol; typically 10–15 mg PO in combination with other prep agents

Indications

  • Short-term relief of occasional constipation
  • Bowel evacuation before procedures or examinations (when applicable)
  • Opioid-induced constipation (as part of multi-modal approach)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Acute abdomen, appendicitis, bowel obstruction, or undiagnosed abdominal pain
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (active ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease)
  • Severe dehydration or electrolyte disturbances
  • Hypersensitivity to bisacodyl
  • Avoid chronic or excessive use: risk of laxative dependence, electrolyte imbalance, and colonic atony
  • Caution with concurrent medications that lower potassium (diuretics, corticosteroids)
  • Enteric-coated tablets: do not crush, chew, or take within 1 hour of antacids or milk (premature dissolution may cause gastric irritation)

Adverse effects

  • Abdominal cramping, discomfort
  • Diarrhea (with excessive use)
  • Nausea
  • Rectal irritation (suppository)
  • Electrolyte imbalance (hypokalemia, hypocalcemia) with chronic overuse
  • Laxative dependency with prolonged use

Preparation & administration

  • Oral tablets (enteric-coated):
    • Swallow whole with water; do not crush or chew
    • Take on empty stomach if possible for more predictable effect
    • Avoid antacids, milk, or dairy products within 1 hour of dosing
    • Usually taken at bedtime for morning bowel movement
  • Rectal suppository:
    • Remove wrapper and insert suppository into rectum, pointed end first
    • Retain in rectum for 15–30 minutes if possible
    • Moisten with water if needed for easier insertion
    • Wash hands before and after insertion

Monitoring

  • Bowel movement frequency and response
  • Abdominal pain or cramping (severe pain warrants reassessment for underlying pathology)
  • Signs of dehydration or electrolyte imbalance with frequent/prolonged use
  • If no response after appropriate use: reassess for bowel obstruction or other causes
  • Discourage chronic use; address underlying causes of constipation (hydration, diet, activity, medications)

At-sea considerations

  • Constipation is common in maritime settings (dietary changes, dehydration, limited mobility, privacy concerns)
  • First-line approach: optimize hydration, dietary fiber, and physical activity when feasible
  • Bisacodyl provides reliable relief but can cause cramping; counsel on expected effects
  • Suppository form offers faster onset when oral route less desirable or ineffective
  • If abdominal pain is severe or new: rule out acute abdomen/obstruction before giving laxatives
  • Avoid habitual use; encourage lifestyle/dietary modifications as primary strategy
  • Document use and educate on bowel health to prevent dependence
