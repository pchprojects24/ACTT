Lactulose

Stocked on board

  • Lactulose 667mg/mL Solution 500mL

Adult dosing

  • Constipation (typical dosing)
    • Initial: 15–30 mL (10–20 g) PO once daily
    • Maintenance: Adjust dose to produce 2–3 soft stools per day; usual range 15–45 mL daily
    • Onset of action typically 24–48 hours
  • Hepatic encephalopathy (specialist-directed)
    • 30–45 mL PO every 6–8 hours; titrate to achieve 2–3 soft stools daily

Indications

  • Treatment of constipation (osmotic laxative)
  • Prevention of constipation (e.g., in opioid therapy, prolonged immobility)
  • Hepatic encephalopathy (reduces ammonia absorption; specialist use)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Galactosemia (contains lactose and galactose)
  • Intestinal obstruction, suspected appendicitis, or acute surgical abdomen
  • Caution in diabetes mellitus (contains sugars; monitor if prolonged use)
  • Electrolyte disturbances: prolonged use or excessive dosing can cause diarrhea with fluid/electrolyte loss
  • Elderly or debilitated patients at higher risk for dehydration and electrolyte imbalance

Adverse effects

  • Abdominal cramping, bloating, flatulence (common, especially early in therapy)
  • Diarrhea (dose-related; reduce dose if excessive)
  • Nausea, vomiting (uncommon)
  • Electrolyte disturbances (hypokalemia, hypernatremia) with excessive use or diarrhea

Preparation & administration

  • Measure dose accurately using a dosing cup or oral syringe
  • May dilute with water, juice, or milk to improve palatability if needed
  • Encourage adequate fluid intake to support osmotic laxative effect and prevent dehydration
  • Onset typically 24–48 hours; counsel patient not to expect immediate bowel movement

Monitoring

  • Frequency and consistency of bowel movements (goal: soft, formed stools)
  • Abdominal symptoms (cramping, bloating, distension)
  • Hydration status and electrolytes if prolonged use or excessive diarrhea
  • In hepatic encephalopathy: mental status, stool frequency (target 2–3 soft stools/day)

At-sea considerations

  • Useful for constipation prevention in limited-activity settings or when opioid analgesics required
  • If acute abdominal pain develops, rule out obstruction or other surgical pathology before continuing
  • Ensure adequate hydration, especially in warm environments or with physical work
  • Counsel on delayed onset; reassure patient and avoid stacking multiple laxatives
  • Store in cool location; verify expiry as efficacy may decrease over time
