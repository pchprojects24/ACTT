Ciprofloxacin

Stocked on board

  • Ciprofloxacin 500mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Urinary tract infections (uncomplicated cystitis)
    • 250 mg PO every 12 hours for 3 days (women; uncomplicated)
    • Or 500 mg PO every 12 hours for 7 days if complicated or male patient
  • Pyelonephritis (acute; when oral appropriate)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours for 7 days (or longer based on severity/response; follow local guidelines)
  • Infectious diarrhea (traveler's diarrhea / bacterial gastroenteritis when indicated)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours for 1–3 days (duration depends on pathogen/severity)
    • Note: increasing fluoroquinolone resistance in many regions; follow local guidelines
  • Skin/soft tissue infections (when fluoroquinolone appropriate and organism susceptible)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours for 7–14 days depending on severity
  • Respiratory tract infections (selected cases; when appropriate pathogen coverage)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours; duration varies by indication (typically 7–14 days)
    • Note: not first-line for most CAP; reserve for specific indications/resistance patterns
  • Bone/joint infections (when oral fluoroquinolone appropriate)
    • 500 mg PO every 12 hours; prolonged course often needed (4–6 weeks or longer; follow specialist guidance)

Indications

  • Urinary tract infections (cystitis, pyelonephritis; when susceptible organisms and appropriate for resistance patterns)
  • Infectious diarrhea (traveler's diarrhea, bacterial gastroenteritis when antibiotics indicated)
  • Skin and soft tissue infections (when appropriate organism and resistance profile)
  • Bone and joint infections (often combined with other agents; specialist guidance)
  • Selected respiratory tract infections (not first-line for CAP in most settings)
  • Intra-abdominal infections (often combined with metronidazole for anaerobic coverage)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to ciprofloxacin or other fluoroquinolones
  • Black box warnings (FDA/Health Canada):
    • Tendinitis and tendon rupture risk (especially Achilles tendon; increased risk in >60 years, corticosteroid use, kidney/heart/lung transplant recipients)
    • Peripheral neuropathy (can be irreversible)
    • CNS effects (seizures, increased intracranial pressure, psychosis; caution in CNS disorders/epilepsy)
    • Myasthenia gravis exacerbation (avoid use if possible)
    • Serious adverse reactions: may outweigh benefits in uncomplicated infections (sinusitis, bronchitis, UTI) where other options exist
  • QT prolongation risk: caution with other QT-prolonging drugs, electrolyte abnormalities, or cardiac disease
  • Pregnancy/lactation: avoid unless no alternative; potential for arthropathy in developing cartilage
  • Renal impairment: dose adjustment required when CrCl <30 mL/min
  • Photosensitivity: advise sun protection during therapy
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea, abdominal pain)
  • CNS effects (headache, dizziness, insomnia; rarely seizures, confusion, psychosis)
  • Tendinitis/tendon rupture (especially Achilles)
  • Peripheral neuropathy (potentially irreversible)
  • QT prolongation, arrhythmias (rare)
  • Photosensitivity reactions
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash; rarely severe)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea
  • Elevated liver enzymes

Preparation & administration

  • May take with or without food; taking with food may reduce GI upset
  • Maintain adequate hydration during therapy
  • Avoid dairy products, calcium, iron, zinc, antacids, and multivitamins within 2 hours before or 6 hours after dose (significantly reduce absorption)
  • Counsel: finish prescribed course; report tendon pain, weakness, numbness/tingling, or CNS symptoms immediately
  • Advise sun protection (photosensitivity risk)

Monitoring

  • Clinical response to infection (symptoms, fever)
  • Tendon pain or inflammation (Achilles, shoulder, hand); STOP if tendinitis suspected
  • Neurologic symptoms (peripheral neuropathy, CNS effects); STOP if severe or concerning
  • QT interval if high-risk patient or concurrent QT-prolonging drugs (when ECG available)
  • Renal function if prolonged course or renal impairment
  • Diarrhea (C. difficile consideration)

At-sea considerations

  • Reserve for appropriate indications where benefits outweigh significant risks; avoid as first-line for simple infections when alternatives exist
  • Key maritime use: UTI/pyelonephritis, infectious diarrhea (traveler's diarrhea), selected resistant infections when other options not available
  • Black box warnings are significant: counsel patients clearly about tendon rupture, neuropathy, and CNS risks
  • If patient develops tendon pain or neurologic symptoms, STOP drug and reassess (do not continue)
  • Document indication clearly; ensure appropriate stewardship (fluoroquinolone resistance is increasing globally)
  • Avoid in older adults when safer alternatives exist (higher risk of serious adverse effects)
