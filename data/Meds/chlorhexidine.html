Chlorhexidine

Stocked on board

  • Chlorhexidine 4% Solution 1 Litre

Adult dosing

Skin antisepsis (preoperative/procedural skin preparation)

  • Apply 4% chlorhexidine solution to clean, dry skin
  • Scrub thoroughly for at least 2 minutes
  • Rinse and dry before applying sterile drape or dressing

Surgical hand scrub

  • Wet hands and forearms; apply ~5 mL of 4% chlorhexidine
  • Scrub hands and forearms for 3 minutes
  • Rinse thoroughly with water and dry with sterile towel

Wound cleansing (when appropriate)

  • Dilute 4% solution (typically 1:20 to 1:40 dilution) for wound irrigation or cleansing
  • Do NOT use undiluted on open wounds or mucous membranes

Indications

  • Preoperative/preprocedural skin antisepsis (central line placement, minor surgical procedures)
  • Surgical hand scrub for sterile procedures
  • General skin antisepsis and cleansing
  • Wound site preparation (external skin around wound; use appropriately diluted for wound contact)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to chlorhexidine (rare but can include severe anaphylaxis)
  • Do NOT use in eyes, ears (ototoxicity risk if enters middle ear), or meninges
  • Do NOT use undiluted 4% solution directly on open wounds or mucous membranes (tissue irritation/damage)
  • Avoid use on neonates (especially premature infants; chemical burn risk)
  • Caution with use on broken/damaged skin (absorption risk, irritation)
  • Do NOT use for preoperative whole-body bathing unless specifically formulated product for that use

Adverse effects

  • Skin irritation, rash, dryness
  • Allergic contact dermatitis (uncommon)
  • Anaphylaxis (rare but documented; more common with repeated mucous membrane exposure)
  • Ototoxicity if enters middle ear
  • Eye irritation/keratitis if eye contact occurs

Preparation & administration

  • For skin prep: use undiluted 4% solution on intact skin; allow to air dry for best effect (typically 30 seconds to 2 minutes)
  • For wound irrigation: dilute appropriately (follow local protocol; typically 0.05%â€“0.2% solutions used for wound contact)
  • Do NOT apply to mucous membranes, eyes, or ears
  • If eye contact occurs: irrigate immediately with copious water and assess for injury
  • Flammable when in alcohol-based formulations; allow to dry completely before cautery/electrical equipment use

Monitoring

  • Skin reaction (redness, irritation, rash)
  • Allergic reaction (urticaria, angioedema, anaphylaxis; rare but important)
  • If used on large surface areas or repeatedly: monitor for systemic absorption effects in high-risk patients

At-sea considerations

  • Excellent broad-spectrum antiseptic for procedural skin prep (central lines, suturing, minor procedures)
  • Stock on board as concentrate: ensure crew knows to dilute appropriately for wound contact (never use 4% directly on open wounds)
  • If performing procedures with limited resources: proper skin prep with chlorhexidine reduces infection risk significantly
  • Document any skin reactions; previous chlorhexidine exposure/sensitization may increase allergy risk on repeat use
