Respiratory / Allergy

Beclomethasone

Stocked on board

  • Beclomethasone 50mcg Nasal Inhaler
  • Beclomethasone 50mcg Oral Inhaler (QVAR)

Adult dosing

Beclomethasone 50mcg Nasal Inhaler (for allergic rhinitis)

  • Allergic rhinitis (seasonal or perennial)
    • 1–2 sprays (50–100 mcg) per nostril twice daily
    • Maximum: 400 mcg/day (8 sprays total per day)
    • Once symptoms controlled, may reduce to lowest effective dose (often 1 spray per nostril once or twice daily)

Beclomethasone 50mcg Oral Inhaler (QVAR; for asthma)

  • Asthma (maintenance therapy; not for acute relief)
    • Initial: 1–2 puffs (50–100 mcg) twice daily
    • Moderate asthma: may increase to 2–4 puffs (100–200 mcg) twice daily
    • Maximum: typically up to 400 mcg/day (follow prescriber guidance and product monograph for specific formulation)
    • Titrate to lowest effective dose once asthma is controlled

Indications

  • Nasal inhaler: Symptomatic relief of seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis
  • Oral inhaler (QVAR): Maintenance treatment of asthma as prophylactic therapy; not for acute bronchospasm or status asthmaticus

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to beclomethasone or formulation components
  • Nasal: Untreated localized infection involving nasal mucosa; recent nasal surgery or trauma (delayed healing risk)
  • Oral inhaler: Primary treatment of status asthmaticus or acute asthma episodes requiring intensive measures; not a rescue inhaler
  • Active or quiescent tuberculosis (respiratory tract); untreated fungal, bacterial, or viral respiratory infections
  • Systemic corticosteroid effects: prolonged high-dose use may cause adrenal suppression, growth suppression (children), osteoporosis, glaucoma, cataracts
  • Oral candidiasis risk with oral inhaler (rinse mouth after use)
  • Immunosuppression: caution with exposure to infections (e.g., chickenpox, measles) especially in non-immune individuals

Adverse effects

  • Nasal inhaler: Nasal irritation, epistaxis (nosebleeds), sneezing, nasal dryness, unpleasant taste/smell, headache
  • Oral inhaler: Oral candidiasis (thrush), hoarseness, throat irritation, cough, headache
  • Rarely: hypersensitivity reactions (rash, angioedema, bronchospasm)
  • With prolonged high-dose use: systemic corticosteroid effects (adrenal suppression, osteoporosis, growth effects, hyperglycemia)

Preparation & administration

Nasal inhaler:

  • Shake well before each use
  • Prime pump if new or not used for several days (follow product instructions)
  • Clear nasal passages (blow nose gently) before use
  • Insert nozzle into nostril, aim slightly away from nasal septum, and spray while inhaling gently through nose
  • Rinse nozzle tip with water after use and dry; do not share between individuals

Oral inhaler (QVAR):

  • Shake well before each use
  • Prime inhaler if new or not used for several days (follow product instructions)
  • Exhale fully, then place mouthpiece in mouth and close lips around it
  • Press canister and inhale slowly and deeply; hold breath for ~10 seconds
  • Rinse mouth with water and spit out after use to reduce oral candidiasis risk
  • Clean mouthpiece regularly as per product instructions
  • Counsel: This is a preventive medication, not a rescue inhaler; continue even when feeling well

Monitoring

  • Nasal: Symptom control (rhinorrhea, sneezing, nasal congestion); nasal mucosa for irritation or epistaxis
  • Oral inhaler: Asthma control (symptom frequency, rescue inhaler use, peak flow if available); monitor for oral candidiasis (inspect mouth/throat periodically)
  • Ensure proper inhaler technique (review periodically to ensure effectiveness)
  • Monitor for systemic effects with prolonged high-dose use (growth in children, bone health, glucose control, adrenal function when clinically indicated)
  • If no improvement within 2–4 weeks, reassess diagnosis, compliance, technique, and consider dose adjustment or alternate therapy

At-sea considerations

  • Ensure crew understands difference between maintenance inhaler (beclomethasone) and rescue inhaler (e.g., salbutamol); do not substitute
  • Review proper inhaler technique; improper use is common and reduces effectiveness
  • For asthma: ensure rescue inhaler is also available; if increasing rescue use or worsening symptoms, escalate care
  • Document asthma action plan if available; consider evacuation for severe or uncontrolled asthma
  • Storage: protect from extreme heat; inhalers may malfunction or lose potency if exposed to high temperatures
  • If crew develops oral thrush, treat appropriately and reinforce mouth rinsing after inhaler use
