Sodium Chloride

Stocked on board

  • Sodium Chloride 0.9% Injection 10mL
  • Sodium Chloride 0.9% Irrigation 1 Litre

Adult dosing

0.9% Injection (10 mL vial) - IV flush / medication dilution

  • IV flush: 3–10 mL before and after IV medication administration to maintain line patency
  • Medication dilution: Volume as required per medication compatibility and dilution requirements

0.9% Irrigation (1 Litre) - Wound/external irrigation

  • Wound irrigation: Use sterile technique; irrigate with sufficient volume to remove debris and contaminants
  • Large wounds: May require 100–1000+ mL depending on wound size and contamination
  • Eye irrigation: Continuous irrigation for chemical exposure (15–30+ minutes); use irrigation fluid or normal saline

Indications

  • IV line flushing to maintain patency and prevent occlusion
  • Dilution of IV medications for administration
  • Wound irrigation and cleansing (sterile irrigation solution)
  • Eye irrigation for chemical exposures or foreign body removal
  • External irrigation of mucous membranes

Contraindications / cautions

  • Do not use irrigation solution for IV administration (non-sterile for injection)
  • Hypernatremia, fluid overload (if using large volumes systemically)
  • Ensure correct product: injection vials for IV use, irrigation bottles for external use only
  • Do not irrigate closed body cavities with large volumes without appropriate drainage

Adverse effects

  • IV flush: Minimal; phlebitis if technique poor or line issues
  • Irrigation: Generally well-tolerated; tissue irritation if prolonged contact
  • Large volume IV use (not typical with these products): hypernatremia, fluid overload, hypokalemia

Preparation & administration

IV flush (0.9% injection):

  • Use aseptic technique; swab injection port before accessing
  • Flush with 3–10 mL using push-pause technique to create turbulence
  • Follow institutional protocol for flushing frequency and volume

Wound irrigation (0.9% irrigation):

  • Use sterile or clean technique as appropriate for wound type
  • Irrigate with gentle pressure (30–60 mL syringe with 18–19 gauge catheter or splash guard)
  • Allow irrigation to run off wound; do not force fluid into deep tissue pockets

Eye irrigation:

  • Position patient to allow fluid to run from medial to lateral aspect
  • Irrigate for at least 15–30 minutes for chemical exposures; continue until pH normalizes if testing available
  • May require eyelid retraction for adequate irrigation

Monitoring

  • IV site: Patency, signs of phlebitis or infiltration
  • Wound irrigation: Adequacy of debris removal, tissue viability
  • Eye irrigation: Symptom relief, continued pain may indicate incomplete removal or injury

At-sea considerations

  • Essential supply: ensure adequate stock of both injection and irrigation products
  • Label clearly: prevent accidental use of non-sterile irrigation solution for IV administration
  • Wound irrigation: prioritize early irrigation of contaminated wounds to reduce infection risk
  • Eye injuries: copious irrigation is critical for chemical exposures; do not delay for diagnostics
  • Resource conservation: use appropriate volumes but do not compromise adequate irrigation in critical situations
