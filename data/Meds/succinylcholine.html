Neuromuscular Blocker / Paralytic

Succinylcholine

Stocked on board

  • Succinylcholine 20 mg/mL Injection 10 mL Vial (200 mg total)

Adult dosing

RSI paralytic (rapid sequence intubation)

  • 1–1.5 mg/kg IV push (typical: 70–100 mg for 70 kg patient)
  • Onset ~30–45 seconds (fastest-acting paralytic)
  • Duration ~5–10 minutes (short; allows rapid reassessment if intubation fails)

Indications

  • Neuromuscular blockade for RSI when rapid onset and short duration needed
  • Preferred paralytic for standard RSI in absence of contraindications

Contraindications / cautions

  • Absolute contraindications: Personal or family history of malignant hyperthermia, known hypersensitivity to succinylcholine, known hyperkalemia, acute phase of denervating injury or burn (see timing guidance below)
  • Hyperkalemia risk with timing-dependent contraindication:
    • Crush injury, severe burns, extensive denervating injury, spinal cord injury, prolonged immobilization, or neuromuscular disease cause upregulation of acetylcholine receptors
    • Risk window: Hyperkalemia risk typically emerges 24–72 hours post-injury and can persist for weeks to months
    • Safe window: Generally safe in first ~6 hours after acute injury before receptor upregulation occurs
    • Clinical guidance: If injury occurred >6 hours prior, use rocuronium preferentially; if >24 hours, succinylcholine is contraindicated
  • Relative contraindications: Penetrating eye injury (transient increase in intraocular pressure), severe burns/crush injury in first 24 hours (may use if benefit exceeds risk and within safe window), neuromuscular disease
  • Awareness risk: Patient is paralyzed but may be awake; MUST provide adequate sedation before paralysis

Preparation & administration

  • Calculate dose based on actual body weight (1–1.5 mg/kg)
  • Draw up and label syringe clearly ("PARALYTIC - SUCCINYLCHOLINE")
  • Administer rapid IV push
  • Refrigerate (2–8°C); stable at room temp for ~14 days; do not use if solution is not clear
  • Compatible with normal saline, D5W, lactated Ringer's
  • CRITICAL: Patient MUST be sedated before paralysis; have airway equipment and ventilation ready BEFORE administering

Monitoring

  • Continuous pulse oximetry, cardiac monitoring, ETCO2 (mandatory)
  • Airway equipment and bag-valve-mask ventilation ready before administration
  • Monitor for fasciculations (muscle twitching; expected; precedes paralysis)
  • Monitor for bradycardia (especially in children or with repeat dosing)
  • Watch for hyperkalemia signs: peaked T waves, widened QRS, dysrhythmias (rare but life-threatening)
  • Monitor for malignant hyperthermia: hyperthermia, rigidity, tachycardia, hypercarbia (rare but fatal; treat with dantrolene if available)

Adverse effects

  • Hyperkalemia (can be life-threatening; see contraindications for risk factors and timing)
  • Bradycardia (especially children, repeat dosing, or concurrent opioids; pretreat with atropine if high risk)
  • Fasciculations (expected; may cause muscle pain post-procedure)
  • Transient increase in intraocular pressure, intracranial pressure, intragastric pressure
  • Malignant hyperthermia (rare but life-threatening; genetic predisposition)
  • Prolonged paralysis in patients with pseudocholinesterase deficiency (genetic; rare)
  • Anaphylaxis (rare)

At-sea considerations

  • Succinylcholine is the fastest-acting paralytic (30–45 sec onset) with shortest duration (5–10 min); ideal for RSI in most cases
  • Timing-critical contraindication: For crush/burn/denervating injuries, assess time since injury:
    • <6 hours: Generally safe
    • 6–24 hours: Consider rocuronium preferentially
    • >24 hours: Succinylcholine is contraindicated; use rocuronium
  • Short duration allows rapid return of spontaneous breathing if intubation fails; however, commit to rescue airway plan before administration
  • Paralysis = apnea: Patient cannot breathe; ensure pre-oxygenation and bag-valve-mask ventilation ready
  • Label syringe "PARALYTIC" to prevent accidental administration
  • Refrigerate; check expiry and storage temp before use
  • If malignant hyperthermia suspected: stop triggering agents, cool patient, hyperventilate, treat hyperkalemia/acidosis, contact medical control urgently
  • Document dose, time, indication, contraindication screen (especially injury timing), and post-paralysis plan
