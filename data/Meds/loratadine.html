Respiratory / Allergy

Loratadine

Stocked on board

  • Loratadine 10mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Allergic rhinitis (seasonal/perennial)
    • 10 mg PO once daily
  • Urticaria (hives) / pruritus from allergic triggers
    • 10 mg PO once daily
  • Dose adjustments
    • Hepatic impairment or renal impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min): 10 mg every other day (or follow product monograph/local guidance)

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of allergic rhinitis (sneezing, rhinorrhea, itchy/watery eyes, nasal/palatal pruritus)
  • Symptomatic relief of chronic idiopathic urticaria and other allergic skin conditions

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to loratadine or any component
  • Generally considered non-sedating, but drowsiness can occur in some individuals; caution with watchkeeping, machinery, and alcohol/sedatives
  • Hepatic or severe renal impairment: dose adjustment required
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: use only if clearly needed; consult product monograph/medical direction

Adverse effects

  • Headache (most common)
  • Fatigue, drowsiness (less common than first-generation antihistamines; similar to cetirizine or less sedating)
  • Dry mouth
  • Nausea, GI upset (uncommon)
  • Rare: tachycardia, palpitations, dizziness

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • Non-sedating for most patients; can be taken in morning or evening based on preference and symptom timing
  • Avoid combining with other sedating medications/alcohol when possible
  • Onset of action typically within 1â€“3 hours; duration ~24 hours

Monitoring

  • Symptom control (rhinitis/urticaria)
  • Sedation/functional impairment (though less likely than with first-generation antihistamines or cetirizine)
  • Reassess need for ongoing therapy and consider step-down or discontinuation if symptoms resolve

At-sea considerations

  • Preferred antihistamine if minimal sedation is critical (e.g., watchkeepers, pilots, machinery operators)
  • Operational safety: generally well-tolerated, but warn about potential drowsiness in sensitive individuals; consider trial dose at a non-critical time
  • If symptoms are primarily nasal congestion, ensure non-sedating options and local pathways are also considered (e.g., nasal steroid use if stocked and appropriate)
  • Document response and any sedation to guide future selection (loratadine vs cetirizine, etc.)
