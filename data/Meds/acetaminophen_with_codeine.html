Acetaminophen with Codeine

Stocked on board

  • Acetaminophen with Codeine Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Moderate to moderately severe pain (short-term use)
    • Typical formulation: Acetaminophen 300 mg / Codeine 30 mg (Tylenol #3 equivalent)
    • 1–2 tablets PO every 4–6 hours as needed for pain
    • Maximum: 12 tablets/24 hours (to limit acetaminophen to 3600 mg/day and codeine exposure)
  • Lower strength formulation (if stocked)
    • Acetaminophen 300 mg / Codeine 15 mg: 1–2 tablets every 4–6 hours as needed
  • Important considerations
    • Use lowest effective dose for shortest duration
    • Limit total acetaminophen from all sources to <4000 mg/day (lower in liver disease, chronic alcohol use)
    • Codeine is a prodrug requiring CYP2D6 metabolism; ~10% of population are poor metabolizers (reduced effect) and ~1–2% ultra-rapid metabolizers (increased toxicity risk)

Indications

  • Moderate to moderately severe acute pain unresponsive to non-opioid analgesics alone
  • Short-term pain management when NSAIDs are contraindicated or inadequate
  • Dental pain, musculoskeletal pain, post-procedural pain (when appropriate)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Codeine hypersensitivity or severe respiratory depression/significant respiratory compromise
  • Acute or severe asthma in unmonitored setting
  • Known or suspected paralytic ileus or GI obstruction
  • Concurrent MAO inhibitor use (or within 14 days)
  • Children <12 years (codeine risk); avoid in adolescents post-tonsillectomy/adenoidectomy
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: codeine crosses placenta and enters breast milk; avoid or use with extreme caution (neonatal respiratory depression risk)
  • Severe hepatic impairment (acetaminophen toxicity risk); dose adjustment in renal impairment
  • Avoid alcohol and CNS depressants (additive sedation/respiratory depression)
  • CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolizers: increased risk of opioid toxicity
  • Substance use disorder history: use cautiously with close monitoring

Adverse effects

  • Drowsiness, sedation, dizziness, lightheadedness
  • Nausea, vomiting, constipation
  • Respiratory depression (dose-dependent; risk with overdose or susceptible patients)
  • Pruritus, sweating
  • Potential for tolerance, dependence, and misuse with prolonged use
  • Acetaminophen: hepatotoxicity with overdose or chronic excessive use

Preparation & administration

  • May take with food or milk to reduce GI upset
  • Do not exceed recommended dose or frequency
  • Avoid concurrent acetaminophen-containing products (cold/flu medications, other analgesics)
  • Counsel on constipation risk: encourage hydration, fiber, activity; consider stool softener if prolonged use
  • Warn about sedation and impaired performance: avoid watchkeeping, machinery, driving until response known
  • Counsel: do not share medication; store securely

Monitoring

  • Pain relief and functional improvement (reassess need frequently)
  • Sedation/CNS depression, respiratory rate (especially first dose and dose escalation)
  • Constipation and other GI effects
  • Signs of misuse, diversion, or dependence if available longer-term
  • Reassess indication daily; transition to non-opioid analgesia as soon as feasible
  • Total acetaminophen intake from all sources

At-sea considerations

  • Opioid stewardship: prescribe only when clearly indicated; use shortest effective course (typically ≤3 days for acute pain)
  • Operational impact: codeine causes sedation—ensure patient is not assigned to critical duties while taking; document fitness restrictions
  • Limited monitoring capability: be conservative in higher-risk patients (elderly, respiratory disease, renal/hepatic impairment)
  • Secure storage: treat as controlled substance; maintain accountability/documentation
  • If prolonged use becomes necessary, plan for taper and evac/consultation to avoid withdrawal
  • Reassess non-opioid alternatives and non-pharmacologic approaches regularly
