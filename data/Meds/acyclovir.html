Acyclovir

Stocked on board

  • Acyclovir 500mg Injection (50mg/mL)

Adult dosing

Note: Stocked formulation is IV injection. Oral acyclovir may be available ashore. Dosing below reflects IV use for severe infections requiring parenteral therapy.

  • Herpes simplex encephalitis (severe; emergency)
    • 10 mg/kg IV every 8 hours for 14–21 days
    • Infuse over at least 1 hour
    • Requires evacuation and hospital-level care; initiate only with medical direction in true emergency
  • Severe mucocutaneous HSV infection (immunocompromised host)
    • 5 mg/kg IV every 8 hours for 7–14 days (or until clinical resolution)
    • Infuse over at least 1 hour
  • Varicella-zoster virus (VZV) – severe or disseminated (immunocompromised)
    • 10 mg/kg IV every 8 hours for 7–10 days
    • Infuse over at least 1 hour
  • Renal dosing adjustments required
    • Dose adjustment necessary in renal impairment; follow product monograph/medical direction
    • Ensure adequate hydration to minimize nephrotoxicity

Indications

  • Severe herpes simplex virus (HSV) infections (encephalitis, disseminated disease, severe mucocutaneous infection in immunocompromised)
  • Varicella-zoster virus (VZV) infections in immunocompromised patients or severe/complicated cases
  • Onboard IV acyclovir is primarily for emergency initiation with evacuation planned; most HSV/VZV cases can be managed with oral antivirals (if available) or supportive care

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to acyclovir or valacyclovir
  • Renal impairment: nephrotoxicity risk; dose adjustment required; ensure adequate hydration
  • Dehydration increases nephrotoxicity risk significantly
  • Neurologic toxicity possible (tremor, confusion, hallucinations, seizures) especially with high doses or renal impairment
  • Phlebitis/inflammation at infusion site: ensure proper dilution and infusion rate
  • Pregnancy: category B; use when benefit outweighs risk
  • IV administration requires appropriate training and monitoring

Adverse effects

  • Nephrotoxicity (crystalluria, acute kidney injury) – especially with rapid infusion or dehydration
  • Phlebitis, inflammation at IV site
  • Neurologic effects: headache, dizziness, confusion, hallucinations, seizures (higher risk in renal impairment)
  • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
  • Transient elevation in liver enzymes

Preparation & administration

  • Reconstitution: Reconstitute 500 mg vial with 10 mL Sterile Water for Injection to yield 50 mg/mL
  • Dilution: Further dilute in compatible IV solution (e.g., Normal Saline or D5W) to concentration ≤7 mg/mL (typically 100 mL to 250 mL depending on dose)
  • Infusion rate: Administer over at least 1 hour (do NOT give as bolus or rapid infusion—nephrotoxicity risk)
  • Hydration: Ensure adequate hydration before and during therapy to reduce crystalluria risk
  • Use within appropriate timeframe after reconstitution/dilution (check product monograph for stability)
  • Monitor IV site for phlebitis; rotate sites as needed
  • Do NOT administer IM or subcutaneously

Monitoring

  • Renal function (creatinine, urine output) before and during therapy—critical in IV use
  • Hydration status and fluid balance
  • Neurologic status (mental status changes, seizures—especially if renal impairment)
  • IV site for phlebitis/extravasation
  • Clinical response to therapy (viral symptoms, fever)
  • Electrolytes if prolonged use or renal concerns

At-sea considerations

  • IV acyclovir is a high-acuity medication requiring intensive monitoring—use only in true emergencies with medical direction and evacuation planning
  • For most HSV/VZV infections onboard: consider oral acyclovir (if available locally) or supportive care; reserve IV for life-threatening/severe disease
  • Limited lab capability: if renal function monitoring not available, use with extreme caution and prioritize evacuation
  • Hydration paramount: ensure IV fluids concurrently to reduce nephrotoxicity risk
  • Training requirement: ensure qualified personnel for IV administration and monitoring
  • Communicate clearly with receiving facility: medication given, dose, time, and clinical indication
