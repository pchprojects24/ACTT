Gentamicin

Stocked on board

  • Gentamicin 0.3% Eye Drops 10mL

Adult dosing

  • Bacterial conjunctivitis
    • Mild to moderate: 1–2 drops in affected eye(s) every 4 hours while awake
    • Severe: 2 drops in affected eye(s) every hour initially; reduce frequency as infection improves (per clinical response)
    • Typical duration: 7–10 days (continue for 48 hours after clinical improvement)
  • Bacterial blepharitis / blepharoconjunctivitis (when topical antibiotic indicated)
    • 1–2 drops in affected eye(s) 3–4 times daily, typically 7–10 days
  • Corneal ulcer / keratitis (in consultation with ophthalmology when feasible; urgent/severe cases)
    • Higher frequency dosing required; follow specialist guidance or emergency protocols

Indications

  • Bacterial conjunctivitis (pink eye)
  • Bacterial blepharitis or blepharoconjunctivitis
  • Superficial ocular infections caused by susceptible bacteria
  • Prophylaxis of infection following ocular injury/foreign body removal (selected cases)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to gentamicin or other aminoglycosides
  • Viral conjunctivitis (e.g., adenoviral "pink eye") or fungal/parasitic eye infections (not effective; may delay appropriate treatment)
  • If corneal involvement is suspected (pain, photophobia, reduced vision, corneal opacity): urgent ophthalmology referral required when feasible
  • Contact lens use: remove lenses before instillation; do not wear contacts during treatment of infection
  • Prolonged use may lead to overgrowth of non-susceptible organisms (including fungi) or bacterial resistance

Adverse effects

  • Transient burning, stinging, or irritation on instillation (common)
  • Punctate keratopathy (superficial corneal irritation) with prolonged use
  • Allergic reactions (itching, redness, swelling—discontinue if occurs)
  • Rare: hypersensitivity reactions, secondary infection with resistant organisms or fungi
  • Systemic absorption minimal; systemic toxicity (nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity) not expected with topical ophthalmic use

Preparation & administration

  • Wash hands before administration
  • Shake bottle well before use (if suspension formulation)
  • Instillation technique:
    • Tilt head back, pull down lower eyelid to create a pocket
    • Instill prescribed number of drops into conjunctival sac (avoid touching dropper tip to eye, eyelid, or any surface)
    • Close eye gently for 1–2 minutes; may apply gentle pressure to lacrimal sac (inner corner of eye) to reduce systemic absorption and increase local retention
  • If using multiple eye drops: wait at least 5–10 minutes between different drops
  • Storage: store at room temperature or refrigerate per product instructions; discard 28 days after opening (follow product label)
  • Avoid sharing eye drops between patients to prevent cross-contamination

Monitoring

  • Clinical response: reduction in redness, discharge, discomfort (typically 48–72 hours)
  • If no improvement after 2–3 days or worsening symptoms: reassess diagnosis and consider alternate pathogen or need for escalation
  • Vision changes, pain, photophobia, or corneal involvement: urgent evaluation required
  • Signs of allergic reaction or intolerance: discontinue and consider alternative

At-sea considerations

  • Differentiate bacterial from viral conjunctivitis when possible (bacterial: purulent discharge; viral: watery discharge, often bilateral, associated URI). Many cases are viral and self-limited; avoid unnecessary antibiotics
  • If vision is affected, significant pain, or corneal involvement suspected: seek urgent medical guidance and consider evacuation
  • Counsel on infection control: frequent hand hygiene, avoid sharing towels/pillows, avoid touching/rubbing eyes
  • Limited shelf life after opening: date bottle when first opened and discard per product guidelines (typically 28 days)
  • Stock adequate supply but recognize waste if infrequently used (small bottle volumes)
