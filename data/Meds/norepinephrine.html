Vasopressor / Cardiac

Norepinephrine

Stocked on board

  • Norepinephrine 1 mg/mL Injection 4 mL Vial (4 mg total)

Adult dosing

Vasopressor support (septic shock, distributive shock)

  • Preparation: Dilute 4 mg in 250 mL D5W or NS (16 mcg/mL concentration)
  • Initial infusion: Start 0.05–0.1 mcg/kg/min (typical: 5–10 mcg/min for 70 kg patient)
  • Titration: Increase by 0.05 mcg/kg/min (or 2–5 mcg/min) every 5–10 minutes to target MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Typical range: 0.1–0.5 mcg/kg/min (10–40 mcg/min); higher doses may be needed in refractory shock

Post-cardiac arrest hypotension

  • Start 0.1 mcg/kg/min; titrate to MAP ≥65 mmHg and adequate perfusion
  • Combine with IV fluids and treat reversible causes

Indications

  • Septic shock or distributive shock requiring vasopressor support (first-line vasopressor)
  • Post-cardiac arrest hypotension (after ROSC)
  • Refractory hypotension despite adequate fluid resuscitation
  • Neurogenic shock, anaphylactic shock (adjunct to epinephrine), or other vasodilatory shock states

Contraindications / cautions

  • Relative contraindications: Hypovolemic shock without concurrent fluid resuscitation (may worsen tissue perfusion), mesenteric or peripheral vascular thrombosis (vasoconstriction may worsen ischemia), known hypersensitivity
  • Use with caution: Ischemic heart disease (increases myocardial oxygen demand), severe arrhythmias, extravasation risk (causes tissue necrosis; use central line if possible)
  • Drug interactions: MAO inhibitors, tricyclic antidepressants (exaggerated hypertensive response)

Preparation & administration

  • Standard concentration: Dilute 4 mg in 250 mL D5W or NS (16 mcg/mL)
  • Alternative concentration (fluid restriction): Dilute 4 mg in 100 mL (40 mcg/mL)
  • Administer via infusion pump through dedicated IV line (preferably central line)
  • Do NOT mix with alkaline solutions (precipitates)
  • Protect from light during infusion
  • Use glass or non-PVC containers if available (some adsorption to PVC)
  • Extravasation management: If infiltration occurs, stop infusion immediately; infiltrate area with phentolamine 5–10 mg diluted in 10 mL NS to reverse local vasoconstriction and prevent necrosis

Monitoring

  • Continuous cardiac monitoring and blood pressure (arterial line ideal but not required)
  • Target MAP ≥65 mmHg; reassess perfusion (mental status, urine output, lactate clearance, capillary refill)
  • Monitor for arrhythmias (tachycardia, VT/VF risk with excessive doses)
  • IV site integrity (check frequently for extravasation; infiltration causes tissue necrosis)
  • Urine output, lactate, and end-organ perfusion markers
  • Reassess volume status; ensure adequate fluid resuscitation before escalating dose

Adverse effects

  • Hypertension, reflex bradycardia
  • Arrhythmias (tachycardia, VT/VF with high doses)
  • Tissue ischemia (renal, mesenteric, peripheral; due to excessive vasoconstriction)
  • Extravasation injury and tissue necrosis (requires phentolamine infiltration)
  • Increased myocardial oxygen demand (risk of ischemia in CAD patients)
  • Anxiety, headache, tremor

At-sea considerations

  • Norepinephrine is the first-line vasopressor for septic shock and distributive shock
  • Requires continuous infusion via pump; ensure adequate supply and backup plan for pump failure
  • Central line preferred to reduce extravasation risk; if using peripheral IV, choose large-bore catheter in large vein and monitor site continuously
  • Titrate to MAP ≥65 mmHg and adequate perfusion (mental status, urine output, lactate clearance)
  • Fluid resuscitation first: Ensure patient is adequately volume-resuscitated; vasopressors do not replace fluids
  • Higher doses (>0.5 mcg/kg/min) may indicate refractory shock; consider adding vasopressin or treating underlying cause
  • Document infusion rate (mcg/min), MAP trend, urine output, and perfusion status at regular intervals
  • Wean slowly once shock resolves; abrupt cessation may cause rebound hypotension
  • Have phentolamine available for extravasation treatment
  • Label infusion clearly; dedicate IV line (do not bolus other meds through same line)
