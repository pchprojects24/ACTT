Aluminum Hydroxide with Magnesium Hydroxide and Simethicone Chewable

Stocked on board

  • Aluminum Hydroxide with Magnesium Hydroxide and Simethicone Chewable Tablets

Adult dosing

Heartburn, acid indigestion, upset stomach, gas/bloating (symptomatic relief)

  • Typical formulation (e.g., Diovol, Maalox): 2–4 chewable tablets as needed after meals and at bedtime
  • Maximum: Follow product-specific labeling (typically up to 12–16 tablets/day; do not exceed recommended daily dose)
  • For best effect, take 1–3 hours after meals and at bedtime

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of heartburn, sour stomach, acid indigestion
  • Relief of gas, bloating, and pressure associated with excess gas (simethicone component)
  • Aluminum and magnesium hydroxides neutralize stomach acid; simethicone reduces surface tension of gas bubbles

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to aluminum or magnesium antacids, simethicone, or any product component
  • Severe renal impairment: risk of aluminum and magnesium accumulation with chronic use (encephalopathy, hypermagnesemia)
  • Hypophosphatemia risk with prolonged aluminum antacid use
  • Drug interactions: antacids can reduce absorption of many medications (tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, iron, levothyroxine, bisphosphonates, digoxin, etc.)—separate administration by 2–4 hours
  • Bowel effects: aluminum tends to cause constipation; magnesium tends to cause diarrhea—combination balances this but individual response varies
  • Not for chronic daily use without medical evaluation; persistent symptoms may indicate serious GI disease

Adverse effects

  • Diarrhea (magnesium component) or constipation (aluminum component)
  • Chalky taste
  • Hypophosphatemia (with prolonged high-dose use)
  • Hypermagnesemia (in renal impairment with chronic use)
  • Aluminum accumulation toxicity (in renal impairment with chronic use)
  • Generally well tolerated for short-term, intermittent use

Preparation & administration

  • Chew tablets thoroughly before swallowing
  • May follow with water if desired
  • Take 1–3 hours after meals and at bedtime for optimal effect
  • Separate from other oral medications by at least 2 hours to avoid absorption interactions
  • For occasional use only; do not use for >2 weeks continuously without reassessment

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief (heartburn, gas, bloating)
  • Bowel pattern (diarrhea or constipation)
  • If symptoms persist or worsen despite treatment, reassess for alternative diagnosis (peptic ulcer disease, gastritis, biliary disease, cardiac chest pain)
  • If prolonged use or renal impairment: monitor for electrolyte disturbances (magnesium, phosphate), renal function
  • Frequency of use: frequent daily use suggests need for medical evaluation and possibly stronger acid suppression

At-sea considerations

  • Effective for quick symptomatic relief of heartburn and gas onboard
  • Encourage non-pharmacologic measures: eat smaller/more frequent meals, avoid late-night eating, elevate head of bed, avoid trigger foods (caffeine, alcohol, spicy/fatty foods)
  • If frequent use is required, consider whether stronger acid suppression (PPI if available) or medical evaluation is needed
  • Document use pattern and response
  • Warn about medication interactions—particularly important if patient is on antibiotics, thyroid medication, or other chronic medications
  • Be conservative in patients with known renal disease
