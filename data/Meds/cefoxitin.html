Antibiotics / Antivirals

Cefoxitin

Stocked on board

  • Cefoxitin 1gm Powder for Injection

Adult dosing

  • Moderate to severe infections (intra-abdominal, gynecologic, skin/soft tissue)
    • 1–2 g IV every 6–8 hours
    • Severe infections: 2 g IV every 6 hours or 3 g IV every 8 hours (up to 12 g/day)
  • Surgical prophylaxis
    • 2 g IV within 30–60 minutes before incision
    • Redose 2 g IV every 2–3 hours during prolonged procedures (shorter half-life than cefazolin)
    • Postoperative: may continue 2 g IV every 6 hours for 24 hours (per protocol)
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease (inpatient treatment)
    • 2 g IV every 6 hours (usually in combination with doxycycline)
  • Uncomplicated gonorrhea (when cephalosporin indicated; less common now)
    • 2 g IM single dose plus probenecid 1 g PO (historical; verify current guidelines)
  • Renal impairment dosing
    • Dose reduction required: follow product monograph based on creatinine clearance

Indications

  • Intra-abdominal infections (peritonitis, abscesses, appendicitis, diverticulitis)
  • Gynecologic infections (endometritis, pelvic inflammatory disease, pelvic cellulitis)
  • Skin and soft tissue infections
  • Bone and joint infections
  • Surgical prophylaxis (especially colorectal, gynecologic, and other procedures requiring anaerobic coverage)
  • Second-generation cephalosporin (cephamycin) with good anaerobic coverage (including B. fragilis)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to cefoxitin or other cephalosporins
  • History of immediate/severe hypersensitivity to penicillins (risk of cross-reactivity; assess severity and type of reaction)
  • Renal impairment: dose adjustment required to prevent accumulation
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk: reassess if significant diarrhea develops during/after therapy
  • Not effective against MRSA, Enterococcus, or Pseudomonas
  • Caution with concurrent nephrotoxic agents
  • May cause false-positive urine glucose tests (copper reduction methods; does not affect glucose oxidase methods)

Adverse effects

  • Injection site reactions (pain, thrombophlebitis, inflammation)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, urticaria; rarely anaphylaxis)
  • GI upset (nausea, diarrhea)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea
  • Elevated liver enzymes (transient)
  • Hematologic effects (eosinophilia, thrombocytopenia, leukopenia – uncommon)
  • Nephrotoxicity (rare; dose-related)
  • Hypoprothrombinemia (rarely; more common with high doses/prolonged therapy)

Preparation & administration

  • Reconstitution: Reconstitute powder with sterile water for injection or 0.9% sodium chloride per manufacturer's instructions
  • IV administration: May give as slow IV push over 3–5 minutes or dilute further and infuse over 30–60 minutes
  • IM administration: May be given IM for selected indications (painful; use large muscle mass)
  • Administer at evenly spaced intervals to maintain therapeutic levels
  • Discard unused reconstituted solution per stability guidelines (typically 24 hours refrigerated; verify product-specific information)
  • Inspect for particulate matter and discoloration before administration
  • Do not mix with aminoglycosides in the same IV line (incompatibility)

Monitoring

  • Clinical response within 48–72 hours (fever, infection signs, symptoms)
  • Allergy/hypersensitivity reactions (rash, anaphylaxis): stop immediately if anaphylaxis suspected
  • Renal function (baseline and during therapy if prolonged course or high risk)
  • Diarrhea severity (assess for C. difficile if severe/persistent)
  • IV site for phlebitis/infiltration
  • CBC and coagulation parameters if prolonged therapy (monitor for hematologic effects and hypoprothrombinemia)
  • Liver function tests if prolonged therapy

At-sea considerations

  • Excellent choice for intra-abdominal and gynecologic infections due to anaerobic coverage (including B. fragilis)
  • Surgical prophylaxis: shorter half-life than cefazolin requires more frequent redosing during prolonged procedures
  • Confirm indication and avoid antibiotics for likely viral/self-limited illness when possible (stewardship)
  • If diagnostics are limited, reassess early non-response and consider alternate diagnosis or need for escalation/evac
  • Ensure proper reconstitution and storage; document preparation date/time
  • Document start date, dose, planned duration, and allergy status clearly
