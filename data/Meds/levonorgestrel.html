Reproductive Health

Levonorgestrel

Stocked on board

  • Levonorgestrel 0.75mg Tablets
  • Levonorgestrel 1.5mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Emergency contraception (unprotected intercourse or contraceptive failure)
    • Single-dose regimen: 1.5 mg PO as a single dose
    • Two-dose regimen: 0.75 mg PO followed by a second 0.75 mg dose 12 hours later (can also take both doses together)
    • Most effective when taken as soon as possible after unprotected intercourse; preferably within 72 hours (efficacy decreases with time)
    • May be used up to 120 hours (5 days) after intercourse, but efficacy is reduced

Indications

  • Emergency contraception following unprotected intercourse or contraceptive failure (e.g., condom breakage, missed oral contraceptive pills)
  • Backup contraception in cases of sexual assault (as part of comprehensive care)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known or suspected pregnancy (not effective if already pregnant; will not terminate existing pregnancy)
  • Hypersensitivity to levonorgestrel or any component
  • Not intended for routine contraception; does not replace ongoing birth control methods
  • Does not protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs); STI testing/prophylaxis should be considered separately if indicated
  • May be less effective in individuals with BMI >25 kg/m² or weight >75 kg (consider alternative emergency contraception methods if available; ulipristal acetate or copper IUD may be more effective in higher-weight individuals)
  • If vomiting occurs within 2–3 hours of dose, repeat dose may be needed

Adverse effects

  • Nausea, vomiting (common; consider antiemetic if high risk)
  • Fatigue, headache, dizziness
  • Abdominal pain, cramping
  • Breast tenderness
  • Menstrual changes: next period may be earlier, on time, or delayed; heavier or lighter than usual
  • If menstrual period is >1 week late, pregnancy test indicated

Preparation & administration

  • Administer as soon as possible after unprotected intercourse for maximum efficacy
  • Can be taken with or without food
  • If using 0.75 mg tablets: may take both doses at once or 12 hours apart (both regimens effective)
  • If vomiting within 2–3 hours, consider repeating dose (consult medical direction if possible)
  • Counsel patient: this is emergency contraception, not a routine contraceptive method
  • Advise to use barrier contraception for remainder of cycle and consider initiating regular contraception

Monitoring

  • Follow-up for next menstrual period (if >1 week late, pregnancy test required)
  • If severe lower abdominal pain develops (especially 3–5 weeks post-dose), consider ectopic pregnancy and seek medical evaluation
  • Provide counseling on regular contraception options and STI risk/testing if applicable
  • Document time of unprotected intercourse and time of levonorgestrel administration

At-sea considerations

  • Time-sensitive medication—ensure readily accessible and crew aware of availability for urgent use
  • Provide non-judgmental, confidential care; document interaction sensitively
  • Counsel on timing: most effective within 24 hours, but can use up to 120 hours; do not delay
  • Advise patient to seek follow-up for pregnancy test if period delayed, ongoing contraception counseling, and STI screening if indicated
  • If sexual assault is disclosed, ensure appropriate medical, psychosocial, and forensic support per local protocols
  • Stock both 0.75 mg and 1.5 mg formulations if possible for flexibility
