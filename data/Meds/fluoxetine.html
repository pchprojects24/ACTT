Fluoxetine

Stocked on board

  • Fluoxetine 20mg Capsules

Adult dosing

  • Major depressive disorder
    • Initial: 20 mg PO once daily (typically in morning)
    • Maintenance: 20–60 mg daily; adjust based on response and tolerance after several weeks (dose changes require provider oversight)
  • Generalized anxiety disorder / panic disorder / OCD
    • Initial: 20 mg PO once daily
    • Dose adjustments as per provider direction; may require higher doses for OCD (up to 60–80 mg daily)
  • Onboard considerations
    • Typically used for continuation of established therapy (not first-line initiation at sea unless under medical direction)
    • 20 mg capsules stocked: suitable for standard maintenance dosing

Indications

  • Major depressive disorder
  • Generalized anxiety disorder
  • Panic disorder
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder
  • Selected other psychiatric conditions (bulimia nervosa, PMDD) per provider direction

Contraindications / cautions

  • MAO inhibitor use within 14 days (serious interaction risk; serotonin syndrome)
  • Pimozide or thioridazine use (QT prolongation risk)
  • Known hypersensitivity to fluoxetine
  • Caution with history of mania/bipolar disorder (may precipitate manic episode)
  • Caution with seizure disorder (lowers seizure threshold)
  • Increased suicide risk monitoring required, especially in young adults during initial treatment/dose changes
  • Long half-life (~4–6 days for active metabolite): effects/interactions persist weeks after discontinuation
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding: risk–benefit assessment required (consult provider)

Adverse effects

  • GI: nausea, diarrhea, decreased appetite (common, often transient)
  • CNS: insomnia, headache, nervousness, drowsiness, dizziness
  • Sexual dysfunction (decreased libido, anorgasmia, erectile dysfunction)
  • Weight changes (loss or gain)
  • Serotonin syndrome (rare but serious; risk with multiple serotonergic agents): agitation, confusion, tremor, hyperthermia, autonomic instability
  • Hyponatremia (especially in older adults or with diuretics)
  • Bleeding risk increase (especially with NSAIDs, ASA, anticoagulants)

Preparation & administration

  • PO capsule; may take with or without food (taking with food may reduce GI upset)
  • Typically given once daily in the morning (may reduce insomnia risk)
  • If drowsiness occurs, consider evening dosing after discussion with provider
  • Do not abruptly discontinue: taper under medical supervision (though fluoxetine's long half-life reduces acute withdrawal compared to other SSRIs)
  • Counsel patient on delayed onset of benefit (2–4 weeks or longer) and importance of adherence

Monitoring

  • Mental status: mood, anxiety symptoms, suicidal ideation (especially early treatment and dose changes)
  • Side effects: GI tolerance, sleep, sexual function, activation/agitation
  • Watch for signs of serotonin syndrome, especially if combined with other serotonergic drugs
  • Hyponatremia risk: monitor if symptoms develop (confusion, headache, weakness) or in high-risk patients
  • Bleeding precautions: counsel on risk and monitor if on concurrent anticoagulants/antiplatelets

At-sea considerations

  • Primarily a continuation medication; avoid initiating without robust medical oversight and ability to monitor closely
  • Ensure patient has prior stable use and adequate supply before extended voyage
  • Suicide risk assessment critical: limited mental health resources at sea require careful planning and communication protocols
  • Long half-life useful for missed doses (less risk of withdrawal) but prolongs washout if switching agents
  • Document baseline mental status, current dose, prescriber, and emergency contact plan
