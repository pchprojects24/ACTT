Cefixime

Stocked on board

  • Cefixime 400mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Uncomplicated gonorrhea (urethral, cervical, rectal)
    • 800 mg PO as a single dose (current Canadian guideline recommendation due to resistance concerns)
    • Note: Older guidelines used 400 mg; verify current local protocol
  • Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic bronchitis
    • 400 mg PO once daily for 10 days
  • Pharyngitis/tonsillitis (streptococcal)
    • 400 mg PO once daily for 10 days
  • Uncomplicated urinary tract infection
    • 400 mg PO once daily for 7–10 days (or per local protocol)
  • Acute otitis media (when oral cephalosporin appropriate)
    • 400 mg PO once daily for 5–10 days
  • Renal impairment dosing
    • Dose reduction required for CrCl <60 mL/min: follow product monograph or clinical reference

Indications

  • Uncomplicated gonorrhea (per current resistance guidelines)
  • Susceptible respiratory tract infections (pharyngitis, bronchitis, otitis media)
  • Uncomplicated urinary tract infections
  • Third-generation cephalosporin with broader gram-negative coverage than first/second-generation agents

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to cefixime or other cephalosporins
  • History of immediate/severe hypersensitivity to penicillins (risk of cross-reactivity; assess severity and type of reaction)
  • Renal impairment: dose adjustment required
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea risk: reassess if significant diarrhea develops during/after therapy
  • Not effective against MRSA, Pseudomonas, or atypical respiratory pathogens
  • Gonorrhea: increasing resistance; use 800 mg dose and consider dual therapy per guidelines (e.g., with azithromycin)

Adverse effects

  • GI upset (diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain) – common
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, urticaria; rarely anaphylaxis)
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea
  • Headache, dizziness
  • Elevated liver enzymes (transient)
  • Hematologic effects (eosinophilia, thrombocytopenia – uncommon)

Preparation & administration

  • May be taken with or without food
  • Taking with food may reduce GI upset
  • Counsel: finish the prescribed course unless told otherwise; do not share leftover antibiotics
  • For gonorrhea treatment: ensure partner notification and testing per public health guidelines
  • Tablets should be swallowed whole with water

Monitoring

  • Clinical response within 48–72 hours (symptoms, fever, function)
  • Allergy/rash; stop and seek help if signs of anaphylaxis
  • Diarrhea severity (consider C. difficile if severe/persistent)
  • For gonorrhea treatment: test of cure recommended (especially pharyngeal infections); follow local STI guidelines
  • Renal function monitoring if prolonged therapy or high-risk patient

At-sea considerations

  • Useful oral third-generation cephalosporin for selected infections when broader gram-negative coverage needed
  • For gonorrhea: verify current dosing recommendations (800 mg preferred in Canada); consider dual therapy and partner management per guidelines
  • Confirm indication and avoid antibiotics for likely viral/self-limited illness when possible (stewardship)
  • If diagnostics are limited, reassess early non-response and consider alternate diagnosis or need for escalation/evac
  • Document start date, dose, planned duration, and allergy status clearly
