Cardiac / Vascular

Valsartan

Stocked on board

  • Valsartan 40mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Hypertension (continuation therapy onboard)
    • Typical maintenance dose: 80–160 mg PO once daily
    • Onboard stock is 40 mg tablets: if patient normally takes 80 mg, give 2 x 40 mg tablets once daily
    • Do NOT initiate new hypertension therapy onboard without medical direction; valsartan is for continuation of established regimens
  • Heart failure (continuation therapy onboard)
    • Target dose typically 160 mg twice daily (if already established)
    • Onboard 40 mg tablets allow for dose adjustments; follow patient's established regimen from home
  • Post-myocardial infarction (continuation therapy)
    • Typical dose: 20–160 mg twice daily (based on established regimen)
    • Ensure patient has been taking this medication prior to boarding; do not initiate new therapy

Indications

  • Continuation of established hypertension treatment (patient ran out or lost medication)
  • Continuation of heart failure treatment (when patient on ARB therapy)
  • Continuation of post-MI therapy in patients who cannot tolerate ACE inhibitors
  • NOTE: Valsartan should NOT be newly initiated onboard—only used to continue established therapy

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to valsartan or ARBs
  • Pregnancy: AVOID—risk of fetal toxicity and death (renal dysgenesis, oligohydramnios, IUGR). If pregnancy detected, discontinue immediately
  • Bilateral renal artery stenosis or single kidney with renal artery stenosis (risk of acute renal failure)
  • Hyperkalemia or high risk of hyperkalemia (concurrent K-sparing diuretics, CKD, diabetes)
  • Severe hepatic impairment or biliary obstruction
  • Volume depletion/dehydration: risk of hypotension; ensure adequate hydration before dosing
  • Do NOT combine with ACE inhibitors or direct renin inhibitors (aliskiren)—increased risk of hypotension, hyperkalemia, renal dysfunction
  • NSAIDs: reduce antihypertensive effect and increase renal dysfunction risk; use caution

Adverse effects

  • Hypotension, dizziness, orthostatic hypotension
  • Hyperkalemia (monitor K+ levels if feasible, especially in renal impairment or diabetes)
  • Acute renal failure or worsening renal function (especially in volume depletion, renal artery stenosis, or concurrent nephrotoxic drugs)
  • Headache, fatigue
  • Angioedema (rare but serious; less common than with ACE inhibitors)
  • Cough (much less common than ACE inhibitors)

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • Ensure patient is well-hydrated before administering (avoid in dehydration or acute volume loss)
  • Confirm patient's usual dose before dispensing; match home regimen as closely as possible with 40 mg tablets
  • If patient normally takes 80 mg once daily, give 2 x 40 mg tablets once daily
  • Monitor BP if feasible; adjust dose only with medical direction

Monitoring

  • Blood pressure (sitting and standing); watch for hypotension/orthostasis
  • Renal function (if labs available): monitor creatinine and BUN, especially in older adults, CKD, or concurrent diuretics/NSAIDs
  • Electrolytes (if labs available): monitor potassium for hyperkalemia
  • Volume status: ensure adequate hydration; avoid in dehydration or acute illness with poor PO intake
  • Signs of angioedema: facial/tongue swelling, difficulty breathing (rare; discontinue immediately and treat)
  • Symptoms of hypotension: dizziness, lightheadedness, syncope

At-sea considerations

  • ONLY use valsartan for continuation therapy—do NOT initiate new treatment onboard without clear medical direction
  • Confirm patient's home regimen and usual dose before dispensing; document clearly
  • If patient is dehydrated (vomiting, diarrhea, heat illness), hold valsartan until volume status normalized—risk of hypotension and renal injury
  • Avoid concurrent NSAIDs when possible (risk of renal dysfunction and reduced BP control); use acetaminophen for pain if appropriate
  • Pregnancy screening: if female of childbearing age, confirm not pregnant before continuing ARB therapy
  • If patient develops significant hypotension, dizziness, or orthostasis, hold dose and rehydrate; contact medical direction for guidance
  • If labs unavailable and patient has diabetes or known CKD, use caution and monitor clinically for hyperkalemia signs (weakness, arrhythmia)
