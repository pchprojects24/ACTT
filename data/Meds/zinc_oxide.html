Zinc Oxide

Stocked on board

  • Zinc Oxide 15% Ointment

Adult dosing

  • Diaper rash / incontinence-associated dermatitis
    • Apply liberally to affected area with each diaper change or after cleansing
    • Reapply as needed to maintain protective barrier
  • Minor skin irritations (chafing, moisture-associated skin damage)
    • Apply thin to moderate layer to affected area 2â€“4 times daily or as needed
    • Especially useful for friction areas, intertriginous areas, or moisture-exposed skin
  • Wound protection / barrier ointment
    • Apply around wound edges or over intact skin at risk of breakdown from moisture or friction
    • Do NOT apply to open wounds or deep puncture wounds
  • Sunburn prevention (mild barrier effect only)
    • Zinc oxide has some UV-blocking properties but 15% concentration is lower than typical sunscreen formulations
    • Not recommended as primary sun protection; use dedicated sunscreen products when available

Indications

  • Skin protectant for minor irritations, chafing, and moisture-associated dermatitis
  • Barrier cream for prevention and treatment of diaper rash or incontinence-associated dermatitis
  • Protection of intact skin at risk of breakdown (friction, moisture exposure)
  • Minor wound care: protection of peri-wound skin

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to zinc oxide or ointment components (rare)
  • Do NOT apply to deep or puncture wounds, animal bites, or serious burns
  • Avoid application to infected skin unless infection is being treated concurrently
  • Not for ophthalmic use; avoid contact with eyes
  • External use only; do not ingest

Adverse effects

  • Generally very well-tolerated with minimal adverse effects
  • Rare: local irritation, contact dermatitis (allergy to zinc oxide or ointment base)
  • Occlusive effect may macerate skin if applied too thickly or if skin not kept clean/dry

Preparation & administration

  • Topical ointment (15% zinc oxide in petrolatum base)
  • Clean and dry affected area before application
  • Apply thin to moderate layer directly to affected skin; may apply liberally for barrier protection (e.g., diaper rash)
  • Reapply after cleansing, bathing, or as needed to maintain barrier
  • Wash hands after application unless treating hands
  • May be difficult to remove due to ointment base; use gentle soap and water or mineral oil if needed

Monitoring

  • Clinical response: reduction in redness, irritation, and discomfort
  • Monitor for worsening or signs of infection (increased redness, warmth, purulent drainage, fever)
  • If rash or irritation does not improve within 7 days or worsens, reassess diagnosis and consider other treatments or evacuation
  • Ensure skin is kept clean and dry between applications to prevent maceration

At-sea considerations

  • Zinc oxide ointment is a versatile skin protectant useful for multiple minor dermatologic issues onboard
  • Common uses: friction injuries (thigh chafing, underarm irritation), moisture-associated rashes (groin, axillae, inframammary), minor burns (superficial only), peri-wound protection
  • For significant skin breakdown or suspected infection, treat infection first (topical or systemic antimicrobials as appropriate) and use zinc oxide as adjunct barrier protection
  • Not a substitute for proper wound care: ensure wounds are cleaned, debrided if needed, and appropriately dressed
  • Limited sunscreen protection: do NOT rely on this formulation for sun protection at sea; use dedicated broad-spectrum sunscreen
  • Document area treated and indication; educate patient on hygiene and moisture control to prevent recurrence
