Senna

Stocked on board

  • Senna 8.6mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Constipation (occasional use)
    • Initial dose: 17.2 mg (2 tablets of 8.6 mg) PO once daily, preferably at bedtime
    • May increase to maximum 34.4 mg (4 tablets) once daily if needed
    • Expect bowel movement within 6–12 hours
  • Bowel preparation (pre-procedure; per protocol)
    • Dosing varies by protocol; typically higher doses used in combination with other agents
    • Follow institutional/procedural guidelines

Indications

  • Short-term relief of occasional constipation
  • Bowel preparation prior to diagnostic or surgical procedures
  • Prevention of constipation in patients on opioid therapy or other constipating medications (short-term; consider alternative laxatives for chronic use)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Known hypersensitivity to senna or any component
  • Intestinal obstruction or suspected obstruction (ileus, bowel perforation risk)
  • Acute abdominal pain, nausea, or vomiting of unknown cause (may indicate serious condition)
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn disease, ulcerative colitis) during acute flare
  • Appendicitis or acute surgical abdomen
  • Severe dehydration or electrolyte imbalance (may worsen with diarrhea)
  • Not recommended for chronic daily use (risk of dependency, electrolyte imbalance, melanosis coli)
  • Pregnancy: generally considered safe for occasional use, but consult guidelines/medical direction
  • Breastfeeding: small amounts excreted in breast milk; monitor infant for diarrhea

Adverse effects

  • Abdominal cramping, discomfort, or pain (common, especially with higher doses)
  • Diarrhea (dose-dependent)
  • Nausea
  • Electrolyte imbalance (hypokalemia) with prolonged or excessive use
  • Melanosis coli (benign brown pigmentation of colon) with chronic use (reversible upon discontinuation)
  • Laxative dependency with chronic use (loss of normal bowel function)
  • Allergic reactions (rare)
  • Discoloration of urine (yellow-brown to red; harmless)

Preparation & administration

  • Take with a full glass of water to prevent dehydration
  • Preferably take at bedtime for overnight effect (bowel movement usually occurs 6–12 hours after dose)
  • Do not crush or chew tablets unless specified; swallow whole
  • Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration
  • If no bowel movement occurs within 12–24 hours or symptoms worsen, reassess and consider alternative therapy or medical evaluation
  • Not recommended for use longer than 1 week without medical supervision

Monitoring

  • Bowel movement frequency and consistency (therapeutic effect)
  • Abdominal pain or cramping severity
  • Signs of dehydration (especially if diarrhea develops)
  • Electrolyte status if prolonged use or high-risk patient (elderly, renal impairment, cardiac disease)
  • Lack of response: if no bowel movement after appropriate time, reassess for obstruction or other serious conditions

At-sea considerations

  • Useful for short-term management of constipation in remote settings (e.g., from limited physical activity, opioid use, dietary changes)
  • Ensure adequate hydration, especially if diarrhea occurs or in hot environments
  • Rule out serious causes of constipation (obstruction, acute abdomen) before use—clinical assessment critical
  • If constipation is chronic or recurrent, address underlying causes (hydration, fiber intake, mobility, medications)
  • Document use, response, and any adverse effects for continuity of care
  • Store in cool, dry place; protect from moisture
