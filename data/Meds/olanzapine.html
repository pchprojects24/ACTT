Olanzapine

Stocked on board

  • Olanzapine 5mg Tablets

Adult dosing

Onboard use typically limited to continuation of established therapy or emergency psychiatric stabilization under medical direction

  • Acute agitation (IM formulation not stocked onboard; oral dosing for cooperative patient under medical direction)
    • Initial: 5–10 mg PO once (typically 5 mg to start); may repeat after 2 hours if needed and medically directed
  • Continuation of established schizophrenia/bipolar treatment
    • Follow patient's home regimen (typical maintenance doses range 5–20 mg PO once daily)
    • Do not initiate new long-term therapy onboard without specialist consultation
  • Antiemetic use (off-label; when other agents ineffective and under medical direction)
    • 2.5–5 mg PO once or twice daily as needed (lower doses than psychiatric indications)

Indications

  • Continuation of established treatment for schizophrenia or bipolar disorder (maintenance therapy)
  • Acute psychiatric agitation when other measures inadequate and under medical direction/evacuation planning
  • Off-label: refractory nausea/vomiting (when conventional antiemetics fail and under medical direction)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to olanzapine
  • Caution in elderly with dementia-related psychosis (increased mortality risk; not approved for this use)
  • Diabetes/hyperglycemia risk: olanzapine associated with metabolic effects including new-onset diabetes and diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Cardiovascular disease: orthostatic hypotension, QTc prolongation risk
  • Hepatic impairment: consider dose reduction and monitor closely
  • Sedation/CNS depression: avoid concurrent CNS depressants (alcohol, benzodiazepines) when possible
  • Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) risk: fever, rigidity, altered mental status, autonomic instability (medical emergency)
  • Anticholinergic effects: caution in prostatic hypertrophy, narrow-angle glaucoma, constipation/ileus risk

Adverse effects

  • Sedation, dizziness, orthostatic hypotension
  • Weight gain, increased appetite
  • Hyperglycemia, dyslipidemia, metabolic syndrome
  • Anticholinergic effects (dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention)
  • Extrapyramidal symptoms (less common than older antipsychotics but possible)
  • Rare but serious: neuroleptic malignant syndrome, seizures, agranulocytosis

Preparation & administration

  • PO tablet; may take with or without food
  • If acute psychiatric emergency: ensure safe environment, de-escalation attempts first, and obtain medical direction before administering
  • Counsel patient on sedation risk; avoid alcohol and other CNS depressants
  • If continuing home therapy: verify dose, confirm patient adherence history, and document clearly

Monitoring

  • Mental status and psychiatric symptoms (agitation, psychosis, mood)
  • Vital signs: BP (orthostatic changes), HR, temperature (watch for NMS signs)
  • Sedation level and fall risk
  • Glucose monitoring if diabetic or at risk (olanzapine can worsen glycemic control)
  • Extrapyramidal symptoms (tremor, rigidity, akathisia)
  • If prolonged use or metabolic risk: weight, lipids, glucose when feasible

At-sea considerations

  • Psychiatric emergencies: prioritize safety (patient and crew), de-escalation, and medical consultation/evacuation planning
  • For patients on established therapy: ensure adequate supply for voyage duration; confirm adherence and stability before departure
  • If administering for acute agitation: document thoroughly (indication, dose, time, response, vital signs, mental status)
  • Limited lab/monitoring capability: use with caution in metabolic or cardiovascular risk patients
  • Plan for follow-up care at port or evacuation if new psychiatric symptoms or serious adverse effects
