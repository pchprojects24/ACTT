Neuromuscular Blocker / Paralytic

Rocuronium

Stocked on board

  • Rocuronium 10 mg/mL Injection 5 mL Vial (50 mg total)

Adult dosing

RSI paralytic (rapid sequence intubation)

  • 1.2 mg/kg IV push (typical: 80–100 mg for 70 kg patient)
  • Onset ~45–60 seconds; duration ~30–45 minutes
  • Higher dose (1.2 mg/kg) provides faster onset closer to succinylcholine

Non-RSI intubation / ongoing paralysis

  • 0.6 mg/kg IV for initial dose; 0.1–0.2 mg/kg for maintenance
  • Onset ~1–2 minutes; duration dose-dependent

Indications

  • Neuromuscular blockade for RSI (preferred paralytic when succinylcholine contraindicated)
  • Maintenance paralysis for intubated patients during prolonged holds
  • Facilitate mechanical ventilation when sedation alone insufficient

Contraindications / cautions

  • Absolute contraindication: Known hypersensitivity to rocuronium or bromide
  • Use with extreme caution: Neuromuscular disease (myasthenia gravis, Eaton-Lambert syndrome), electrolyte disturbances (hypermagnesemia, hypocalcemia), hepatic impairment (prolonged duration), severe burns or crush injury >24–72 hours post-injury (denervation hypersensitivity and hyperkalemia risk; use with caution and monitor closely)
  • Awareness risk: Patient is paralyzed but may be awake; MUST provide adequate sedation and analgesia before and during paralysis
  • Cannot assess neurologic status: Avoid or use with extreme caution in head injury if serial neuro checks required

Preparation & administration

  • Calculate dose based on actual body weight (1.2 mg/kg for RSI)
  • Draw up and label syringe clearly ("PARALYTIC - ROCURONIUM")
  • Administer rapid IV push for RSI; slower push for non-RSI paralysis
  • Refrigerate vial (2–8°C); do not freeze; stable at room temp for 60 days if removed from fridge
  • Compatible with normal saline, D5W, lactated Ringer's; do not mix with alkaline solutions
  • CRITICAL: Patient MUST be sedated and analgesed before and during paralysis; have airway equipment and ventilation ready BEFORE administering

Monitoring

  • Continuous pulse oximetry, cardiac monitoring, ETCO2 (mandatory)
  • Airway secured and mechanical ventilation confirmed before administration
  • Train-of-four monitoring (if available) to assess depth of blockade and guide re-dosing
  • Sedation depth assessment (use sedation scale; assume patient is aware unless deeply sedated)
  • Monitor for prolonged paralysis or recurarization after initial recovery

Adverse effects

  • Prolonged neuromuscular blockade (dose-dependent; increased in hepatic/renal dysfunction)
  • Bradycardia (rare; less vagolytic than other paralytics)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions including anaphylaxis (rare but serious)
  • Awareness during paralysis if inadequate sedation/analgesia
  • Respiratory depression (expected; patient cannot breathe; mechanical ventilation mandatory)

At-sea considerations

  • Rocuronium is the preferred RSI paralytic when succinylcholine is contraindicated (crush/burn injury >6 hours, denervating injury, hyperkalemia risk)
  • Longer duration than succinylcholine (~30–45 min vs ~5–10 min); commit to airway management for extended period
  • Paralysis = ventilation dependency: Patient cannot breathe; ensure airway secured and ventilation plan before administering
  • Sedation is mandatory: Assume patient is aware; provide continuous sedation (ketamine, midazolam, or combination) during paralysis
  • Label syringe "PARALYTIC" to prevent accidental administration
  • Train-of-four monitoring ideal but not always available; use clinical judgment and time-based re-dosing if needed
  • In prolonged holds: minimize paralytic use; wake patient and assess neuro status when safe
  • Document dose, time, indication, sedation plan, and ventilation status clearly
