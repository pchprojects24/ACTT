Analgesia / Anti-inflammatory

Methoxyflurane

Stocked on board

  • Methoxyflurane 3mL Inhaler (Penthrox)

Adult dosing

  • Acute pain (trauma, procedures)
    • Inhale intermittently as needed for pain relief; patient self-administers via Penthrox inhaler
    • Maximum single use: 3 mL (one inhaler) over one treatment session
    • Maximum total dose: 6 mL per day, 15 mL per week (to reduce nephrotoxicity risk)
    • Onset of analgesia: typically 6–10 inhalations (~2–3 minutes)
    • Duration: analgesic effect lasts ~20–30 minutes after stopping inhalation

Indications

  • Short-term relief of moderate to severe acute pain in conscious adults (e.g., trauma, burns, procedures, acute MSK injury)
  • Particularly useful when IV access is not available or opioids are not appropriate/available

Contraindications / cautions

  • Contraindicated: significant renal impairment; clinically significant cardiovascular or respiratory instability; impaired consciousness; hypersensitivity to fluorinated anesthetics; malignant hyperthermia risk
  • Nephrotoxicity: avoid exceeding maximum doses; do not use repeatedly over short periods
  • CNS depression/sedation: patient must be able to self-administer and follow instructions; caution with concurrent CNS depressants/opioids
  • Hepatotoxicity risk (rare): avoid in significant liver disease
  • Pregnancy/lactation: avoid unless clearly needed (limited data)
  • Use in well-ventilated area; avoid use in confined spaces (occupational exposure concern for bystanders)

Adverse effects

  • Dizziness, drowsiness, headache
  • Nausea (uncommon at analgesic doses)
  • Euphoria, dysphoria (dose-related)
  • Nephrotoxicity (dose-related; risk increases with cumulative dose)
  • Hepatotoxicity (rare)
  • Cough, throat irritation

Preparation & administration

  • Patient self-administers using the Penthrox inhaler device (green whistle)
  • Insert 3 mL vial into inhaler chamber as per manufacturer instructions
  • Patient inhales through mouthpiece intermittently as needed for pain
  • Instruct patient: "Take slow, deep breaths through the mouthpiece when you feel pain. Stop when pain is controlled."
  • Use the dilutor (adjustable valve) for less intense dosing if needed
  • Activated charcoal chamber filter reduces environmental exposure
  • Monitor patient continuously; they must remain conscious and able to self-administer

Monitoring

  • Pain relief (self-reported pain score)
  • Level of consciousness and respiratory effort (patient must remain alert enough to self-administer)
  • Sedation/dizziness (fall risk; keep patient seated/lying down)
  • Vital signs (HR, BP, RR, SpO2 if available)
  • Do not exceed maximum doses (6 mL/day, 15 mL/week) to reduce nephrotoxicity risk

At-sea considerations

  • Excellent option for acute pain when IV access is difficult or opioids are not immediately available
  • Patient must be conscious, cooperative, and able to follow instructions (not appropriate for severely altered patients)
  • Use in well-ventilated areas when possible; minimize bystander exposure in confined spaces
  • Document total dose used (each 3 mL inhaler) to avoid exceeding weekly limits if repeated use
  • If pain persists or worsens, reassess for underlying injury requiring definitive care/evacuation
  • Not a substitute for definitive analgesia in prolonged pain; consider transition to oral/parenteral analgesia as appropriate
