Clotrimazole

Stocked on board

  • Clotrimazole 1% Cream

Adult dosing

  • Cutaneous fungal infections (tinea corporis, tinea cruris, tinea pedis)
    • Apply a thin layer to affected area twice daily (morning and evening)
    • Continue treatment for 2–4 weeks depending on location and severity
  • Candidal skin infections (intertrigo, cutaneous candidiasis)
    • Apply a thin layer to affected area twice daily
    • Typical duration: 2–4 weeks; reassess if no improvement after 1–2 weeks

Indications

  • Superficial fungal skin infections (dermatophyte infections: tinea corporis, tinea cruris, tinea pedis)
  • Cutaneous candidiasis (skin yeast infections)
  • May also be used for vulvovaginal candidiasis when vaginal formulations are available (not topical cream)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to clotrimazole or other azole antifungals
  • Not for ophthalmic use (not indicated for eye infections)
  • Local irritation/burning possible at application site
  • Avoid occlusive dressings unless directed (may increase systemic absorption and maceration)

Adverse effects

  • Local burning, stinging, or irritation at application site
  • Erythema, pruritus (uncommon)
  • Allergic contact dermatitis (rare)

Preparation & administration

  • Clean and dry affected area before application
  • Apply a thin layer to the affected area and surrounding skin (extend ~1 cm beyond visible lesion)
  • Wash hands after application (unless treating hands)
  • Counsel: complete the full treatment course even if symptoms improve early; avoid sharing personal items (towels, clothing)

Monitoring

  • Clinical response: reduction in itching, redness, scaling within 1 week
  • Local irritation or worsening of symptoms (consider alternate diagnosis or contact dermatitis)
  • If no improvement after 2 weeks of treatment, reassess diagnosis and consider alternative therapy or medical consultation

At-sea considerations

  • Fungal infections are common in warm, humid maritime environments—emphasize prevention (keeping skin dry, changing socks/clothing)
  • If multiple crew members affected, consider environmental sources (shared showers, towels) and implement infection control measures
  • Document location, extent, and response to guide continued therapy or need for oral antifungal if extensive/refractory
  • For groin/foot infections, encourage good hygiene, drying thoroughly after showers, and using clean/dry clothing
