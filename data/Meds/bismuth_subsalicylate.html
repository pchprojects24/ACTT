Bismuth Subsalicylate

Stocked on board

  • Bismuth Subsalicylate 300-325mg Tablets

Adult dosing

  • Diarrhea (acute, non-bloody)
    • 525 mg (2 tablets if using 262 mg tablets; or use as labeled) PO every 30–60 minutes as needed
    • Maximum 8 doses per 24 hours
    • Do not use for more than 2 days without medical reassessment
  • Nausea / indigestion / heartburn
    • 262–525 mg PO as needed
    • Maximum 8 doses per 24 hours
  • Traveler's diarrhea prophylaxis (when recommended)
    • 262–525 mg PO four times daily with meals and at bedtime (follow local/travel medicine guidance)

Indications

  • Symptomatic relief of mild diarrhea, nausea, heartburn, indigestion, and upset stomach
  • Traveler's diarrhea (treatment and prophylaxis when indicated)
  • Adjunct in H. pylori eradication regimens (quadruple therapy; provider-directed)

Contraindications / cautions

  • Salicylate hypersensitivity (including aspirin allergy)
  • Active bleeding disorder or concurrent anticoagulant use (contains salicylate)
  • Renal impairment (risk of bismuth accumulation)
  • Gout (may affect uric acid levels)
  • Avoid in children/adolescents with viral illness (Reye's syndrome risk from salicylate component)
  • Pregnancy/lactation: limited safety data; generally avoid or use only if clearly needed
  • Caution with concurrent aspirin or NSAIDs (additive salicylate effects)
  • Do not use for high fever or bloody/severe diarrhea (may mask serious conditions)

Adverse effects

  • Darkening of tongue and/or stools (temporary, benign; counsel patient)
  • Constipation (with prolonged or high-dose use)
  • Nausea, vomiting (uncommon)
  • Salicylate toxicity (rare; more likely with overdose or renal impairment): tinnitus, hearing loss, confusion
  • Neurotoxicity (very rare; bismuth encephalopathy with chronic high-dose use)

Preparation & administration

  • Chew tablets well or dissolve in mouth before swallowing, or swallow whole with water
  • May be taken with or without food
  • Shake liquid suspension well before use (if liquid formulation available)
  • Counsel patient about harmless darkening of tongue/stools
  • Advise to maintain hydration, especially with diarrhea

Monitoring

  • Symptom relief (diarrhea frequency, nausea, indigestion)
  • Hydration status (especially with ongoing diarrhea)
  • Duration of symptoms: reassess if diarrhea persists beyond 2 days or worsens (may indicate bacterial/parasitic infection or other serious condition)
  • Signs of salicylate toxicity if prolonged use or high doses: tinnitus, hearing changes, confusion
  • Stool appearance: stop and seek care if bloody diarrhea or high fever develops

At-sea considerations

  • First-line for mild acute diarrhea; reassess early if no improvement or if symptoms suggest bacterial/severe infection (fever, blood, severe cramping)
  • Ensure adequate oral rehydration alongside bismuth use
  • Document start date, dose, frequency, and response clearly
  • If used for traveler's diarrhea: consider antibiotic therapy per protocol if moderate-severe or not improving within 24–48 hours
  • Warn crew about stool/tongue darkening to avoid alarm
