Benztropine

Stocked on board

  • Benztropine 1mg Tablets
  • Benztropine 1mg/mL Injection

Adult dosing

  • Acute dystonic reaction (drug-induced extrapyramidal symptoms)
    • IM/IV: 1–2 mg as a single dose; may repeat after 30 minutes if needed
    • Onset: IM within 15 minutes; IV within minutes
    • Most acute reactions respond to initial 1–2 mg dose
  • Prevention/maintenance (if continuing causative agent)
    • PO: 1–2 mg once or twice daily
    • Adjust dose based on response and tolerability (range 0.5–6 mg daily in divided doses)
    • Use lowest effective dose
  • Parkinsonism (adjunct therapy)
    • PO: Initial 0.5–1 mg at bedtime; increase gradually as needed (range 1–6 mg daily)
    • Specialized use; follow neurology/medical direction if applicable

Indications

  • Treatment of acute dystonic reactions (muscle spasm, torticollis, oculogyric crisis) from antipsychotics or other dopamine antagonists
  • Prevention of drug-induced extrapyramidal symptoms when continuing causative medication
  • Adjunct treatment of Parkinson's disease and drug-induced parkinsonism

Contraindications / cautions

  • Hypersensitivity to benztropine or anticholinergic agents
  • Narrow-angle glaucoma
  • Tardive dyskinesia (anticholinergics do not help and may worsen)
  • Myasthenia gravis, obstructive GI/GU disease
  • Caution in older adults: increased risk of confusion, cognitive impairment, urinary retention, falls
  • Caution in hot environments: decreased sweating can lead to hyperthermia
  • Prostatic hypertrophy/urinary retention risk
  • Significant cardiovascular disease (tachycardia risk)

Adverse effects

  • Anticholinergic effects: dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention, constipation
  • CNS: sedation, confusion, dizziness, memory impairment (especially older adults)
  • Decreased sweating, heat intolerance
  • Tachycardia
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Paradoxical worsening of movement disorders with excessive dosing

Preparation & administration

  • IM injection: 1 mg/mL solution; inject deep IM into large muscle (deltoid or gluteal)
  • IV injection: may give undiluted 1 mg/mL solution by slow IV push if needed for urgent dystonic reaction
  • PO tablets: may take with or without food; take at consistent times
  • For acute dystonic reaction: IM route preferred if IV access not established; response usually rapid
  • After acute treatment: observe for recurrence; consider short oral course if continuing causative medication

Monitoring

  • Clinical response to acute dystonic symptoms (should improve within 15–30 minutes after IM/IV dose)
  • Recurrence of symptoms (may require repeat dosing or oral continuation)
  • Anticholinergic side effects (dry mouth, urinary retention, confusion)
  • Mental status changes, especially in older adults
  • If used chronically: reassess ongoing need and consider gradual taper when appropriate

At-sea considerations

  • Acute dystonic reactions can be distressing and alarming; reassure patient that symptoms typically resolve rapidly with treatment
  • If dystonia from antipsychotic/antiemetic (metoclopramide, prochlorperazine): consider discontinuing or switching causative agent
  • Heat stress: anticholinergic effects impair thermoregulation; monitor in hot/humid conditions and ensure adequate hydration
  • Cognitive/sedation effects: caution with watchkeeping and safety-sensitive duties
  • Document triggering medication, symptom onset, and response to treatment for future reference
  • If recurrent dystonia: consider prophylactic oral benztropine while continuing causative drug, or seek alternative to causative agent
