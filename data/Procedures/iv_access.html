IV access

Indications

  • Need for IV medications or fluids
  • Suspected shock, major trauma, sepsis, anaphylaxis, ACS/stroke pathways
  • Need for blood sampling (if part of local workflow)
  • Anticipated deterioration requiring rapid medication delivery

Contraindications / cautions

  • Avoid compromised limbs:
    • Ipsilateral mastectomy/lymphedema (if known), AV fistula, severe infection/cellulitis, major burns, distal ischemia, significant trauma
  • Avoid joints/flexion areas if possible (infiltration/dislodgement risk)
  • Use aseptic technique; consider needlestick safety

Equipment

  • Tourniquet
  • IV catheter(s) appropriate gauge (consider 2 large-bore in major hemorrhage if feasible)
  • Antiseptic (chlorhexidine/alcohol per local practice)
  • Flush (saline), extension set, connector/cap
  • Tape/securement device; transparent dressing
  • Gloves/eye protection
  • Sharps container

Steps (checklist)

1) Site selection

  • Choose straight, palpable vein; prioritize forearm/AC depending on situation
  • Apply tourniquet; use gravity/warmth/vein tapping as needed

2) Skin prep

  • Clean with antiseptic; allow to dry (do not repalpate or re-clean if contaminated)

3) Cannulation

  • Anchor vein; insert catheter bevel up
  • Advance until flash seen
  • Lower angle; advance slightly to ensure catheter in vein
  • Thread catheter fully; release tourniquet
  • Occlude vein proximally; remove needle; connect extension/cap

4) Flush and secure

  • Flush to confirm patency (no swelling/pain)
  • Apply dressing and secure with tape/securement
  • Label with date/time and gauge/site if your system uses labels

Confirmation / success criteria

  • Flushes easily without swelling or pain
  • Good flow when aspirating/infusing
  • Site remains stable with movement

Complications + immediate management

  • Infiltration/extravasation
    • Stop infusion; remove catheter; elevate limb; treat per med type if vesicant
  • Hematoma
    • Remove catheter; direct pressure; choose alternate site
  • Phlebitis
    • Remove/replace; monitor
  • Infection
    • Use asepsis; replace if signs of infection
  • Needlestick injury
    • Follow exposure protocol immediately

Aftercare / monitoring

  • Reassess site frequently (especially with pressors/irritants)
  • Secure well for transport/movement
  • Document: site, gauge, attempts, complications

IV site and line maintenance

  • Non-aseptic insertion: Replace VAD within 24–48 hours.
  • Flush: Use preservative-free 0.9% Normal Saline. Scrub connections for 15 seconds prior to use.
  • Dressing change: Every 7 days, or sooner if integrity is compromised or complications arise.
  • Re-site: Only if signs of complications (infiltration, phlebitis, infection) and VAD is still needed.
  • Administration set — continuous infusion: Replace every 7 days, with new VAD, when IV solution changes, or if contamination occurs.
  • Administration set — blood products: After 4 units or 4 hours, whichever comes first; between different products.
  • Administration set — propofol: Every 6–12 hours.
  • Labelling: Label all IV sets with date/time. Label line close to the patient with medication name. Record site, gauge, and date of insertion.
