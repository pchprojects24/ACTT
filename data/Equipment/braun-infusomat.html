B. Braun Infusomat

Volumetric infusion pump used for controlled IV fluid and medication delivery with programmable rate and volume limits.

Quick start

  1. Inspect pump body, power cable, battery indicator, and verify approved Infusomat-compatible tubing set.
  2. Prime IV line manually, close roller clamp, and load tubing into pump channel per on-screen/door guide.
  3. Open clamp, connect to patient access, and program infusion rate and VTBI (volume to be infused).
  4. Confirm medication concentration, dose/rate, and line selection with independent check when required.
  5. Start infusion and verify drip chamber movement, pressure trend, and absence of upstream/downstream occlusion alarms.

Safety: Use the drug library/guardrails profile if available, and always confirm line tracing from pump to patient before starting high-risk infusions.

Common functions

  • Set continuous infusion by rate (mL/hr) and VTBI.
  • Run a bolus/manual dose (if enabled by local policy) with close hemodynamic monitoring.
  • Pause/resume infusion for line changes, transport, or clinical reassessment.
  • Review infused volume history and clear totals between patients.

Troubleshooting

  • Downstream occlusion: Check for closed clamps, infiltrated IV site, kinked tubing, or high resistance catheter.
  • Upstream occlusion / air-in-line: Ensure bag is not empty, spike seated correctly, line fully primed, and air removed.
  • Door/open-set alarm: Reseat tubing in the correct channel path and close door until latch confirms.
  • Battery low: Plug into AC power and verify charging icon; swap to charged backup pump for critical infusions.
  • Flow discrepancy concern: Re-check programmed rate, concentration, and actual line setup; escalate for pump functional testing if mismatch persists.

Care & consumables

  • Use only manufacturer-approved Infusomat administration sets.
  • Clean exterior with approved disinfectant wipes between patients; avoid liquid entry into keypad and door mechanism.
  • Perform pre-use alarm and battery checks each shift; schedule biomedical preventive maintenance/calibration.
  • Label lines clearly for multi-infusion patients to prevent wrong-line medication administration.
