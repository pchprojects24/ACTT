B. Braun Infusomat Space

Large‑volume IV infusion pump for continuous, intermittent, and bolus fluid/medication delivery. Supports a drug library for dose‑error‑reduction and operates on mains or internal battery.

Step-by-step startup

  1. Turn on: Press and hold the on/off button until the pump powers up.
  2. Install tubing: Press and hold the door-open button. Confirm clamp line. Thread tubing from right to left — the white piece only fits one way. Be gentle with the door mechanism. Press the green end in and hook out until the green press button pops out. Close the door with two fingers and hold. Wait to open the clamp.
  3. Prime the line: Either prime manually (remove the anti-gravity cap) or use the pump's built-in prime function. Confirm no air remains in the line before connecting to the patient.
  4. Connect to mains power or confirm battery is charged before starting.
  5. Program and start: Select Basic Infusion or use the Drug Library. Enter required parameters and press Start.

Button reference

  • Use the OK button or left arrow to select menu items.
  • C/back button cancels or goes back.
  • Any button press silences active alarms temporarily.

Programming — basic infusion

Program any two of the following three parameters (the third is calculated automatically):

  • Rate (mL/hr)
  • VTBI (volume to be infused, mL)
  • Time (hr:min)

Confirm settings and press Start/Run.

Programming — secondary (piggyback) infusion

  1. Stop the primary infusion first.
  2. Scroll to Secondary Infusion.
  3. Select No when asked "Use Dose Rate Calculation".
  4. Program any two of: Rate, VTBI, or Time. Recommendation: set VTBI and Time — the pump calculates rate for you.
  5. Press Select Start. Upon completion, the pump automatically returns to the primary infusion.

Drug Library

  • Critical Care library — contains inotropes and vasopressors with dose-error reduction limits.
  • Generic library — contains approximately 1,200 drug names and concentrations. Be aware that multiple concentrations may be listed for the same drug; always confirm the concentration matches what is prepared.

Standby and tubing removal

  • Standby: Press and hold the on/off button. The pump saves current settings until it runs out of battery or is reprogrammed. Press C to cancel standby and return to programming.
  • Remove tubing: Stop the infusion, press the Eject button, and follow on-screen instructions. May need to pull gently to detach the green hook.

Quick start

  1. Secure the pole clamp to the IV pole (turn the black knob clockwise). Slide the pump onto the clamp until it clicks.
  2. Connect to mains power or confirm the battery is charged. Press the Power button to turn on.
  3. Open the pump door by pressing the door‑release button. Thread the compatible IV set into the tubing guide and close the door until it snaps shut.
  4. Use the on‑screen priming function to remove air from the tubing; confirm no air bubbles remain in the line.
  5. Select Basic Infusion or choose a medication from the Drug Library. Enter the prescribed rate (mL/h) and volume to be infused (VTBI). Confirm settings and press Start/Run.

Safety: Always verify the correct IV set is loaded, the line is free of air, and the programmed rate matches the prescriber’s order before starting. Use the drug library whenever available to benefit from institution‑defined dose limits.

Common functions

  • Rate change: Press Stop, adjust the rate with the arrow keys, confirm, and press Start to resume.
  • Bolus delivery: Access the Bolus menu, enter the bolus volume or dose, and confirm. The pump delivers the bolus and returns to the programmed rate.
  • Secondary infusion (piggyback): Program the secondary bag parameters; the pump pauses the primary infusion, delivers the secondary, then resumes the primary automatically.
  • Keep‑vein‑open (KVO): When VTBI is reached, the pump automatically reduces to a KVO rate to maintain line patency.
  • Alarm silence: Press the alarm‑mute key to temporarily silence; correct the cause promptly. Unresolved alarms will re‑annunciate.

Troubleshooting

  • Door not closed: Press Stop, open the door fully, reseat the IV set in the guide, and close the door firmly until the latch engages.
  • Air‑in‑line alarm: Stop the infusion, inspect the tubing and drip chamber for air. Prime the line to clear bubbles, then restart.
  • Occlusion (upstream or downstream): Check for kinked tubing, closed clamps, infiltrated IV site, or a clogged filter. Correct the obstruction before pressing Start.
  • Battery low: Connect to mains power immediately. If the battery does not charge, contact biomedical engineering.
  • Device won’t power on: Verify the mains connection and check the battery status indicator; try a different outlet or power cord.
  • Persistent error code: Note the code displayed, refer to the error‑code table in the operator’s manual, and contact service personnel if the issue cannot be resolved at the bedside.
  • Downstream occlusion: Check for closed clamps, infiltrated IV site, kinked tubing, or high resistance catheter.
  • Upstream occlusion / air-in-line: Ensure bag is not empty, spike seated correctly, line fully primed, and air removed.
  • Door/open-set alarm: Reseat tubing in the correct channel path and close door until latch confirms.
  • Battery low: Plug into AC power and verify charging icon; swap to charged backup pump for critical infusions.
  • Flow discrepancy concern: Re-check programmed rate, concentration, and actual line setup; escalate for pump functional testing if mismatch persists.

Care & consumables

  • Use only B. Braun‑compatible IV administration sets designed for the Infusomat Space.
  • Clean the exterior and pole clamp with approved disinfectant wipes after each patient; do not autoclave or immerse the pump.
  • Inspect the door mechanism, tubing sensor, and air detector during routine checks; report damage to biomedical engineering.
  • Charge the battery fully after each use; battery capacity diminishes over time and may require periodic replacement.
  • Schedule preventive maintenance (calibration, safety checks) per manufacturer and institutional intervals.
  • Use only manufacturer-approved Infusomat administration sets.
  • Clean exterior with approved disinfectant wipes between patients; avoid liquid entry into keypad and door mechanism.
  • Perform pre-use alarm and battery checks each shift; schedule biomedical preventive maintenance/calibration.
  • Label lines clearly for multi-infusion patients to prevent wrong-line medication administration.
