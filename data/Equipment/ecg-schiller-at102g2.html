ECG — Schiller CARDIOVIT AT-102 G2

12‑lead resting ECG acquisition and printing unit with on-device interpretation, rhythm strip options, and patient database. Designed for hospital and primary care environments.

Quick Start Key Specs Functions Troubleshooting Care Videos References

Quick start

  1. Connect to mains or ensure the battery is charged. Confirm thermal paper is loaded correctly (print side up; close door firmly).
  2. Power on, select Resting ECG, and enter patient ID/age/sex as required.
  3. Prep skin (clean, dry, hair-free sites), place limb and precordial electrodes; check the lead-quality indicator for noise or detachment.
  4. Ask the patient to stay still and breathe normally; press Start to acquire and print the 12-lead tracing.
  5. Review printout for quality; save/export to USB or network if configured. Repeat if artifact is unacceptable.

Key specifications

Acquisition12-lead simultaneous; 3-channel and 6-channel print formats
InterpretationSchiller SEMA (automatic ECG interpretation); physician override/confirmation
Rhythm stripSelectable lead, variable duration; rhythm recording mode
Sample rate1,000 samples/second per lead (2,000 optional)
FiltersMuscle artifact (EMG), AC (mains) interference, and diagnostic/baseline wander filters
PaperZ-fold thermal ECG paper; speeds of 25 and 50 mm/s; amplitudes 5 and 10 mm/mV
StorageInternal patient database; USB export; network/LIS connectivity configurable
BatterySealed lead-acid or Li-ion (model-dependent); ≈ 80–100 ECGs per charge
DimensionsCompact desktop/portable unit; approximately 3.5 kg

Common functions

  • Print a rhythm strip (select lead and duration) in addition to the 12‑lead snapshot.
  • Apply filters (muscle/AC/diagnostic) to improve signal quality when motion or mains noise is present.
  • Store or export ECGs via USB/network if configured.
  • Re-run an ECG quickly using the last patient details when appropriate.

Troubleshooting

  • Lead-off/poor signal: Re-prep skin, replace electrodes, and ensure cables are fully seated.
  • Paper jam/out of paper: Reload Z-fold thermal paper with the print side up; close the door firmly.
  • No print: Check paper orientation and that the printer cover is latched.
  • Battery low: Connect to AC power and allow the battery to recharge.

Care & consumables

  • Use compatible thermal ECG paper; keep spares with the device.
  • Clean lead wires and clips routinely; replace worn or corroded electrodes/cables.
  • Disinfect the keyboard and surfaces after use; avoid liquid ingress.
