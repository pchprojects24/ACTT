ZOLL Propaq MD

Portable monitor/defibrillator for 12-lead acquisition, synchronized cardioversion, transcutaneous pacing, and manual/AED defibrillation.

Quick start

  1. Confirm battery charge, attach AC power when available, and verify electrodes/paddles, therapy cable, and printer paper are present.
  2. Power on and perform the startup self-test. Check ECG, SpO2, NIBP, and EtCO2 accessories as needed.
  3. Apply pads in anterior-lateral (or anterior-posterior) position and connect the OneStep cable.
  4. Monitoring: select lead view, set alarm limits, and acquire 12-lead if indicated.
  5. Defibrillation/cardioversion: choose energy, charge, announce clear, and deliver shock (sync on for cardioversion).

Safety: During defibrillation or pacing, ensure oxygen delivery devices and flammable materials are moved away from the chest, keep all staff clear of patient/bed, and verbally confirm "all clear" before shock delivery.

Common functions

  • Manual defibrillation: select Joules, press Charge, then Shock when the patient and environment are clear.
  • Synchronized cardioversion: press Sync, confirm R-wave markers, choose energy, then shock.
  • Transcutaneous pacing: press Pacer, set rate (ppm) and output (mA), then increase current until electrical and mechanical capture.
  • AED mode: follow prompts for rhythm analysis and advised shocks when non-manual operators are using the unit.
  • 12-lead ECG: connect full lead set, ensure low artifact, and print/transmit tracing if configured.

Troubleshooting

  • No charge / charge fault: Confirm battery and AC connection, reseat therapy cable, and retry after power cycle.
  • Cannot sync: Increase ECG signal quality by changing lead, reducing motion artifact, and confirming R-wave markers are visible.
  • Pacer not capturing: Reassess pad placement/adhesion, increase mA gradually, and treat reversible causes (e.g., severe acidosis/hypoxia).
  • Printer issues: Reload thermal paper with proper orientation and ensure printer door is fully latched.
  • SpO2 / NIBP errors: Reposition sensor/cuff, minimize motion, and warm poorly perfused extremities.

Care & consumables

  • Maintain charged spare batteries and test battery runtime during checks.
  • Replace defibrillation pads before expiry and after each patient use.
  • Stock thermal printer paper, ECG electrodes, SpO2 sensors, and NIBP cuffs in the response bag.
  • Perform shift checks: self-test pass, charge/discharge test per protocol, alarm audio check, and accessory integrity inspection.
