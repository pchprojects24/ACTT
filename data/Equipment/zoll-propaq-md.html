Zoll Propaq MD

Portable monitor/defibrillator with 12‑lead ECG, SpO2, NIBP, EtCO2, manual and AED defibrillation, synchronized cardioversion, and external pacing. Includes Real CPR Help® feedback.

Quick start

  1. Insert a charged battery into the compartment; connect therapy pads or paddles and any monitoring cables (ECG, SpO2, NIBP, EtCO2).
  2. Press the Power button. The device performs a self-test; confirm the correct date/time if prompted.
  3. Select the patient mode (Adult/Pediatric/Neonate) using the navigation keys at the top of the display.
  4. Apply ECG electrodes (3‑, 5‑, or 12‑lead), SpO2 probe, and NIBP cuff as required. Verify waveforms and numeric values on screen.
  5. Set alarm limits appropriate to the patient. Start a manual NIBP cycle to confirm cuff operation.

Safety: During defibrillation, ensure all personnel are clear of the patient before delivering a shock. Use only ZOLL‑approved therapy pads. Verify energy selection before charging.

Common functions

  • Manual defibrillation: Select energy with the charge control, press Charge, wait for the ready tone, clear the patient, and press Shock.
  • AED mode: Apply therapy pads; the device analyses the rhythm and advises shock or no‑shock. Follow voice/screen prompts.
  • Synchronized cardioversion: Enable Sync mode, confirm R‑wave markers on the display, charge to the selected energy, and deliver the shock.
  • External pacing: Select Pacer, set the rate and output (mA), and confirm electrical and mechanical capture on the display.
  • Real CPR Help: During CPR, the device provides real‑time feedback on compression depth and rate via on‑screen bar graphs and voice prompts.
  • 12‑lead ECG: Connect the 12‑lead cable, press the 12‑Lead key, acquire, and review/print the tracing.
  • Silence/acknowledge alarms with the Alarm Silence button; review and correct the underlying condition promptly.

    Portable monitor/defibrillator for 12-lead acquisition, synchronized cardioversion, transcutaneous pacing, and manual/AED defibrillation.

    Quick start

    1. Confirm battery charge, attach AC power when available, and verify electrodes/paddles, therapy cable, and printer paper are present.
    2. Power on and perform the startup self-test. Check ECG, SpO2, NIBP, and EtCO2 accessories as needed.
    3. Apply pads in anterior-lateral (or anterior-posterior) position and connect the OneStep cable.
    4. Monitoring: select lead view, set alarm limits, and acquire 12-lead if indicated.
    5. Defibrillation/cardioversion: choose energy, charge, announce clear, and deliver shock (sync on for cardioversion).

    Safety: During defibrillation or pacing, ensure oxygen delivery devices and flammable materials are moved away from the chest, keep all staff clear of patient/bed, and verbally confirm "all clear" before shock delivery.

    Common functions

    • Manual defibrillation: select Joules, press Charge, then Shock when the patient and environment are clear.
    • Synchronized cardioversion: press Sync, confirm R-wave markers, choose energy, then shock.
    • Transcutaneous pacing: press Pacer, set rate (ppm) and output (mA), then increase current until electrical and mechanical capture.
    • AED mode: follow prompts for rhythm analysis and advised shocks when non-manual operators are using the unit.
    • 12-lead ECG: connect full lead set, ensure low artifact, and print/transmit tracing if configured.

    Troubleshooting

    • Device won’t power on: Verify battery is seated and charged; try a second battery or connect AC power.
    • No ECG signal or artifact: Check electrode placement and cable connections; re‑prep skin and replace electrodes if needed.
    • NIBP/SpO2/EtCO2 reading failed: Confirm sensor placement and connections; reposition the cuff or probe and retry.
    • Unable to charge or shock: Ensure therapy pads are properly connected and not expired; check for displayed error messages.
    • Persistent alarm: Press Alarm Silence to temporarily mute; address the alarm cause. If the alarm recurs, refer to the operator’s guide error‑code table.
    • Battery low: Replace with a fully charged battery or connect to AC/vehicle power.
    • No charge / charge fault: Confirm battery and AC connection, reseat therapy cable, and retry after power cycle.
    • Cannot sync: Increase ECG signal quality by changing lead, reducing motion artifact, and confirming R-wave markers are visible.
    • Pacer not capturing: Reassess pad placement/adhesion, increase mA gradually, and treat reversible causes (e.g., severe acidosis/hypoxia).
    • Printer issues: Reload thermal paper with proper orientation and ensure printer door is fully latched.
    • SpO2 / NIBP errors: Reposition sensor/cuff, minimize motion, and warm poorly perfused extremities.

    Care & consumables

    • Keep spare SurePower™ batteries charged and rotated; charge after every use and monthly during storage.
    • Inspect therapy pads for expiry dates and packaging integrity; replace expired or damaged pads immediately.
    • Clean the exterior with a soft cloth dampened with approved disinfectant; avoid liquid entry into connectors.
    • Check cables, paddles, and connectors routinely for damage; replace as needed.
    • Perform the operator daily readiness check (power‑on self‑test, pad/cable inspection, battery level) before each shift or deployment.
    • Maintain charged spare batteries and test battery runtime during checks.
    • Replace defibrillation pads before expiry and after each patient use.
    • Stock thermal printer paper, ECG electrodes, SpO2 sensors, and NIBP cuffs in the response bag.
    • Perform shift checks: self-test pass, charge/discharge test per protocol, alarm audio check, and accessory integrity inspection.
