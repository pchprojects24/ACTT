Belmont buddy lite Blood/Fluid Warmer

Portable battery-powered in-line fluid warmer for warming crystalloids and blood products prior to or during IV infusion in emergency and critical care settings.

Quick Start Key Specs Performance Alarms Battery Care Videos References

Quick start

  1. Install the disposable set into the Heater Unit: align the orientation hub (RED to RED notch); the unit will not close if misaligned. Close the Heater Unit and secure the latch.
  2. Hang the fluid bag and connect the administration set (not supplied). Close the roller clamp before connecting. Using aseptic technique, connect the administration set to the BLUE luer on the disposable set.
  3. Prime the disposable set with the Heater Unit closed: open the roller clamp, tap the Heater Unit to release trapped air, then close the roller clamp. Connect patient extension tubing.
  4. Place the Heater Unit on a flat surface near the injection site. Connect the disposable set to the patient and adjust flow with the roller clamp.
  5. Press the ON/OFF button on the Battery Housing to power the Heater Unit. Confirm normal operation: Blue LED turns ON and Red LED flashes once.

Safety: Do not operate the system with the disposable set removed or apply pressure to the system in that state. Do not submerge the device. Disposable sets are single-use only. When infusing blood or blood products, the set can vent only small amounts of air — remain vigilant to avoid air embolism, especially when using a hand squeeze pump.

Performance

  • Warms crystalloid at 20°C to physiological temperature at flow rates up to 4.8 L/hr (80 ml/min).
  • Warms anticoagulated packed red blood cells at 10°C to physiological temperature at flow rates up to 50 ml/min.
  • At higher flow rates, fluid may not reach normothermia — reduce flow or use supplemental warming if needed.
  • Power: 14.4 VDC, 6.2 Ah battery. Each of 5 LCD segments represents 20% of full charge.

End of procedure

  1. Close the roller clamp.
  2. Clamp the disposable set closed using the blue slide clamp.
  3. Disconnect the disposable set from the patient.
  4. Press ON/OFF to power off.
  5. Open the Heater Unit and remove the disposable set.
  6. Discard the disposable set and administration set per hospital policy.
  7. Remove the battery from the housing and recharge.

Alarms & troubleshooting

  • CHECK FOR AIR (LEDs alternate red/blue): Fluid source empty or air in line, or line clamped. Replace fluid bag; open clamps. Press ON/OFF off then on to reset.
  • NO HEAT (LEDs alternate red/blue): Microprocessor or temperature sensor failure. Reset by cycling power; if it recurs, replace the system and contact service.
  • OVER TEMPERATURE (LEDs alternate red/blue): Caused by repeated stopping/restarting at high flow, or sensor failure. Clamp the line, cycle power off and on. If it recurs, replace the system.
  • PROBE FAULT (LEDs alternate red/blue): Hardware temperature override circuit fault. Cycle power; replace the system if it recurs.

At all alarms the system stops heating and flashes LEDs alternately. Reset by pressing ON/OFF to OFF, then back ON.

Battery

  • The 5-segment LCD on the battery displays state of charge; each segment = 20% capacity.
  • Remove the battery from the Housing to charge it.
  • The Battery Charger Status Window LED turns solid green when fully charged.
  • Recharge after every use; store with a charged battery.

Care & consumables

  • Disposable sets are single-use; discard after each patient per hospital policy.
  • Do not submerge the device for cleaning; wipe exterior with a damp cloth or mild disinfectant.
  • Inspect the Heater Unit latch, hinge, and cable connections before each use.
  • Water vapour condensation at the side vents after prolonged use is normal — not a seal failure.
  • Ingress rating: IPX1 (protected against vertically dripping water only).
