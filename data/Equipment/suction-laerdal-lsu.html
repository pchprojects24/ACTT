Suction — Laerdal Suction Unit (LSU)

Portable battery-powered suction unit for airway clearance and secretion removal in pre-hospital, transport, and clinical settings. Operates on internal rechargeable battery, AC mains, or vehicle power.

Quick Start Key Specs Functions Troubleshooting Care Videos References

Quick start

  1. Inspect the canister, lid, tubing, hydrophobic filter, and suction catheter. Confirm the battery is charged, or connect to AC/vehicle power.
  2. Assemble the canister and tubing. Confirm the hydrophobic filter is correctly seated and the canister lid seals firmly.
  3. Turn the unit on. Set the vacuum regulator to the appropriate level: adult ≈300 mmHg, pediatric 80–120 mmHg, neonate 60–80 mmHg. Occlude the catheter tip to verify suction.
  4. Insert the catheter with suction off, then apply suction and clear secretions in short passes (<10 seconds in adults).
  5. After use, turn off, empty/dispose of canister contents per policy, and clean the unit.

Safety: Limit each suction pass to ≤10 seconds in adults (shorter in pediatric/neonatal patients). Pre-oxygenate before suctioning intubated patients. Monitor heart rate and SpO2; stop immediately if bradycardia or desaturation occurs.

Key specifications

Maximum vacuum≈530 mmHg (free airflow at maximum setting)
Vacuum regulationFront-panel knob; adjustable for adult, pediatric, and neonatal use
Canister volume≈1,000 mL (disposable or reusable canister); visual fill indicator
Battery typeRechargeable Ni-MH or Li-ion (version-dependent)
Battery runtime≈45 minutes continuous suction at max vacuum (battery health-dependent)
Power optionsInternal battery; AC mains (100–240 V); vehicle DC (12–24 V via adapter)
FilterHydrophobic overflow protection filter — single-use; replace between patients
IndicatorLED battery and power indicator; alarm on low battery
Weight≈2.5 kg (5.5 lb) including battery; carry handle and bag-compatible

Common functions

  • Switch between internal battery, AC, and vehicle power without interrupting suction.
  • Adjust vacuum using the front regulator knob to match patient size; verify with the gauge while occluding the catheter.
  • Swap between reusable and disposable canister configurations as supplied; reusable canisters require cleaning between patients.
  • Monitor battery level via the charge indicator LED; plug in for recharging after prolonged use.

Troubleshooting

  • No or low suction: Check for kinks or occlusion in the tubing; replace a saturated or obstructed filter; reseat the canister lid and gasket; verify all connections are tight.
  • Suction weaker than expected: Confirm the regulator is not turned down; check for a leak in the canister seal or at the catheter connection.
  • Low battery alarm: Connect to AC/vehicle power and allow charging; replace the battery pack if run time remains short after a full charge.
  • Continuous alarm or leak sound: Inspect the canister seal, filter, and all connections; replace consumables and retry.
  • Canister full: Stop suctioning, close clamps, empty/replace the canister per biohazard policy, and resume.

Care & consumables

  • Hydrophobic filters and disposable canisters are single-use; replace after each patient.
  • Clean and disinfect exterior surfaces and reusable canister components after each use; avoid fluid ingress into the pump housing.
  • Charge the battery fully after each operational use; if stored, charge monthly and run a function check before deployment.
  • Inspect tubing, lid seals, and catheter connections routinely; replace worn or cracked components.
