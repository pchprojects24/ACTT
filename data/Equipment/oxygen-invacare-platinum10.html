Oxygen — Invacare Platinum 10

Stationary 10 L/min oxygen concentrator for continuous oxygen delivery. Produces medical-grade oxygen from room air using molecular sieve technology — no cylinders or refills required during normal operation.

Quick Start Key Specs Functions Troubleshooting Care Videos References

Quick start

  1. Place the unit upright with 12–18 inches clearance around the vents. Check that filters and power cord are intact, then plug into a grounded outlet.
  2. If humidification is ordered, fill the bottle with sterile/distilled water to the line and attach it to the outlet fitting.
  3. Connect the patient tubing/cannula to the outlet. Turn the power switch on and set the prescribed flow using the flowmeter knob.
  4. Allow a 5–10 minute warm-up; verify steady flow at the cannula and ensure no alarms are active.
  5. Shut down: When no longer needed, turn the flow to zero, switch the power off, and disconnect from the outlet. Allow the unit to cool before covering or storing.

Safety: Keep the concentrator and all oxygen tubing away from open flames, smoking, oil, grease, and heat sources. Oxygen-enriched air increases fire risk. Post “No Smoking — Oxygen in Use” signage in the treatment area.

Key specifications

Maximum flow rate10 L/min (continuous)
Oxygen purity≥90% at 0.5–5 L/min; slightly lower at higher flows — confirm with OEM specifications
Power requirement240 V AC (50 Hz); ≈ 600 W at maximum flow
BackupNo internal battery — requires uninterrupted AC or UPS; keep O2 cylinders as backup
Alarm systemPower failure alarm; low oxygen purity alarm (model-dependent); high temperature indicator
FiltersExternal foam intake filter (user-cleanable); internal HEPA/bacterial filters (biomedical service)
HumidifierOptional bubble humidifier bottle; sterile/distilled water; replace daily
Operating noise≈ 45–48 dB at 1 m
Weight≈ 18 kg (40 lb); casters for repositioning

Common functions

  • Adjust flow with the flowmeter; keep the ball centered on the line for the prescribed L/min.
  • Silence/reset alarms after correcting the issue (power, flow obstruction, or low oxygen indicator).
  • Swap to backup cylinders during power failures; turn the concentrator off when switching sources.

Troubleshooting

  • Low oxygen/service light: Ensure vents and filters are clear, check for kinks in tubing, and try a power reset; contact service if it persists.
  • No power: Check the outlet/circuit breaker and confirm the power switch is on.
  • High temperature or overheat: Improve ventilation around the unit, power off to cool, and remove nearby obstructions.

Care & consumables

  • Clean or replace the foam intake filter per schedule (often weekly); HEPA/internal filters are serviced by biomedical staff.
  • Rinse the humidifier bottle daily and refill with sterile/distilled water; replace if cracked or discolored.
  • Keep away from smoking, open flames, or oil/grease contamination.
