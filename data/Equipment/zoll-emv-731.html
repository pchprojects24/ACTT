Ventilator — Zoll EMV+ 731

Portable critical‑care ventilator for adult, pediatric, and infant patients. Operates on internal battery (≈ 10 h runtime), AC, or vehicle power with optional external O2 supply.

Quick start

  1. Confirm the battery is charged (or connect AC/vehicle power). Attach the correct patient circuit for the intended patient size (adult, pediatric, or infant).
  2. If supplemental oxygen is required, connect the high‑pressure O2 hose to the inlet and the gas source; otherwise the device ventilates on ambient air.
  3. Press the Power button. The ventilator boots and may run an automatic pre‑use self‑test—follow on‑screen prompts.
  4. Select the ventilation mode (AC, SIMV, CPAP, NIV, or Pressure/Volume Control) and enter patient parameters: tidal volume, respiratory rate, FiO2, PEEP, and I:E ratio.
  5. Connect the circuit to the patient interface (ETT, tracheostomy, or mask). Confirm chest rise, waveform display, and absence of alarms before leaving the bedside.

Safety: Always have a manual resuscitation bag immediately available as a backup. Verify alarm settings after every mode or parameter change. Never silence alarms without investigating the cause.

Common functions

  • Ventilation modes: Assist/Control (AC) for full support; SIMV ± Pressure Support for weaning; CPAP/NIV for spontaneously breathing patients.
  • FiO2 adjustment: Set from 21 % (room air) to 100 % when connected to an external O2 source.
  • Alarm management: The Smart Help feature displays the alarm cause and suggested corrective actions on screen. Silence the alarm temporarily while correcting the issue.
  • Monitoring: View real‑time pressure, volume, and flow waveforms; review minute ventilation, peak pressure, and SpO2 (if pulse‑ox module is connected).
  • Transport: Disconnect from AC, confirm battery level, secure the circuit, and continue ventilation during patient movement.

Troubleshooting

  • High‑pressure alarm: Check for circuit kinks, biting on the ETT, mucus plugging, or bronchospasm. Suction the airway and reassess.
  • Low‑pressure / disconnect alarm: Inspect all circuit connections and the patient interface for leaks; reseat or replace any loose fitting.
  • Low minute ventilation: Look for circuit leaks, cuff deflation, or inadequate patient effort; adjust settings or troubleshoot the circuit.
  • Apnea alarm: Confirm the patient circuit is connected and the patient is triggering breaths; switch to a mandatory mode if needed.
  • O2 supply pressure alarm: Verify the oxygen source is turned on and the supply hose is securely connected.
  • Battery low: Connect to AC or vehicle power immediately. Replace the battery if it no longer holds an adequate charge.

Care & consumables

  • Replace or clean the intake air filter per the maintenance schedule; a clogged filter reduces performance and triggers alarms.
  • Use single‑use patient circuits and bacterial/viral filters; replace between patients.
  • Clean the exterior with approved disinfectant wipes; do not immerse the device or allow liquid into ports.
  • Charge the battery fully after each use; cycle the battery periodically to maintain capacity.
  • Perform a pre‑use self‑test before every deployment and log results per institutional policy.
