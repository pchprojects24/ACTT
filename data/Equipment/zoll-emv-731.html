Ventilator — Zoll EMV+ 731

Portable turbine ventilator for adult, pediatric, and infant patients. Operates on internal battery (≈ 10 h runtime), AC, or vehicle power with optional external O2 supply. Suitable for hospital, transport, and austere settings.

Quick start

  1. Inspect ventilator, battery status, breathing circuit, bacterial/viral filter, and oxygen source connection (if higher FiO2 is required).
  2. Press the Power button; the ventilator boots and runs an automatic pre‑use self‑test — follow on‑screen prompts. Select patient category (Adult/Pediatric/Infant) and verify units/alarms.
  3. Assemble circuit, add HME/filter, and connect to a test lung. If supplemental O2 is required, connect the high‑pressure hose to the inlet and gas source; otherwise the device ventilates on ambient air.
  4. Select the ventilation mode (A/C-VC, SIMV, CPAP, NIV, or Pressure Control) and enter patient parameters: tidal volume, respiratory rate, FiO2, PEEP, and I:E ratio.
  5. Connect the circuit to the patient interface (ETT, tracheostomy, or mask). Observe waveforms and values for 1–2 minutes; confirm chest rise and absence of alarms before leaving the bedside.

Safety: Always have a manual resuscitation bag immediately available as a backup. Verify alarm settings after every mode or parameter change. Never silence alarms without investigating the cause. Any sudden deterioration requires immediate airway reassessment and manual ventilation.

Initial approach by scenario

  • Post-intubation, normal lungs: A/C-VC; VT 6–8 mL/kg PBW; RR 14–18; PEEP 5; FiO2 1.0 then down; EtCO2 35–45 mmHg (35–40 if TBI).
  • Hypoxic/ARDS or sepsis: A/C-VC; VT ≈ 6 mL/kg PBW; RR 18–24 as needed for EtCO2; PEEP 8–10; keep plateau < 30 cmH2O; accept mild permissive hypercapnia unless neuro concerns.
  • Obstructive (asthma/COPD): A/C or SIMV + PS; VT 6–8 mL/kg PBW; RR 10–12; I:E 1:3 to 1:4; PEEP 0–5; watch for air trapping and rising auto‑PEEP.
  • Neuro/TBI: Avoid hypoxia/hypotension; maintain EtCO2 35–40 mmHg (≈30 only as a short rescue for impending herniation); keep plateau < 30 cmH2O.
  • Transport/limited staffing: Use the simplest full‑support mode (A/C‑VC), confirm alarms, and keep suction and BVM at the bedside for any move.

Common functions

  • Ventilation modes: Assist/Control (AC) for full support; SIMV ± Pressure Support for weaning; CPAP/NIV for spontaneously breathing patients.
  • FiO2 adjustment: Set from 21% (room air) to 100% when connected to an external O2 source.
  • Alarm management: The Smart Help feature displays the alarm cause and suggested corrective actions on screen. Silence temporarily while correcting the issue.
  • Monitoring: View real‑time pressure, volume, and flow waveforms; review minute ventilation, peak pressure, and SpO2 (if pulse‑ox module connected).
  • Transport: Disconnect from AC, confirm battery level, secure the circuit, and continue ventilation during patient movement.

Troubleshooting

  • High‑pressure alarm: Kinked circuit, patient biting ETT, mucus plugging, bronchospasm, endobronchial intubation, pneumothorax, or stiff lungs. Suction, give bronchodilator if needed, confirm tube depth, and consider chest decompression if tension suspected.
  • Low‑pressure / disconnect alarm: Circuit disconnect, cuff leak, loose HME/filter, cracked tubing, or air leak around mask. Reseat connections, re‑inflate cuff, and check for tube displacement.
  • Low tidal/minute ventilation: Leak, cuff deflation, inadequate support, or fatigue. Increase support or switch to controlled mode and reassess sedation.
  • Apnea alarm: Ensure patient is connected and triggering; enable apnea backup and switch to mandatory mode if spontaneous drive is unreliable.
  • Patient‑ventilator dyssynchrony: Verify pain/sedation, check trigger sensitivity, adjust inspiratory flow or pressure support, and correct delays from circuit condensation or a clogged filter.
  • O2 supply pressure alarm: Confirm source is on, hose seated, and regulator functioning; revert to room‑air ventilation if O2 unavailable.
  • Battery low: Connect to AC/DC power immediately. Runtime is ≈10 hours on internal battery when healthy.
  • Persistent alarm with instability: Disconnect from ventilator and ventilate with BVM while troubleshooting; re‑establish controlled ventilation only after cause is fixed.

Prolonged-hold monitoring

  • Document VT, RR, PEEP, FiO2, PIP, plateau, and alarm limits after every change; verify against EtCO2 and SpO2 targets.
  • Recheck tube depth, cuff pressure, and circuit securement after movement or agitation; assign a team member to guard the airway during transport.
  • Clear condensation from the circuit safely, suction before/after turns, and replace clogged HMEs or filters to prevent sudden obstruction.
  • Track battery time and remaining O2 supply; keep BVM, inline suction, and backup power immediately available.
  • Review comfort and synchrony each watch: pain control, sedation level, agitation triggers, and whether ventilator assist settings match patient effort.

Shipboard and transport considerations

  • Stabilize the ventilator and circuit before moving through passageways; secure tubing to avoid kinking or traction on the airway.
  • Use the simplest effective mode for transport to reduce programming errors; confirm alarms are audible in noisy spaces.
  • Anticipate power transitions: plug in early when arriving, and confirm battery charge before leaving. Keep a charged spare battery if available.
  • Protect the intake and exhaust vents from salt spray and debris; avoid blocking airflow when stowing the device.

Care & consumables

  • Replace or clean the intake air filter per the maintenance schedule; a clogged filter reduces performance and triggers alarms.
  • Use single‑use patient circuits and bacterial/viral filters; replace between patients.
  • Clean the exterior with approved disinfectant wipes; do not immerse the device or allow liquid into ports.
  • Charge the battery fully after each use; cycle the battery periodically to maintain capacity.
  • Perform a pre‑use self‑test before every deployment and log results per institutional policy.
  • Keep spare single-use circuits, HMEs, bacterial/viral filters, and ETCO2 adapters available.
  • Charge batteries after each use and run operational checks before standby storage.
  • Clean external surfaces with approved disinfectant wipes; keep intake/exhaust vents unobstructed.
  • Replace consumables after each patient and follow biomedical preventive maintenance schedule.
