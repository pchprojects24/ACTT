Masimo Rad-57 Pulse CO-Oximeter

Handheld noninvasive pulse oximeter with multiwavelength CO-oximetry for continuous SpO₂, pulse rate, SpCO, and SpMet measurement.

Quick start

  1. Power on the device; confirm battery charge is adequate for the intended monitoring duration.
  2. Select the appropriate sensor for the patient (adult finger, pediatric finger, or ear clip) and connect to the device.
  3. Apply the sensor to a well-perfused site — typically the index or middle finger. Ensure it is clean, dry, and free of nail polish or external dye.
  4. Observe the Signal IQ bar; wait for a stable waveform and valid readings before recording values.
  5. Note all four displayed parameters: SpO₂, pulse rate, SpCO, and SpMet. Treat any elevated SpCO or SpMet as clinically significant until proven otherwise.

Safety: The Rad-57 screens for SpCO and SpMet but does not replace arterial blood gas co-oximetry for definitive diagnosis. Elevated SpCO (>3% in nonsmokers, >10% in smokers) warrants immediate clinical assessment and confirmatory testing. Treat SpCO elevation consistent with the clinical picture, not the number alone.

Parameters & normal ranges

  • SpO₂ — oxygen saturation; normal ≥95% in healthy adults at sea level.
  • Pulse rate — heart rate derived from plethysmography; confirm with ECG if needed.
  • SpCO — carboxyhemoglobin estimate; >3% (nonsmokers) may indicate CO exposure; >25% is a severe poisoning threshold.
  • SpMet — methemoglobin estimate; >1–2% warrants investigation; >20–30% is associated with clinical cyanosis and altered mentation.

Common functions

  • Use SpCO trending to monitor response to high-flow oxygen therapy in suspected CO poisoning.
  • Trend SpMet when administering agents that can cause methemoglobinemia (dapsone, local anesthetics, nitrites).
  • Enable the alarm limits for SpO₂ and SpCO when leaving the device unattended on a patient.
  • The device stores spot-check and trending data; review history via the onscreen log as needed.

Troubleshooting

  • Low signal / no reading: Reposition the sensor, check perfusion at the site (warm the finger if cold/vasoconstricted), and confirm the sensor cable is fully seated.
  • Motion artifact: Use the Masimo SET motion-tolerant mode; stabilize the patient's hand and avoid sites with movement or IV access.
  • Sensor fault / sensor check alert: Disconnect and reconnect the sensor; replace with a new sensor if the fault persists.
  • Readings fluctuating widely: Assess for poor peripheral perfusion, hypothermia, or arrhythmia and move to an alternate site (earlobe).
  • Low battery warning: Replace AA batteries or connect to external power; the device will indicate remaining battery level.

Care & consumables

  • Sensors are patient-specific or single-use depending on the type supplied; confirm before reuse.
  • Clean the device exterior with a damp cloth or mild disinfectant wipe; do not submerge or spray directly.
  • Inspect sensor cables for kinking, cracking, or connector damage before each use; replace damaged sensors.
  • Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and extreme humidity.
  • Replace batteries as indicated; carry spare AAs in the equipment kit.
