Cocoon Warming Device

Active patient warming system for prevention and treatment of peri-arrest, exposure, and post-resuscitation hypothermia.

Quick start

  1. Inspect control unit, warming blanket/cocoon, hose connections, and power source; verify blanket is clean and intact.
  2. Position patient and apply the warming wrap/blanket to maximize skin coverage while keeping access to airway/monitoring sites.
  3. Connect hose to the blanket port, power on, and choose initial warming level per patient status and protocol.
  4. Monitor core temperature and skin condition every 15–30 minutes; adjust setting to avoid over- or under-warming.
  5. When target temperature is reached, reduce to maintenance setting and continue periodic reassessment.

Safety: Do not place warming output directly against bare skin without the intended blanket interface. Check pressure points and insensate areas frequently to prevent thermal injury.

Common functions

  • Use higher initial setting for moderate/severe hypothermia, then step down as temperature normalizes.
  • Use maintenance mode for post-procedure/post-resuscitation temperature support.
  • Combine with passive warming (dry blankets, warm environment) and warmed IV fluids where indicated.
  • Pause warming when performing procedures requiring full access, then resume promptly.

Troubleshooting

  • No heat / low airflow: Check power connection, hose seating, and whether intake/outlet vents are blocked.
  • Temperature rising too slowly: Improve blanket seal/coverage, reduce heat loss, and verify adequate device setting.
  • High-temperature alarm: Lower setting, inspect blanket placement, and reassess skin immediately.
  • Patient discomfort/overheating: Decrease level or pause device; reassess core temperature and hydration status.
  • Persistent fault code: Remove from service and switch to backup warming method pending biomedical review.

Care & consumables

  • Use single-patient-use blankets/cocoons if supplied as disposable; otherwise reprocess according to manufacturer instructions.
  • Disinfect control unit exterior and hose surfaces between patients.
  • Store blankets dry and protected from tears; replace damaged items immediately.
  • Document warming start time, temperatures, and setting changes in the clinical record.
