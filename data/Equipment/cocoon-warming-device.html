Cocoon Warming Device

Active forced-air patient warming system for prevention and treatment of hypothermia in peri-arrest, exposure, post-trauma, and post-resuscitation care.

Quick Start Key Specs Functions Troubleshooting Care Videos References

Quick start

  1. Inspect the control unit, warming blanket/cocoon, hose connections, and power source. Verify the blanket is clean and structurally intact.
  2. Position the patient and apply the warming wrap/blanket to maximise skin coverage while maintaining access to the airway, monitoring sites, and IV lines.
  3. Connect the hose to the blanket inlet port. Power on the unit and select the appropriate warming level per patient status and protocol.
  4. Monitor core temperature and skin condition every 15–30 minutes. Adjust the setting to avoid over-warming or thermal injury.
  5. When target temperature is reached, reduce to a maintenance setting and continue periodic reassessment until temperature is stable.

Safety: Do not direct warming airflow against bare skin without the intended blanket interface. Check pressure points and insensate areas frequently to prevent thermal burns. Do not use near oxygen-enriched environments without precaution — the blanket material is not designed for oxygen-rich atmospheres.

Common functions

  • Use a higher initial setting for moderate/severe hypothermia, then step down incrementally as temperature normalises to avoid overshoot.
  • Use a maintenance/low setting for post-resuscitation or post-procedure temperature support once normothermia is approached.
  • Combine with passive warming (dry insulating blankets, warm environment) and warmed IV fluids where indicated to improve rewarming efficiency.
  • Pause warming when performing procedures requiring full patient access; resume promptly after completion.
  • Document warming start time, core temperature readings, setting used, and any changes in the clinical record.

Troubleshooting

  • No heat or low airflow: Check power connection, confirm hose is fully seated at both the unit and blanket ends, and verify intake/outlet vents are unobstructed.
  • Temperature rising too slowly: Improve blanket coverage and seal, reduce environmental heat loss, and verify the unit is set to an adequate warming level.
  • High-temperature alarm: Immediately reduce to a lower setting, inspect blanket placement for obstructions, and reassess skin for signs of overheating or pressure injury.
  • Patient discomfort/overheating: Decrease the setting or pause the device; reassess core temperature, hydration, and patient comfort.
  • Persistent fault code or unusual noise: Remove from service and switch to passive warming methods (warm blankets, warm IV fluids) pending biomedical review.

Care & consumables

  • Use single-patient-use blankets/cocoons if supplied as disposable; reprocess reusable blankets per the manufacturer's instructions for use.
  • Disinfect the control unit exterior and hose surfaces between patients using approved wipes.
  • Store blankets dry and protected from tears or punctures; replace damaged items immediately.
  • Inspect hose and connections for cracks or loose fittings before each use.
